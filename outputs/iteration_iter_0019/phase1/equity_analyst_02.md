## Evidence & Reasoning

### Claim 1 (Correlation as Valuation Regime Identifier)
The Gordon Growth Model decomposition — P/E = 1/(r - g) where r = risk-free + ERP — makes the mechanism precise. When inflation is low and rates stable, r barely moves and P/E fluctuations are dominated by g (earnings revisions). Earnings disappointments drive equity declines while bonds rally (flight to safety): negative correlation. When inflation is elevated, r is volatile and rate changes dominate P/E compression irrespective of g: both stocks and bonds decline simultaneously, producing positive correlation. The 2022 case is definitive: S&P 500 EPS grew ~4% yet the index fell ~19%, with the entire drawdown attributable to P/E compression driven by the 10Y yield moving from 1.5% to 3.9%. Within this drawdown, highest-cash-conversion-quintile stocks declined ~12% versus lowest-quintile ~32% — quality acted as a duration shield precisely because high-quality earnings have shorter effective duration.

### Claim 2 (Reverse-DCF Implied Rate Bet)
With the S&P 500 near ~5,200 (March 2026), forward EPS of ~$250-260, and a forward P/E of ~20-21x, a two-stage DDM (11% EPS growth for 5 years, 4% terminal) implies an equity discount rate of ~8.5-9.5%. If the risk-free component is set at the current 10Y (~4.3%), the implied ERP is ~4.2-5.2%. If instead the risk-free rate is set at ~3.5% (consistent with ~100-150bp of cuts priced into Fed funds futures), the implied ERP rises to a more comfortable ~5.0-6.0%. The market's valuation thus embeds an implicit rate-normalization assumption. If cuts fail to materialize, maintaining the current P/E requires accepting an ERP at the bottom of its historical range — an irrational equilibrium that would self-correct through a 10-15% de-rating.

### Claim 3 (Non-GAAP Duration Masking)

Numerically, 1pp compression on ~$15T invested capital destroys ~$150B in annual economic profit. ## Connections to Other Topics

### Monetary Policy (monetary_policy)
The Fed's reaction function is the primary determinant of the correlation regime. Credible inflation-targeting (anchored expectations, stable discount rate) produces negative correlation, rewarding growth equities. Questioned credibility (2022, 1970s) produces discount rate volatility and positive correlation, rewarding quality equities. The current market expectation of ~100-150bp cuts is an implicit bet on negative-correlation reversion. If the Fed is constrained by inflation persistence or fiscal dominance from cutting, positive correlation extends — making earnings quality the dominant equity allocation factor for longer. The "last mile" of disinflation determines whether the correlation regime flips back.

### Inflation Regimes (inflation_regimes)
Inflation is the root variable governing the correlation regime. Transitory inflation (supply-shock resolution) preserves earnings quality and supports negative correlation. Cost-push inflation that compresses margins degrades earnings quality selectively in input-cost-sensitive sectors. A wage-price spiral systematically destroys earnings quality across the market through nominal illusion, inventory profit distortion, and depreciation understatement — the hallmark of the 1970s positive-correlation regime. Classifying the current inflation regime (likely transitional between supply-shock normalization and structurally higher services inflation) determines which earnings quality screens are most predictive.

### Volatility Regimes (volatility_regimes)
The VIX-MOVE divergence framework connects directly: when MOVE (rates vol) is elevated but VIX (equity vol) is suppressed, it signals the rates market is pricing a regime change that equities have not yet absorbed. From an earnings quality perspective, this divergence is a warning that the discount rate denominator is moving while equity prices assume stability — meaning low-quality, long-duration equities are carrying unpriced correlation risk. The 2022 episode was preceded by exactly this VIX-MOVE divergence. Monitoring this spread offers a real-time earnings quality risk gauge.

### Equity Cycles (equity_cycles)
The correlation regime functions as a factor rotation catalyst within the equity cycle. Negative correlation → growth/momentum leadership → P/E expansion acceptable → ERP narrative dominates. Positive correlation → value/quality leadership → P/E compression → ROIC-WACC spread becomes the relevant valuation framework. The 2022 correlation flip coincided precisely with the growth-to-value factor rotation. Crucially, deteriorating accruals quality in growth stocks (observable from Q3 2021) provided a leading signal of the rotation before the correlation flip formalized it in Q1 2022.

### Fiscal Dominance (fiscal_dominance)
If fiscal supply dynamics structurally elevate long-end yields, the 30Y-SPX positive correlation channel becomes semi-permanent. This is the structural bear case for terminal equity valuations and for the 60/40 portfolio construct. Under fiscal dominance, short-duration, high-cash-conversion equity strategies become the only equity allocation with genuine diversification properties — effectively replacing the bond allocation's traditional role.

### Sovereign Debt Sustainability (sovereign_debt)
Reverse-DCF applied to UST yields implies the market prices an optimistic fiscal trajectory. If debt sustainability concerns force a risk premium into long-end yields (term premium expansion), the resulting rate repricing cascades into equity terminal values through the 30Y channel — producing a sovereign-credit-driven positive correlation event that monetary policy cannot offset because the source is fiscal, not monetary. This is the tail-risk channel connecting sovereign credit to equity valuations.

## Open Questions

1. **What is the precise inflation threshold that triggers the correlation regime flip from negative to positive?** Academic estimates range from 2.5% to 4% core CPI, but the answer likely depends on the inflation's source (supply vs. demand), its persistence, and central bank credibility at the time. Pinpointing this threshold would enable real-time calibration of earnings quality factor tilts.

2. **Can accruals-based earnings quality metrics serve as a genuine leading indicator of correlation regime shifts, or only as a concurrent confirmation?** The 2021-2022 episode is suggestive — quality factor deterioration started ~2 quarters before the correlation flipped — but this is a single observation. Systematic backtesting across the 1960s-2020s correlation regime history is needed.

3. **How does the mega-cap concentration distort index-level correlation measurement?** The 5-7 largest S&P 500 companies (with net-cash balance sheets, minimal rate sensitivity, and exceptional ROIC) may anchor the index correlation near zero even as the "S&P 493" exhibits strongly positive correlation. All correlation analysis should be conducted at the constituent or sector level, not just the cap-weighted index.

4. **Is the maturity-dependent correlation bifurcation (2Y negative, 30Y positive) a stable equilibrium or a transitional state?** If stable, it implies permanently bifurcated equity strategies. If transitory (converging to uniformly positive or negative), portfolio construction implications differ radically. The fiscal dominance thesis suggests the bifurcation persists; mean-reversion toward credible inflation targeting suggests convergence.

5. **How should stock-based compensation be incorporated into cross-asset correlation models?** SBC creates real dilution excluded from most non-GAAP and free cash flow definitions. If SBC grows secularly at ~8-10% annually, the "true" equity earnings yield is falling even as reported earnings grow — increasing hidden duration and positive correlation exposure in a way no standard model captures.

6. **What is the optimal earnings-quality-conditioned portfolio construction under persistent positive correlation?** Specifically: what is the right blend of short-duration Treasuries (which retain negative correlation per maturity bifurcation), high-cash-conversion equities, and explicit convexity instruments (gold, trend-following, put options) that replaces the traditional 60/40 framework's diversification properties?

7. **In a stagflationary scenario where rates stay elevated AND earnings growth decelerates, how severe is the ROIC-WACC double compression?** This is the tail-risk scenario where both channels (higher WACC from rates, lower ROIC from margin compression) move against equities simultaneously, potentially triggering a 1970s-style extended derating. Quantifying this scenario's probability and magnitude is the single most important open analytical question for equity allocation.
