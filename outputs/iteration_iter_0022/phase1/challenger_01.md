**Summary of the contrarian analysis on commodity supercycles:**

The core thesis: the supercycle narrative is the most crowded structural trade in macro today, and its foundations are more fragile than positioning implies. Seven specific challenges:

1. **Crowded positioning** — net commodity overweights at +1.8σ, 13/15 sell-side desks structurally bullish, copper managed money longs in 89th percentile
2. **Energy transition demand overstated** — lithium's 80% price collapse from 2022 peak is a template for supply response; substitution (aluminum-for-copper) is accelerating
3. **OPEC+ structural weakness** — 700kb/d overproduction accelerating, every price-defense cycle since 1985 ended in disorderly supply release
4. **China rotation overstated** — copper intensity rising but total commodity intensity per GDP declining 2-3%/yr; double-counting in demand estimates
5. **Historical analogues misapplied** — the 2000s was a 1.3B-person urbanization event with no successor; India is services-led at lower commodity intensity
6. **Capex underinvestment misleading** — capital efficiency improved 30-40%; productivity-adjusted investment is far closer to 2014 levels
7. **Strong dollar headwind** — local-currency cost curves keep marginal supply online, creating a self-defeating mechanism for the scarcity thesis

Key cross-topic links to monetary transmission (strong dollar kills commodity scarcity), stock-bond correlation (no supercycle → less supply-shock inflation → supports negative correlation return), and the carry-commodity reflexive loop (supercycle unwind propagates through FX).
