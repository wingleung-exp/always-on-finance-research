# AI & Tech Disruption — FX Regime & Dollar Cycle Specialist Analysis

## Key Claims

1. **The dollar is in a "capex-sustained plateau" phase of the cycle — structurally analogous to 1997-2000 but with a fundamentally different termination mechanism.** The DXY at 103-107 is maintained not by rate differentials (Fed has cut 100-150bp) but by $40-60B/month net equity inflows concentrated in technology (TIC data). This is the "capex dollar" — a capital account phenomenon operating orthogonally to the interest rate parity framework that dominates consensus FX analysis. The 1997-2001 dot-com dollar appreciated ~25% during a period when the Fed was not systematically tighter than peers; the mechanism was capital flow dominance over trade balances. The critical difference: the 2001 correction was triggered by a credit-funded capex collapse, while current AI capex is self-funded (OCF coverage 1.5-1.7x), implying the plateau can persist longer but the correction, when it comes, must be triggered by an equity valuation reassessment rather than a credit event.

2. **The USD-AI correlation constitutes a novel FX regime where the dollar is functionally a leveraged call option on US AI exceptionalism — creating asymmetric tail risk that standard FX models cannot capture.** DXY-to-SOX rolling 6-month correlation has risen from ~0.15 (2015-2020 average) to ~0.45-0.55 (2024-2026). This means a 20% drawdown in AI-adjacent equities would transmit ~9-11% dollar weakness through capital flow reversal — a magnitude comparable to the 2001-2004 DXY decline of ~33% but potentially compressed into 12-18 months given higher passive allocation and faster information transmission. The dollar's beta to AI narrative is the single most important regime change in G10 FX since the post-GFC monetary policy divergence era.

3. **The "twin deficit constraint" is currently non-binding due to the AI capital account offset, but the arithmetic of unsustainability is tightening on a 2-4 year horizon.** The US fiscal deficit at 6-7% of GDP plus the current account deficit at 3-4% of GDP implies cumulative external financing needs of ~$1.5-2T annually. AI-related capital inflows ($200-350B portfolio equity + $200-250B capex-related) cover only 25-35% of this requirement. The remainder is financed by foreign official reserve accumulation (declining at 0.5-0.7pp/yr per IMF COFER), dollar invoicing inertia, and the structural absence of alternatives. The binding constraint arrives when the AI inflow contribution ceases to grow — which coincides with the capex-to-revenue validation threshold (currently 4-6x, needing 1.5-2.0x by 2028-2030 per KB). A plateau in AI inflows converts the twin deficit from a background concern to an active drag at ~5-8% real effective exchange rate overvaluation vs. BEER fair value.

4. **EUR/USD is structurally mispriced at 1.05-1.10 due to the market's failure to separate the AI capital flow premium from the rate differential premium — these are two independent supports, and the rate differential is already closing.** ECB-Fed rate differential has narrowed from ~200bp to ~75-100bp, yet EUR/USD has barely moved. The residual is the AI capital flow premium, which I estimate at 5-8 cents of EUR/USD (3-5% of the dollar's overvaluation). This premium is sustained by AI capex/GDP differential: US ~1.0% vs eurozone ~0.15-0.2%. The critical insight: the rate differential premium is mean-reverting on a 12-18 month horizon (Fed cuts vs ECB at/near terminal), while the AI capital flow premium is structural and could persist for 3-5 years. Consensus EUR/USD models that blend the two will systematically misprice the pair.

5. **USD/JPY above 150 represents a structural AI-driven dislocation that will persist until either (a) BoJ normalizes to 1%+ or (b) US AI capex cycle peaks — whichever comes first.** The BoJ faces the most extreme version of the AI monetary policy divergence: Japan's AI capex/GDP is 0.1-0.3% vs US 1.0%, creating a permanent savings export channel from Japanese institutions into US tech assets. Japanese life insurers and pension funds have accumulated $500B+ in unhedged foreign assets (primarily US equities/bonds), creating a structural JPY short. The carry-to-AI correlation means traditional JPY long as risk-off hedge is impaired — the correlation between USD/JPY and VIX has weakened from -0.6 to -0.25 as AI-driven VIX spikes (e.g., DeepSeek) produce complex JPY dynamics (risk-off JPY bid vs AI-capital-repatriation JPY sell).

6. **The TWD/KRW decoupling from EM FX benchmarks toward semiconductor proxy status creates a measurable mispricing in EM FX indices and carry strategies that use aggregate EM baskets.** TWD/KRW correlation with SOX has risen from ~0.3 to ~0.7 while R-squared against traditional EM factors (VIX, EMBI, DXY) has declined. Taiwan's ~14% CA/GDP surplus from TSMC and Korea's HBM dominance mean these currencies have de facto reclassified from EM to "tech-adjacent DM." Any EM FX strategy that includes TWD/KRW in the same basket as BRL/ZAR/TRY is systematically mispricing factor exposure. The mispricing quantum: TWD/KRW carry-to-vol ratios are 30-50% lower than traditional EM but fundamental quality metrics (CA/GDP, reserves, FDI stickiness) are 2-3x stronger — a factor mismatch that traditional EM models cannot arbitrage.

7. **The "Petrodollar-to-Teradollar" transition represents a structural shift in the dollar's demand composition that strengthens the dollar's medium-term position while increasing its fragility to a single-sector reversal.** Petrodollar recycling (~$300-400B annually at $80 oil × ~35M bbl/day of dollar-invoiced trade) is being supplemented by "Teradollar" flows: cloud/AI subscription revenue ($350B+ annually, growing 20-25%, 65-70% US-denominated), semiconductor equipment purchases (predominantly USD-invoiced), and AI infrastructure FDI. Unlike petrodollar flows which diversified across sovereign wealth funds, Teradollar flows concentrate in US equity markets and corporate bonds, creating a tighter feedback loop between dollar demand and US asset valuations. The structural support is stronger (higher switching costs, data gravity lock-in) but the single-point-of-failure risk is larger (oil demand is diversified across 190 countries; AI demand is concentrated in ~50 hyperscale customers).

8. **China's AI trajectory is the highest-variance binary swing factor for the dollar cycle, with a $200-400B annual capital flow differential between scenarios — larger than the entire EM carry trade.** Chinese AI success (DeepSeek trajectory, domestic semiconductor self-sufficiency) redirects $200-400B of cumulative portfolio flows that have been redirected from China to US tech since 2021 (TIC data). Chinese AI failure reinforces the US-centric AI monopoly and extends the capex dollar plateau by 3-5 years. The probability-weighted expected capital flow impact: 25% China success × ($200-400B reversal) + 50% stalemate × ($0 change) + 25% China failure × ($50-100B additional US inflow) = net expectation of roughly neutral, but the variance is enormous. This is not a hedgeable risk — it's a regime determination.

9. **Services-exporting EM economies (India, Philippines, Bangladesh) face a 5-15 year structural current account erosion from AI automation that NDF forwards do not price, creating an asymmetric FX opportunity in the 2-5 year tenor.** India IT services (~$200B, ~6% GDP) and Philippines BPO (~$33-35B, ~8% GDP) face 60% task automation potential by 2030-2040 (McKinsey). NDF curves price these currencies based on rate differentials and short-term capital flows, with zero structural break embedded for AI disruption. The asymmetry: the gradual erosion (not sudden stop — refuted per KB) means the opportunity is in selling long-dated vol or positioning via 2-3 year NDF forwards against the prevailing carry, with the degradation path providing a slow-moving but persistent edge.

10. **The dollar correction, when it comes, will be 15-25% over 3-5 years and will be triggered by AI capex cycle maturation — not by twin deficit dynamics, rate convergence, or dedollarization.** The trigger taxonomy: (a) rate convergence alone is insufficient (EUR/USD barely moves despite 100bp+ narrowing — Claim 4); (b) twin deficit is non-binding with AI inflows growing (Claim 3); (c) dedollarization is a 0.5-0.7pp/yr stock adjustment, not a flow catalyst; (d) AI capex maturation (capex/revenue normalizing from 4-6x toward 1.5-2x, hyperscaler capex growth decelerating from 30-50% to 5-10%) removes the structural capital account support. Historical calibration: DXY from ~120 peak (2001) to ~80 trough (2008) was 33%, driven by tech capex cycle completion + twin deficit normalization. Current starting overvaluation is smaller (~12-18% vs BEER), suggesting a 15-25% correction to DXY low-to-mid 80s over 3-5 years. Confidence in direction is high; confidence in magnitude and timing is low (Claim 3's arithmetic shows the non-binding constraint can persist for years).

## Evidence & Reasoning

**Claim 1 (Capex-Sustained Plateau):**
- DXY at 103-107 despite 100-150bp Fed cuts confirms rate differentials are not the primary driver
- TIC data: net equity inflows $40-60B/month concentrated in technology (KB: `dollar_overvaluation_ai_inflows`, confidence 8)
- BEA financial account: $300-400B annual net inflows to US equities
- EPFR: $300B+ into US tech-focused funds vs $45B EM outflows over 18 months
- Historical parallel: 1997-2001 DXY +25% when Fed was not systematically tighter than peers
- The self-funded nature of current capex (OCF 1.5-1.7x coverage, KB confidence 9) structurally extends the plateau vs credit-funded analogues

**Claim 2 (Dollar as AI Call Option):**
- DXY-SOX correlation elevation from ~0.15 to ~0.45-0.55 is observable in rolling correlations
- DeepSeek event: CNY strengthened ~1%, NVDA fell ~17% intraday (KB: `china_ai_competitiveness_catalyst`) — dollar weakened on the day, confirming the transmission channel
- Passive allocation (~55% of US equity AUM in indexed vehicles) accelerates transmission speed vs prior cycles
- Momentum factor 70%+ overlap with AI names (KB confidence 9) means factor unwind transmits to FX
- No standard FX factor model (UIP, PPP, BEER) includes an AI-equity beta term — this is a genuine model gap

**Claim 3 (Twin Deficit Non-Binding but Tightening):**
- Fiscal deficit $1.8-2T (6-7% GDP) + CA deficit 3-4% GDP = ~$1.5-2T annual external financing need
- AI-related inflows: $200-350B (portfolio equity) + $200-250B (capex-related) ≈ $400-600B
- Gap: ~$900B-$1.4T financed by official reserves, dollar invoicing inertia, and structural absence of alternatives
- IMF COFER: USD reserve share declining at 0.5-0.7pp/yr — a stock adjustment, not a flow catalyst
- Capex-to-revenue ratio trajectory (4-6x current → 1.5-2x target by 2028-2030) provides the timeline for inflow deceleration
- BEER fair value estimate: DXY overvalued by 12-18% on PPP-adjusted, terms-of-trade-adjusted basis

**Claim 4 (EUR/USD Structural Mispricing):**
- ECB-Fed rate differential narrowed ~100bp+ with minimal EUR/USD response — the residual is the AI premium
- AI capex/GDP differential: US ~1.0% vs eurozone ~0.15-0.2% (KB: `monetary_policy_divergence_usd_bid`, confidence 7)
- US ~65% of top 100 AI companies by revenue; EU ~5-10% (KB evidence)
- ECB impossible-trinity bind: EUR depreciation from AI outflows imports inflation, constraining easing
- The rate differential component is cyclical (12-18 month horizon); the AI premium is structural (3-5 year)
- EU AI Act regulatory cost may widen rather than narrow the investment gap

**Claim 5 (USD/JPY Structural Dislocation):**
- Japan AI capex/GDP 0.1-0.3% vs US 1.0% — the widest AI investment gap among G10
- Japanese life insurer/pension unhedged foreign asset accumulation ($500B+) creates structural JPY short
- BoJ policy rate at 0.25-0.50% vs Fed 4.25-4.50% — even with normalization to 1%, gap remains ~300bp
- JPY-VIX correlation weakening from -0.6 to -0.25 impairs traditional risk-off hedging properties
- The structural savings export channel from aging demographics + AI capital seeking combine to sustain weakness

**Claim 6 (TWD/KRW Benchmark Mispricing):**
- TWD/KRW-SOX correlation rising from 0.3 to 0.7 (KB: `twd_krw_semiconductor_decoupling`, confidence 6)
- Taiwan CA surplus ~14% of GDP from TSMC dominance
- TSMC AI/HPC revenue from ~10% to ~50% of total — the currency is a semiconductor derivative
- Traditional EM factor R-squared declining
- EM index construction (MSCI EM, JPM EMBI) does not adjust for this reclassification

**Claim 7 (Petrodollar-to-Teradollar):**
- Extension of Gopinath dominant currency paradigm to cloud/AI services (KB: `dollar_invoicing_premium_ai`, confidence 6)
- US ~65-70% of global hyperscale cloud revenue (~$350B, growing 20-25%)
- Switching costs: data gravity + API lock-in create stickier demand than oil invoicing
- Concentration risk: ~50 hyperscale customers vs 190 oil-importing nations
- Cloud subscription revenue is recurring and contractual, unlike volatile commodity flows

**Claim 8 (China Binary Swing Factor):**
- TIC data: $200-400B cumulative portfolio flow redirection from China to US tech (KB confirmed)
- China ADR market cap declined from ~$2T to ~$800B-$1T; net FDI turned negative
- DeepSeek R1 demonstrated frontier capabilities at fraction of training cost — the success scenario is real
- US-China tech bifurcation accelerating: semiconductor export controls, entity list, CHIPS Act
- 25/50/25 probability distribution across success/stalemate/failure (KB calibration)

**Claim 9 (Services EM CA Erosion):**
- India IT services ~$200B/yr (~6% GDP); Philippines BPO ~$33-35B (~8% GDP) (KB confirmed)
- McKinsey: 60% of current BPO tasks automatable by 2030-2040
- NDF curves embed zero structural break premium — confirmed by absence of term structure inversion
- Philippines: $100B reserves, IG rating, flexible FX — not crisis-prone, but structural erosion is distinct from sudden stop
- "Sudden stop" framing properly refuted (KB); this is comparative advantage erosion over 5-25 years

**Claim 10 (Dollar Correction Trigger Taxonomy):**
- Rate convergence insufficient: demonstrated by EUR/USD inelasticity to 100bp+ narrowing
- Twin deficit non-binding while AI inflows grow: Claim 3 arithmetic
- Dedollarization: 0.5-0.7pp/yr reserve share decline is background noise, not catalyst
- AI capex maturation: the only trigger with sufficient magnitude ($200-350B annual flow reduction)
- Historical calibration: 2001-2008 DXY correction was 33% from peak, triggered by tech capex cycle completion
- Current overvaluation smaller (~12-18% vs BEER), suggesting 15-25% correction

## Connections to Other Topics

**To Rates/Monetary Policy:**
- The `fed_ai_r_star_trilemma` directly feeds Claim 5: BoJ faces an amplified version where AI investment differential creates an additional structural channel beyond rate differentials. The bimodal terminal rate distribution (2.0-2.5% vs 4.5-5.0%) implies a bimodal dollar: strong-dollar scenario under AI realization (higher US r*, persistent capital inflows) vs weak-dollar scenario under AI bust (aggressive Fed easing, capital repatriation). The `central_bank_ai_put` (475bp net easing asymmetry from 1998-2001 precedent) is the mechanism through which dollar correction would overshoot on the downside — the Fed eases aggressively into a tech bust, collapsing rate differentials and removing both supports simultaneously.

**To Equity Markets:**
- The `ai_equity_concentration_amplification` (top 10 at 35-38% of S&P vs 27% at 2000 peak) is the transmission mechanism from equity repricing to FX. The SBC reflexivity loop ($14-16/share S&P 500 impact) operates as an amplifier: in a correction, rising perceived SBC costs compress margins, accelerating equity outflows and dollar weakness. The `mag7_implied_revenue_gap` ($600B-$1T incremental required) is the fundamental vulnerability that the capex dollar is pricing over — when the revenue gap becomes undeniable (capex/revenue fails to improve from 4-6x), the equity-to-FX transmission activates.

**To Credit/Macro:**
- The `kalecki_identity_capex_profits` creates a macro-to-FX channel: AI capex (~$250B) sustaining aggregate profits sustains the capital account surplus that supports the dollar. A 25% capex cut reduces profits by ~$60-75B, which reduces foreign investor appetite for US equities, which weakens the capex dollar. The `credit_channel_bifurcation` (85-90% AI capex self-funded) means this channel operates outside the traditional monetary transmission mechanism — the Fed cannot influence the capex dollar through rate policy. The `minsky_speculative_ponzi_transition` in leveraged lending creates a secondary channel where credit stress in non-AI sectors could trigger risk-off flows that paradoxically strengthen the dollar short-term (safe-haven bid) before weakening it medium-term (recession easing).

**To Fiscal Policy:**
- The twin deficit dynamic (Claim 3) intersects directly with fiscal sustainability concerns. DOGE-driven fiscal consolidation is paradoxically dollar-negative: reducing the fiscal deficit shrinks the Kalecki profit floor, which reduces equity earnings, which reduces capital inflows, which weakens the capex dollar. This creates a counterintuitive dynamic where fiscal responsibility weakens the currency — the opposite of textbook predictions — because the marginal dollar of fiscal spending is generating more capital inflow through the profit-equity-capital flow loop than the credibility premium from fiscal consolidation.

**To Energy Transition:**
- The `energy_ai_nexus` (150-300+ TWh by 2028-2030) creates a secondary dollar demand channel through USD-denominated energy infrastructure investment. PJM capacity auction prices up 10x, natural gas forwards shifted $0.50-1.00/MMBtu, nuclear restarts — all predominantly USD-denominated investment. This reinforces the Teradollar framework (Claim 7) by adding energy infrastructure to compute infrastructure as a dollar demand driver. The CPI impact (0.06-0.24pp) is macro-immaterial for FX purposes, but the capital flow impact ($50-100B annually in energy-related AI infrastructure) is material.

**To Trade/Deglobalization:**
- US-China tech bifurcation (Claim 8) is a trade fragmentation story with direct FX implications. Semiconductor export controls create a bifurcated supply chain where TWD/KRW benefit from "friend-shoring" while CNY suffers from exclusion. The EM tripartite segmentation (AI-adjacent, services-threatened, commodity-adjacent) maps directly onto deglobalization winners and losers, creating a new FX factor that should be incorporated into EM allocation models.

## Open Questions

1. **Is the DXY-SOX correlation causal or driven by a common factor (global risk appetite, USD funding conditions)?** If common factor, the dollar's beta to AI is lower than Claim 2 estimates, and the correction trigger taxonomy (Claim 10) needs revision.

2. **At what capex/revenue ratio do AI capital inflows begin to decelerate — is there a threshold effect or a gradual fade?** The difference matters enormously for the dollar correction path: threshold implies sudden correction; gradual implies slow depreciation that carry strategies can absorb.

3. **Can the AI capital flow premium and the rate differential premium be decomposed with sufficient precision to trade?** If not, Claim 4's EUR/USD framework is analytically useful but not actionable, and the mispricing may persist indefinitely.

4. **Does BoJ normalization to 1%+ overwhelm the AI capital flow channel for USD/JPY, or are they additive?** If BoJ at 1% + AI capital flows = USD/JPY 140-145 (rate convergence dominates) vs USD/JPY 155-160 (AI flows dominate), the answer determines whether JPY longs are viable.

5. **How does the globalization of AI data center construction (projects in Malaysia, Saudi Arabia, Indonesia) change the US-centric capex dollar thesis over a 3-5 year horizon?** If AI infrastructure investment diversifies geographically even while US companies finance it, the capital account support weakens faster than the revenue capture.

6. **Is the "capex dollar" a permanent structural feature or a cyclical phenomenon with a defined lifespan?** Historical tech investment cycles (railroads, telecom) suggest 7-15 year capex supercycles with 3-5 year correction periods. If AI capex is mid-cycle (~3 years in), the plateau could extend to 2028-2030 before correction begins — longer than most positioning horizons.

7. **What is the correct FX hedge ratio for a portfolio with significant AI equity exposure, given the novel USD-AI correlation?** Traditional currency hedging frameworks assume FX and equity risks are separable; the AI-driven correlation violates this assumption, potentially requiring dynamic hedge ratios that adjust with the capex cycle phase.

8. **Does the Teradollar framework survive if European/Asian cloud hyperscalers (OVHcloud, Alibaba Cloud) gain meaningful share in their home markets?** Digital sovereignty initiatives (EU Data Act, GDPR localization) could create regional cloud champions that erode the US invoicing premium from 65-70% to 45-50% over 5-10 years — material for the structural dollar thesis.

9. **Is the CNY a better short than EUR for expressing AI-driven dollar strength, given China's AI trajectory uncertainty and capital account pressures?** The bifurcation: EUR/USD has a rate convergence tailwind working against the AI premium, while USD/CNH has both rate differential and AI capital outflows working in the same (dollar-positive) direction, but with higher geopolitical risk of discontinuous reversal.

10. **The capex-equity-FX correlation asymmetry: do long-dollar hedges provide adequate diversification during AI tech selloffs, or does the dollar weaken simultaneously with equities (as in the DeepSeek event)?** If the latter, the traditional role of dollar hedges in equity portfolios is impaired, requiring alternative risk management (long JPY convexity, long gold, EM segmentation trades) that account for the novel correlation regime.
