# Credit-Equity Linkage & Contagion Channels — Chief Synthesis

## HIGH_CONFIDENCE

**1. The Maturity Wall ($900B-$1.1T, 2025-2027) is the Deterministic Clock on Credit-Equity Contagion**
- **Confidence: 9/10**
- **Originating agents:** credit_analyst_01 (9/10), credit_analyst_02 (9/10), generalist_01 (7/10), generalist_02 (7/10), macro_strategist_01, macro_strategist_02, rates_strategist_01
- **Surviving evidence:** This is arithmetic, not forecast. Bonds/loans issued at 3-5% (HY) and 2-3% (IG) must refinance at 7-9% and 5-6% respectively. Average 400bp coupon step-up on $900B yields ~$36B incremental annual interest expense — a 14% increase for HY universe, or 3-5pp FCF margin erosion. For borrowers at true 7.5-8.5x leverage (post-addback), debt service consumes 70-80% of actual cash EBITDA at 500bp+ OAS. This forces a binary outcome: refinance at higher costs (equity repricing via earnings compression) or default (credit repricing via loss realization). Survived all four debates without challenge. The only uncertainty is the rate/spread environment at refinancing time.

**2. Credit Generally Leads Equity by 2-6 Months in Fundamental Deteriorations, but the Lead-Lag is Regime-Dependent**
- **Confidence: 8/10**
- **Originating agents:** credit_analyst_01 (8/10), generalist_02 (8/10), macro_strategist_01, macro_strategist_02 (8/10); challenger_01 partially contra
- **Surviving evidence:** 5 of 6 major episodes since 1990 show credit leading or moving simultaneously with equity. The regime-dependent formulation won the debates: credit leads in fundamental deteriorations (2007-08 canonical case, 6-8 month lead); equities lead or move simultaneously in sentiment/exogenous shocks (2020, 2018 Q4). Challenger_01's own table concedes the 2007 case and shows simultaneous movement in 4 of 7 episodes — which still supports the "credit does not lag" claim. The credit_analyst_01 formulation — that the *direction* of lead-lag is diagnostic of the shock type — is the most actionable version. Note: passive/ETF flows may be compressing the lead time toward zero, per generalist_02's Open Question 1.

**3. EBITDA Addbacks (15-40%) and Covenant-Lite (>90%) Create Hidden Leverage and Delayed Default Recognition**
- **Confidence: 8/10**
- **Originating agents:** credit_analyst_01, credit_analyst_02, generalist_01, macro_strategist_01, macro_strategist_02
- **Surviving evidence:** ECB and Fed regulatory analyses document the 15-40% addback range. >90% of leveraged loans lack maintenance covenants. True leverage of 7.5-8.5x vs. reported ~6x. Default models trained on pre-2010 covenanted data are miscalibrated for the current universe. Covenant-lite delays default 12-24 months but increases loss-given-default — the "fewer but larger" pattern with estimated recoveries of 40-50 cents vs. historical 60-70 cents. Combined effect: the Merton distance-to-default is significantly narrower than reported financials indicate, and observable credit metrics understate true cycle positioning by an indeterminate but material amount. Survived all debates.

**4. Current HY OAS (350-420bp) is Unambiguously Below Historical Stress Thresholds**
- **Confidence: 9/10**
- **Originating agents:** All 8 agents
- **Surviving evidence:** Below the minimum threshold for equity stress in every historical analogue. 40th-55th percentile historically. No agent disputes this. The corollary — that any shift toward the 450-600bp zone would be significant — is also consensus.

**5. Credit-Equity Correlation is Regime-Dependent, Not Constant**
- **Confidence: 8/10**
- **Originating agents:** generalist_02 (7/10), macro_strategist_01 (7/10), rates_strategist_01, challenger_01, macro_strategist_02
- **Surviving evidence:** Normal-period correlation ~0.3-0.4; crisis correlation ~0.7-0.9. Crisis contagion regime active only ~7% of calendar time (challenger_01's time-weighted analysis: 30 of 420 months since 1990). Positive co-movement regime active ~49% of time. Generalist_02 documents a critical pattern: correlation *drops* to 0.3-0.5 during early stress (Merton model decouples) before snapping to 0.8-0.9 during acute stress (forced selling creates mechanical linkages). This instability at regime transitions means the Merton model is least reliable when needed most. Implications: risk models using constant or full-sample correlations systematically misprice tail risk; portfolios designed for crisis correlation are suboptimal 85% of the time.

**6. The Kalecki Fiscal Channel (6-7% Deficit) is the Critical State Variable Currently Sustaining Both Credit Quality and Equity Valuations**
- **Confidence: 8/10**
- **Originating agents:** macro_strategist_01, macro_strategist_02 (7/10), generalist_01 (references), credit_analyst_01 (implicit)
- **Surviving evidence:** Kalecki-Levy identity mechanically channels fiscal deficits to corporate profits. This sustains interest coverage ratios (supporting credit quality) and the P/E denominator (supporting equity valuations) simultaneously. The pair_2 debate identified this as the "most important consensus finding" — the system's resilience is fiscally contingent, not structurally earned. Fiscal consolidation exceeding 2pp GDP would, per the Minsky-Kalecki framework, trigger credit cycle downshift within 4-6 quarters. The artificial decorrelation between credit spreads and equity risk premia is a Minsky "speculative" phase signal: surface stability concealing growing fragility.

**7. CRE-to-Bank-Equity Contagion Operates Through a Multi-Stage Regional Bank Balance Sheet Mechanism**
- **Confidence: 7/10**
- **Originating agents:** All 8 agents agree on mechanism
- **Surviving evidence:** ~$2.7T bank CRE exposure; regional banks at 28-30% CRE concentration (vs. 6-8% for largest banks). Office down 25-40% from peak. Three-stage mechanism: mark-to-market → provisioning → lending pullback. 1990 and 2023 as templates. Banking channel involvement is the key severity discriminant (generalist_02: median 38% drawdown with banking channel active vs. 22% without, duration 18-30 months vs. 3-6 months). **However:** challenger_01 persuasively argues this is now consensus, not contrarian — KRE persistently shorted since March 2023, CMBS BBB- at 800-1200bp implying 40-60% cumulative loss, every major sell-side desk has published the report. The "Japan model" (slow extend-and-pretend) is the more probable resolution than sudden cascade, given 13-14% CET1 ratios and regulatory forbearance incentives. Confidence 7/10 on *mechanism*, 5/10 on *timing*, 4/10 on *full contagion cascade* per challenger_01's joint probability analysis (3-8%).

**8. Private Credit ($1.7T) Represents an Unprecedented Opacity Risk — Existence is Certain, Spillover Magnitude is Not**
- **Confidence: 7/10 on opacity, 5/10 on spillover magnitude**
- **Originating agents:** All 8 agents
- **Surviving evidence:** Mark-to-model practices, quarterly reporting lags, manager-determined NAVs. PIK toggle usage at 5-8% (up from ~2% in 2022). Outside all public indices, default statistics, and financial conditions measures. No historical analogue at this scale — generalist_02's base rate framework (5 analogues: S&Ls, SIVs, junk bonds, Japanese jusen, AT1) shows 60-80% disorderly repricing rate with 4-5 year median lag, but analogues are structurally dissimilar. Three identified spillover channels: (a) semi-liquid vehicle redemption cascades (BDCs, interval funds, ~$300B), (b) insurance company RBC rebalancing (~$700B exposure), (c) information contagion when first forced mark-to-market reprices the whole sector. The mark-to-recovery gap could be 5% or 25% — this range is too wide to be actionable. All debates confirmed this is the highest-importance, lowest-precision risk in credit-equity linkage.

**9. Credit → Equity → Labor Sequencing (3-12 Month Leads) is Empirically Robust**
- **Confidence: 9/10**
- **Originating agents:** credit_analyst_01, generalist_01, generalist_02, macro_strategist_01, macro_strategist_02
- **Surviving evidence:** Documented across 2000-01, 2007-09, and 1990 episodes. KB entry at confidence 9. Theoretically grounded in both New Keynesian (external finance premium is forward-looking; labor is lagging) and Post-Keynesian (endogenous money creation → spending → income → profits → equity validation → labor adjustment) frameworks. The credit-equity linkage analysis provides the structural mechanism for this previously empirical observation.

## MODERATE_CONFIDENCE

**1. The Merton Model is Necessary but Insufficient for Credit-Equity Linkage**
- **Confidence: 7/10**
- **Originating agents:** credit_analyst_01 (9/10 → revised), credit_analyst_02 (8/10 critique), macro_strategist_01 (8/10), macro_strategist_02 (8/10 critique), challenger_01 (7/10)
- **Status:** All debates converged on "useful foundation, dangerous if treated as complete." KMV-Merton explains 50-70% of cross-sectional CDS spread variation (Bharath & Shumway 2008). But: systematic Type I errors of 40-60%; credit spread puzzle (underpredicts IG by 50-100bp); mechanically irrelevant for the ~35% of S&P 500 index weight held by net-cash mega-caps. Correct synthesis: Merton for within-regime relative value; Minsky/regime frameworks for identifying transitions. Neither alone is sufficient.

**2. An HY Spread Threshold Zone Exists (~450-600bp) Where Credit Stress Becomes Self-Reinforcing, but the Precise Level is Regime-Dependent**
- **Confidence: 6/10**
- **Originating agents:** credit_analyst_01 (600bp, 7/10), credit_analyst_02 (500bp, 5/10), generalist_01 (550-650bp, 5/10), macro_strategist_01 (500-550bp, 6/10), macro_strategist_02 (regime-dependent, 6/10), challenger_01 (contra)
- **Status:** All specific thresholds were refuted for false precision across multiple debates. The directional claim survived: above some spread level, primary market access shuts down (credit_analyst_01), CLO arbitrage economics invert (credit_analyst_02), and the maturity wall becomes a solvency wall. Generalist_02's regime-dependent framing won: breadth of widening and banking system involvement matter more than the absolute level. Challenger_01's correction is important — only 4 of 11 episodes where HY crossed 500bp produced >20% equity drawdowns, and the signal is largely redundant with recession indicators. The Kalecki fiscal buffer may currently elevate the threshold by 100-150bp (macro_strategist_02), but this higher threshold is itself destabilizing because more damage accumulates before the signal fires.

**3. The Credit Impulse Remains a Valid but Structurally Degraded Leading Indicator for US Equities**
- **Confidence: 6/10**
- **Originating agents:** credit_analyst_01 (8/10), macro_strategist_01 (7/10), macro_strategist_02 (7/10) validate; challenger_01 (8/10) documents degradation
- **Status:** IS-curve mechanism is textbook and BIS-validated. But challenger_01's R² decline from 0.38 (1975-2007) to 0.12 (2010-2025) is significant. The structural shift — bank credit-to-GDP flat since 2008, corporate bond market doubled, private credit from $200B to $1.7T, buybacks averaging ~$800B/yr — means bank credit impulse misses the dominant funding channels for the equity-cap-weighted economy. The credit impulse still captures the SME/consumer channel (~40% of economy) and may still predict the *macro* catalyst for drawdowns even if it doesn't explain mega-cap returns. The private credit measurement gap (unmeasured $1.7T impulse) is the most important blind spot both sides identify. Currently sending a divergent signal from equity pricing — decelerated from 2023-2024 peak while equities expanded.

**4. CLO OC Test Mechanics Create a Discrete, Non-Linear Contagion Channel**
- **Confidence: 7/10**
- **Originating agents:** credit_analyst_02 (8/10), credit_analyst_01 (references)
- **Status:** Identified in pair_1 debate as the "single most specific and actionable contagion mechanism." When CCC-bucket holdings breach the 7.5% threshold, cash flow diversion from equity to senior tranches triggers simultaneously: (a) CLO equity return destruction, (b) forced selling in leveraged loan markets, (c) shutdown of new CLO formation (removing ~65-70% of leveraged loan demand). This is a step function, not a smooth degradation. The ~$1T CLO market makes this systemically relevant. Key unknown: current CCC-bucket utilization across the market (4% = comfortable buffer; 6.5% = one downgrade wave from cascade). Data exists (Intex, LCD) but is not freely available.

**5. Reverse Causality Through Synthetic Credit Contaminates the "Credit Leads Equity" Signal**
- **Confidence: 6/10**
- **Originating agents:** challenger_01 (7/10)
- **Status:** CDX HY open interest grown from ~$200B to ~$900B; credit ETFs at ~$400B. Mechanism: equity vol rises → managers buy CDX protection or short HYG as proxy hedge → mechanical spread widening → analysts cite "credit stress" → equity investors sell → confirmation loop. Supported by intraday evidence (CDX lags SPX by 5-30 minutes during stress) and ETF flow data (largest outflows correlate with equity vol spikes, not credit-specific events). Dealer inventory decline from ~$250B to ~$30-40B amplifies non-fundamental flow impact. Pair_3 debate identified this as an important novel insight. Implication: a significant portion of high-frequency credit-equity correlation is equity → credit, not credit → equity. The signal is partially "eating its own tail."

**6. Fallen Angel Risk ($350-500B BBB at Elevated Downgrade Risk) is a High-Impact Near-Term Transmission Channel**
- **Confidence: 7/10**
- **Originating agents:** credit_analyst_01 (8/10)
- **Status:** BBB is the largest single rating cohort (~$3.5-4.0T). IG-mandated funds (~$2T AUM) must sell within 30-90 days post-downgrade — creating concentrated supply shock in HY markets. Fallen angel equity reprices 15-30% around downgrade events. Ford/GM 2005 template: $200B+ moving from IG to HY created multi-month dislocation and 100-150bp HY spread widening. Pair_1 debate flagged credit_analyst_02's failure to address this as a "glaring omission." Not corroborated by other agents but also not challenged — this is an underappreciated channel.

**7. The Financial Accelerator (BGG) Amplifies Credit-Equity Transmission by ~1.5-2x and is Currently "Loaded"**
- **Confidence: 7/10**
- **Originating agents:** macro_strategist_01 (7/10), macro_strategist_02 (agrees via Minsky framework)
- **Status:** Embedded in Fed DSGE models (FRB/US, SIGMA). Amplification factor from calibration exercises, not direct measurement. The key insight: EBITDA addback distortion means the accelerator is more loaded than baseline calibrations suggest — true leverage of 7.5-8.5x implies a narrower distance-to-default and a higher external finance premium elasticity than observable data indicate. Both NK and Post-Keynesian frameworks arrive at the same conclusion: the feedback loop (equity decline → collateral impairment → tighter credit → further equity decline) is quantitatively important and currently primed.

**8. Banking Channel Involvement is the Key Severity Discriminant in Credit-Equity Episodes**
- **Confidence: 8/10**
- **Originating agents:** generalist_02 (8/10)
- **Status:** Banking channel active (1990, 2007-08): median equity drawdown -38%, duration 18-30 months. Banking channel inactive (1998, 2015-16, 2020): median equity drawdown -22%, duration 3-6 months. Pair_0 debate confirmed this is "the most actionable insight" — monitor bank equity and bank CDS to distinguish contained from systemic. Operational signpost framework (generalist_02): if regional bank equities fall in sympathy with HY widening, the 1990/2007 template is activating. If HY widens but bank equity is stable, it's a 2015-16-style contained episode.

**9. Term Premium (Not Rate Level) is a Primary Common Discount-Rate Channel**
- **Confidence: 7/10**
- **Originating agents:** rates_strategist_01 (8/10)
- **Status:** Term-premium-driven yield rises hit credit and equity simultaneously without growth offset, unlike rate-expectations-driven rises. Confirmed empirically by 2013 Taper Tantrum, 2018 Q4, 2023 Q3 — all produced synchronized credit-equity weakness. Supply-driven term premium (post-2020 fiscal expansion, $2T+ annual net issuance) is a qualitatively different and worse type of tightening than policy-driven. Pair_3 debate partially supported: challenger_01 correctly notes mechanism operates on shrinking share of credit universe due to private credit migration, but the mechanism remains the most actionable for *observable* markets.

**10. Basis Trade ($800B-$1T, 50-100x Leverage) Creates a Novel Cross-Market Contagion Channel**
- **Confidence: 7/10**
- **Originating agents:** rates_strategist_01 (8/10), macro_strategist_01 (references)
- **Status:** Mechanism: unwind → Treasury selling → swap spread widening → higher funding costs → reduced dealer liquidity → wider credit bid-asks → forced selling by levered credit funds → VIX spike → equity risk-parity deleveraging. Partially validated by September 2019 repo crisis and March 2020 Treasury dysfunction. Fundamentally alters the flight-to-quality dynamic — Treasury market itself becomes a contagion source rather than safe haven. Pair_3 debate identified this as rates_strategist_01's strongest unique contribution. Key uncertainty: current size and leverage (may have grown or shrunk since estimate).

**11. The Modified Taylor Rule Includes a De Facto Credit Spread Term**
- **Confidence: 7/10**
- **Originating agents:** macro_strategist_01 (8/10)
- **Status:** Curdia-Woodford (2010) formalization. ~100bp HY spread widening ≈ 25-50bp FFR tightening equivalent. Fed's revealed behavior is consistent: 2018 Q4 pivot (spreads widened → "patient"), March 2020 (emergency response to spread blowout), 2023 pause (banking stress as substitute for rate hikes). Gilchrist-Zakrajsek Excess Bond Premium predicts GDP at 1-4 quarters with R² of 15-25%. Tradeable implication: if spreads widen 200bp, expect 50-100bp of "shadow easing" from the Fed. This partially endogenizes the HY contagion threshold — the Fed will likely ease before primary markets shut down, unless inflation blocks it.

## LOW_CONFIDENCE

**1. Structural Decoupling of Credit and Equity Market Composition**
- **Confidence: 4/10**
- **Originating agent:** challenger_01 (7/10)
- **Status:** The factual observation is correct: top 10 S&P 500 hold ~$500B net cash, Merton linkage is mechanically zero for them; HY issuers are $2-5B market-cap leveraged companies — "small-cap leveraged equity dressed in fixed-income clothing." But pair_3 debate partially refuted the strong form: demand channel breaks decoupling (Apple doesn't need credit; Apple's customers do). The weak form (HY signals are more relevant for Russell 2000 than S&P 500) survives as a useful portfolio construction insight. The decoupling thesis also assumes current tech mega-cap dominance is permanent, which is a bet on equity market composition.

**2. Multi-Step Contagion Chains Complete Only ~15-25% of the Time**
- **Confidence: 5/10**
- **Originating agent:** challenger_01 (6/10)
- **Status:** Based on 6 episodes (too few for statistical inference). Median completion rate ~38%. Policy intervention is the primary chain-breaker. The framework is disciplinarily useful as a counterweight to narrative-driven tail-risk analysis, but the specific probability is unreliable at ±10pp uncertainty. Private credit spillover chain requires 6 steps, historically suggesting 15-25% completion — "significant enough to monitor but far from base case."

**3. Private Credit Spillover as Sectoral Balance (Flow-of-Funds) Disruption Rather Than Price Contagion**
- **Confidence: 5/10**
- **Originating agent:** macro_strategist_02 (7/10)
- **Status:** Godley framework: private credit channels savings into corporate spending via Kalecki identity; disruption to flow is more fundamental than price contagion. Complementary to portfolio balance channel (macro_strategist_01: acute, days-to-weeks) — this is the chronic transmission (quarters-to-years). Neither agent recognized these as different time horizons of the same phenomenon. Theoretically rigorous but empirically untested.

**4. Credit Impulse Structural Break for the US (R² from 0.38 to 0.12)**

The R² decline could reflect (a) genuine structural break (post-GFC funding shift), or (b) extended lag structure (12-18 months vs. 6 months) as credit transmits through longer intermediation chains. The impulse still captures SME/consumer channel. Testing requires variable-lag VAR specifications over longer windows.

**5. Higher Pain Threshold (Due to Fiscal Buffer) is Itself Destabilizing**

- **Status:** Classic Minsky insight applied to credit-equity dynamics. If the Kalecki fiscal buffer raises the HY threshold to 550-600bp, more credit damage accumulates before equities register the problem — analogous to suppressing small forest fires. Conceptually powerful but isolated to one agent and lacks empirical calibration.

**6. Yield-Curve-Mediated Contagion Sequence (Inversion → Bear Steepener → Credit Blowout → Equity Drawdown)**

Pair_3 debate downgraded from "contagion follows this sequence" to "bear steepening is a risk factor worth monitoring." The bear steepener phase as uniquely dangerous (long end repricing without growth offset) retains independent value even without the full sequence.

**7. 5y5y Forward as Joint Credit-Equity Constraint**
- **Confidence: 5/10**
- **Originating agent:** rates_strategist_01 (7/10)

But the specific 4.5% threshold is approximate and the 5y5y-equity relationship may have structural breaks from the QE era. Useful as directional framing, not as a precise trigger.

## REFUTED

**1. HY OAS-to-VIX Ratio as "Single Best Real-Time Indicator"** (rates_strategist_01, 6/10)
- **Killed by:** Pair_3 debate. Challenger_01's full-sample evidence shows only 36% hit rate (4/11) for HY threshold → equity drawdown. Self-acknowledged lack of backtesting. Specific thresholds (15, 25) are post-hoc calibration. Sensitive to VIX methodology and HY composition changes. A heuristic at best, not "the single best" anything.

**2. "Shadow Term Premium" as a Quantifiable Concept** (rates_strategist_01, 5/10)
- **Killed by:** Pair_3 debate. Unfalsifiable by design — the opacity IS the problem. Author's own caveat: "could be 5% or 25%." A worry dressed in term-premium terminology. The underlying concern (private credit adds hidden risk to the cost of capital) is valid; the term-premium formulation adds false precision.

**3. "Mainstream Structural Models All Fail"** (macro_strategist_02, 9/10)
- **Killed by:** Pair_2 debate. Category error: Merton models aren't used for macro regime forecasting. BGG and Curdia-Woodford partially address the endogenous credit critique within the NK framework. SFC/Minsky models remain computationally impractical and lack real-time forecasting track records. Downgraded from 9/10 to ~6/10. The critique has theoretical merit; the sweeping dismissal does not.

**4. Contagion Direction Has "Reversed"** (credit_analyst_02, 7/10)
- **Killed by:** Pair_1 debate. The *mechanisms* have evolved (CLO plumbing, warehouse lines, fund financing vs. mortgage-backed securities), but the *direction* (credit stress → equity impact) is the same. Framing a change in pipes as a change in flow direction is misleading and could cause practitioners to dismiss valid historical patterns.

**5. Equity Options Contain "More Accurate" Credit Information Than Credit Spreads** (generalist_01)
- **Killed by:** Pair_0 debate. Conflates speed of repricing with accuracy of signal. A fast-repricing but noisy instrument (equity-specific factors: buybacks, index rebalancing, options market-making) isn't necessarily superior to a slower but structurally informed one (CLO managers with bottom-up analysis). No empirical validation provided.

**6. Credit-to-Equity Causal Primacy is Universal** (macro_strategist_02, 8/10)
- **Killed by:** Pair_2 debate. Fails the 2020 test (simultaneous exogenous shock), 2022 test (rate-driven equity duration hit), and dot-com episode (equity bubble collapsed first, credit stable 18 months). The weaker claim — "credit-to-equity primacy holds in credit-cycle-driven downturns" — survives. The universal claim does not.

**7. Specific HY Thresholds as Precise Point Estimates** (multiple agents: 500bp, 550-650bp, 600bp)
- **Killed by:** Multiple debates. All specific numbers carry false precision from samples of 4-6 heterogeneous episodes. Market structure changes (CLOs, ETFs, private credit) mean historical thresholds are not directly transferable. The directional claim (a threshold zone of ~450-600bp exists, regime-dependent) survived; point estimates did not.

**8. "Faster Contagion = Lower Net Risk for Medium-Term Investors"** (challenger_01, strong form)
- **Killed by:** Pair_3 debate. The temporal compression for non-recessionary episodes is real (post-GFC crises resolve in 6-7 months vs. 10-12 months). But the argument excludes 2001-02 and 2007-09 as "genuine recessions" — which are precisely the scenarios where credit-equity contagion matters most. A framework that shows crises are less dangerous after excluding the dangerous crises is circular.

## KEY_DISAGREEMENTS

**1. Does the Credit Impulse Retain Predictive Power for US Equities?**
- **Pro:** credit_analyst_01 (8/10), macro_strategist_01 (7/10), macro_strategist_02 (7/10) — IS-curve mechanism is standard, BIS-validated, still captures SME/consumer channel.
- **Contra:** challenger_01 (8/10) — R² collapsed from 0.38 to 0.12 post-GFC; post-GFC funding has shifted to retained earnings, bond markets, and private credit; bank credit impulse is irrelevant for the mega-cap tech sector driving 35% of index returns.
- **Resolution needed:** Decompose credit impulse into bank vs. non-bank components; test variable-lag specifications; determine whether the credit impulse predicts *macro recessions* (still likely) even if it no longer predicts *S&P 500 returns* (plausibly degraded).

**2. Can the Kalecki Fiscal Channel Indefinitely Mask Credit Deterioration?**
- **Pro (unlimited masking):** The 6-7% fiscal deficit mechanically sustains profits, keeping distance-to-default wide. If deficits continue, the credit-equity contagion mechanism may never activate at the macro level.
- **Contra (temporary masking):** macro_strategist_02 argues masking is a Minsky "speculative" phase — the higher pain threshold means more damage accumulates before the signal fires. credit_analyst_01 argues the Kalecki floor is sufficient for IG/upper HY but insufficient for lower HY/leveraged loans where firm-level interest expense overwhelms aggregate demand support.
- **Resolution needed:** Determine the interaction between micro credit stress (maturity wall at the firm level) and macro profit support (fiscal deficits at the aggregate level). Is the threshold number of simultaneous defaults required to overwhelm the Kalecki floor reachable during the maturity wall window?

**3. Acute vs. Chronic Private Credit Spillover Pathways**
- **Acute channel (macro_strategist_01):** Forced selling by common asset owners (insurance RBC, pension rebalancing, BDC redemptions) — price/liquidity transmission in days-to-weeks.
- **Chronic channel (macro_strategist_02):** Credit flow withdrawal reduces spending, erodes Kalecki profits, and degrades the private sector financial surplus — flow-of-funds transmission in quarters-to-years.
- **Resolution needed:** Neither agent recognized these as complementary time horizons. The acute channel determines whether there's a *market* crisis; the chronic channel determines whether there's an *economic* crisis. Both are plausible; neither is quantifiable with available data.

**4. Has Passive/Algorithmic Trading Compressed the Credit-Equity Lead-Lag to Near-Zero?**
- **For compression:** Credit ETFs, risk-parity, and algorithmic market-making create mechanical linkages absent from the 1990/2001/2007 analogues. The 2020 episode (simultaneous) may be representative of the new microstructure.
- **Against compression:** Fundamental credit analysis still has informational lead time. CLO/private credit structures still suppress credit price discovery, maintaining the "credit moves slowly" pattern.
- **Resolution needed:** High-frequency lead-lag analysis across pre-2015 vs. post-2015 samples. If lead times have compressed to <1 month, much of the credit-as-leading-indicator thesis becomes operationally irrelevant.

**5. Whether CRE-Bank Contagion is a Genuine Tail Risk or Priced Consensus**
- **Tail risk:** credit_analyst_01, credit_analyst_02, generalist_01, macro_strategist_01 — the mechanism is live, losses are large ($100-200B+), and Stage 3 (lending pullback) is untested.
- **Priced consensus:** challenger_01 (8/10) — KRE shorted since March 2023, CMBS BBB- at 800-1200bp, every sell-side desk has published the report; joint probability of full cascade is 3-8%; "Japan model" slow bleed is more probable.
- **Resolution needed:** The risk-reward for *trading* CRE-bank contagion may already be unfavorable (it's priced), even if the *analytical* mechanism is correct. These are different questions that the debate conflates.
