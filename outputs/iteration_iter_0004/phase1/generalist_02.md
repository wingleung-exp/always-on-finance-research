## Key Claims

1. **Credit-equity contagion follows a reliable directional sequence — credit stress leads equities by 2-6 months in 5 of 6 major episodes since 1990 — but the magnitude and duration vary dramatically depending on whether the banking system is the transmission mechanism.** When banks are the channel (1990, 2007-08, 2023 regional), equity drawdowns are 2-4x larger and longer than when the stress is contained to capital markets (1998, 2015-16, 2020).

2. **The HY OAS threshold at which equity markets suffer sustained drawdowns is not fixed but regime-dependent: the critical level has ranged from ~500bp (2015-16) to ~1000bp (2007-08) to ~1100bp (2020), with the discriminating variable being whether the spread widening reflects idiosyncratic sector stress or systemic funding withdrawal.** The current HY OAS range of 350-420bp is firmly in the "benign" zone across all historical analogues.

3. **CRE-to-bank-equity contagion follows a specific historical template best mapped by the 1990-92 and 2023 episodes: CRE valuations decline → bank loan loss provisions rise → bank equity selloff → credit contraction to broader economy → broader equity weakness.** The lag from initial CRE stress recognition to peak bank equity impact has historically been 12-24 months, and the severity is a function of bank CRE concentration (regional banks in both episodes).

5. **Private credit's current scale (~$1.7T AUM) has no true historical analogue — this is genuinely unprecedented territory for credit-equity linkage analysis.** The closest structural analogues are the S&L industry in the 1980s (opacity, mark-to-model, regulatory lag) and the SIV/conduit sector pre-2008 (off-balance-sheet, maturity mismatch, liquidity illusion), but both are imperfect. 6. **The Merton model structural framework — where equity is a call option on firm assets and credit spreads should co-move with equity volatility — holds well empirically in aggregate but breaks down at the sector level and during regime transitions, which is precisely when it matters most.** Historical decomposition shows the credit-equity correlation is ~0.7 in normal periods but drops to 0.3-0.5 during the first 1-3 months of a stress episode before snapping back to 0.8-0.9 as contagion broadens.

### Claim 2: HY OAS Threshold Is Regime-Dependent

Historical decomposition of the HY OAS level at which equities entered sustained drawdowns:

- **2015-16**: HY OAS reached ~500bp in energy sector; S&P fell ~14%. But the stress was contained because energy was ~15% of HY index and banking system CRE exposure was minimal.
- **2000-01**: HY OAS hit ~750bp; equity drawdown deepened as telecom defaults cascaded.
- **2007-08**: HY OAS crossed 500bp by mid-2007 but equities didn't break down until 800bp+ in Q3 2008, and reached 2000bp+ at the March 2009 trough.
- **2020**: HY OAS spiked from ~350bp to ~1100bp in three weeks; equities fell 34% but recovered in 5 months due to Fed intervention.

The key insight is not the absolute spread level but the *rate of change* and the *breadth of widening*. When widening is concentrated in one sector (energy 2015-16, telecom 2001), equity contagion is limited. When widening broadens to include financials and investment-grade crossover credits, the equity impact multiplies. This is because broad-based widening signals funding market stress, not just fundamental deterioration.

Current HY OAS at 350-420bp is below the minimum threshold for equity stress in any historical analogue. This corroborates the existing knowledge base assessment.
