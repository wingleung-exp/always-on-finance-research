## HIGH_CONFIDENCE

**1. Inflation passthrough bimodality: a 2-3x multiplier exists between anchored and de-anchored regimes.**
- **Confidence: 9/10**
- **Originating agents:** quant_researcher_01, quant_researcher_02, generalist_01, commodities_analyst_02, risk_analyst_01
- **Surviving evidence:** Cross-country panel data across 40+ economies (Carrière-Swallow et al. 2016, Forbes 2019). Replicated across research groups, sub-sample analysis (pre/post-GFC), alternative inflation measures, and different shock identification strategies. Both quant agents independently converge at confidence 9 — the only claim where this occurs. The regime boundary *location* remains unidentified, but the *existence* of bimodality is the single most empirically grounded finding in the entire knowledge base. No agent challenged it; no debate weakened it.

**2. Wage-price spiral probability is low (<10-15%) under current conditions.**
- **Confidence: 9/10**
- **Originating agents:** quant_researcher_01, quant_researcher_02 (implicit), equity_analyst_01 (reference)
- **Surviving evidence:** 2/~16 post-war cycles produced true spirals. Current conditions satisfy 0/3-4 conjunctive prerequisites (union density, COLA coverage, sustained wage growth >5%, expectations de-anchoring). Conjunctive probability framework is sound. The 2021-23 natural experiment (2/3 conditions temporarily met, no spiral) provides genuine out-of-sample validation. Structural decline in union density and COLA coverage is secular, not cyclical. No agent contested this finding.

**3. The shelter/OER lag will create a "mission accomplished" trap window, likely Q2-Q4 2026.**
- **Confidence: 8/10**
- **Originating agents:** equity_analyst_01, generalist_01, generalist_02, quant_researcher_02, risk_analyst_01
- **Surviving evidence:** OER deceleration from ~5.0% to ~3.5-4.0% delivers 40-80bp of mechanical headline CPI relief — this is arithmetic, not forecast. The 4/6 base rate for premature "mission accomplished" during Phase 2 goods disinflation is consistent across agents. The 1983 and 1995 precedents specifically involved shelter-led headline declines. The Fed's revealed reaction function makes premature dovish signaling during this window likely. All four debate reports confirmed this as the highest-conviction shared finding. The sole uncertainty is whether services ex-shelter decelerates enough independently to prevent the trap. The 1995 exception required productivity growth at 2.5%+, which is absent today.

**4. The equity-bond correlation sign-flips when inflation persistently exceeds ~2.5%, destroying 60/40 and risk parity diversification assumptions.**
- **Confidence: 8/10**
- **Originating agents:** quant_researcher_02, generalist_01
- **Surviving evidence:** Well-grounded in academic literature (Ilmanen 2011, Campbell-Sunderam-Viceira updates). Post-GFC calibration assumed negative equity-bond correlation (-0.3 to -0.4) under core PCE of ~1.5-1.8%. When inflation persistently exceeds 2.5%, the correlation averages +0.2 to +0.4. If the structural floor has indeed shifted to 2.3-2.8%, risk parity and mean-variance portfolios calibrated to the 2010-2020 covariance matrix are structurally mispriced. The pair_2 debate affirmed this as "the single most consequential parameter change" for multi-asset portfolios. Confidence is 8 rather than 9 because the exact inflation threshold for the flip is sample-dependent.

**5. Services inflation persistence is the binding constraint on disinflation — directional finding.**
- **Confidence: 7/10 (downgraded from KB's 9)**
- **Originating agents:** equity_analyst_01, generalist_01, generalist_02, risk_analyst_01, commodities_analyst_02 (implicit)
- **Surviving evidence:** Services CPI at 3.5-4.0% with strong unit labor cost support (3.0-4.0%). The 0/5 base rate for resolution without recession is directionally informative — resolution without demand destruction appears historically rare. generalist_02 expands to 1/7 including 1994-95 (productivity-assisted success absent today). **However:** quant_researcher_01's statistical critique is decisive for calibration — the Clopper-Pearson 95% CI is [0%, 52.2%], and the 5 episodes are not i.i.d. draws from a stationary process. challenger_01 correctly identifies novel disinflationary channels (immigration surge of 3-4M workers, sectoral AI deployment) with no analogue in the reference set. The direction is supported; the KB's stated certainty (confidence 9, "never resolved") overstates what n=5 can support.

**6. Cross-asset markets price mutually inconsistent inflation outcomes.**
- **Confidence: 7/10**
- **Originating agents:** generalist_01, equity_analyst_01, generalist_02, risk_analyst_01, quant_researcher_02
- **Surviving evidence:** Equity multiples (P/E ~20-21x) embed a disinflationary glide path. Credit spreads (IG ~90-100bp, HY ~300-350bp) embed a soft landing. The rates curve prices 3-4 cuts by year-end. These cannot all be correct simultaneously given services persistence findings. generalist_02's "financial conditions paradox" validates from history: 4/5 episodes of services >3.5% saw loosening financial conditions extend the credit cycle by 12-18 months. The inconsistency is the diagnosis; the *resolution direction* is where agents diverge (see KEY_DISAGREEMENTS). Note: the breakeven-specific component of this inconsistency is weaker than originally claimed (see REFUTED #1).

**7. Covenant-lite structures (>90% of leveraged loans) delay default transmission by 12-24 months, converting gradual adjustment into clustered stress.**
- **Confidence: 7/10**
- **Originating agents:** equity_analyst_01, generalist_01, generalist_02, quant_researcher_02, risk_analyst_01
- **Surviving evidence:** >90% covenant-lite is an observable market structure fact. The 15-40% EBITDA addback phenomenon overstates credit quality metrics. The delay-then-burst mechanism has structural analogues (LTCM, SVB — opacity creates information gaps that resolve suddenly). The 2020 episode was too brief and rapidly reversed by policy to be fully informative, but the mechanism is well-identified. quant_researcher_01's caveat is valid — the counterfactual (what Phillips Curve would have done without covenant-lite) is unobservable, so causal claims about macro impact remain identification problems.

**8. The Kalecki fiscal channel (6-7% GDP deficits) is suppressing defaults by 100-150bp of spread equivalent, creating an unstable equilibrium.**
- **Confidence: 7/10**
- **Originating agents:** equity_analyst_01, generalist_01, generalist_02, risk_analyst_01, challenger_01
- **Surviving evidence:** All four debate reports confirmed this as load-bearing and fragile. The channel's direction is uncontested — fiscal deficits flow into corporate revenues and credit metrics. The fragility is also uncontested: any material fiscal consolidation (DOGE, debt ceiling, reconciliation) withdraws this support. The magnitude estimate (100-150bp) is model-dependent and sensitive to fiscal multiplier assumptions (0.5-1.5), per quant_researcher_01. The unprecedented scale of the deficit during a non-recession, non-war disinflation attempt means no historical analogue fully validates the estimate.

## MODERATE_CONFIDENCE

**1. The structural inflation floor has shifted upward — direction supported, magnitude genuinely uncertain.**
- **Confidence: 7/10 direction, 4-5/10 magnitude (30-80bp range)**
- **Originating agents:** equity_analyst_01, generalist_01, commodities_analyst_02, quant_researcher_02
- **Challenging agents:** challenger_01, quant_researcher_01
- **Status:** Direction is supported by multiple independent lines: housing shortage is physical (3.5-5.5M units), deglobalization is observable, healthcare cost inflation is structural. quant_researcher_01 demonstrates the honest CI is approximately [-10bp, +100bp] when methodology uncertainty and correlated errors across components are incorporated. The additive decomposition (housing 10-20bp + deglobalization 10-20bp + healthcare 10-15bp) implies false precision because component errors are correlated. commodities_analyst_02 adds 5-10bp from agricultural structural factors. The lower bound includes zero — this must be stated explicitly in all downstream analysis that uses this estimate.

**2. The Fed's reaction function exhibits a dovish asymmetry — qualitative existence supported, specific magnitude fragile.**
- **Confidence: 6-7/10 qualitative, 4/10 for ~200bp magnitude**
- **Originating agents:** equity_analyst_01, generalist_01, generalist_02, risk_analyst_01
- **Challenging agents:** challenger_01, quant_researcher_01
- **Status:** The political economy argument (recession cost is politically salient; 2.8% inflation is not) provides independent theoretical support beyond the single-cycle observation. But quant_researcher_01's critique is dispositive on the magnitude: estimating two thresholds from two observations is a just-identified system with zero degrees of freedom. Multi-cycle evidence shows asymmetry is not a structural constant — absent under Greenspan 1994-95, reversed under Volcker. The 2025-2026 framework review and FOMC composition changes could shift it. Confidence properly attaches to "the current FOMC exhibits dovish bias" — not to a permanent structural feature.

**3. The three-phase disinflation sequencing (goods → shelter → services) is a useful descriptive framework, not a precision timing model.**
- **Confidence: 6/10 (downgraded from KB's 8)**
- **Originating agents:** generalist_01, generalist_02, equity_analyst_01, quant_researcher_02
- **Challenging agents:** quant_researcher_01
- **Status:** Pattern-matched from 6 episodes with researcher degrees of freedom in defining phases, thresholds, and "trap" declarations. quant_researcher_01's demand for pre-registered predictions is well-taken — without this, the 4/6 "trap" rate is susceptible to hindsight bias. The framework's value is heuristic (it identifies a sequencing pattern worth monitoring), not predictive (it cannot time factor rotations or sector trades at the specificity some agents attempt).

**4. Agricultural supply shock tail severity is increased by the biofuel mandate amplifier.**
- **Confidence: 7/10 for the amplifier mechanism; 6/10 for base probability of activation**
- **Originating agents:** commodities_analyst_02 (primary), generalist_01 (connection)
- **Status:** The biofuel amplifier (Claim 3, self-assessed 9/10) is the strongest contribution from commodities_analyst_02. The arithmetic is clean: ~40% inelastic corn demand from RFS mandates means the residual 60% absorbs 100% of supply shocks, amplifying price moves by 1.5-2.5x. This is demand elasticity math applied to a legislatively observable fact. The simultaneous multi-crop tightness across wheat/corn/soybeans impairing cross-substitution buffers is a valid structural concern. However, pair_1 correctly rules that the proposed upgrade from confidence 6 to 7 on base probability requires confirming data (current WASDE, specific weather event, or triggered export ban) that has not yet materialized.

**5. The financial conditions paradox: falling headline inflation loosens conditions while the underlying regime is unresolved, extending credit cycles by 12-18 months.**
- **Confidence: 7/10**
- **Originating agents:** generalist_02 (primary)
- **Status:** Documented across 4/5 historical episodes (1967-68, 1984-85, 1995-96, 2005-06). The causal mechanism (falling headline → loosened financial conditions → extended credit cycle → delayed but amplified correction) is clear and consistent. The exception (1995-96) required productivity growth to validate the loosening ex post. This finding directly challenges the timing assumptions of agents proposing near-term credit repricing.

**6. A compound analogue framework is methodologically sounder than reliance on any single historical episode.**
- **Confidence: 7/10 for the approach**
- **Originating agents:** generalist_02 (primary)
- **Status:** pair_0 judged the compound approach stronger than single-analogue reliance. No episode scores above 7.0/10 similarity due to four structural novelties (mortgage lock-in, >90% covenant-lite, AI investment cycle, simultaneous deglobalization + fiscal expansion at 6-7% GDP). The caveat: the seven-dimension scoring creates an illusion of objectivity — dimension weights are subjective. The honest conclusion is that outcome distributions are wider than any single analogue framework suggests.

**7. Fiscal consolidation risk is the single largest analytical gap in the KB framework.**
- **Confidence: 6/10**
- **Originating agents:** challenger_01 (primary), risk_analyst_01 (partial)
- **Status:** challenger_01 correctly identifies that a fiscal impulse reversal from +1.5% GDP to -0.5% GDP would be a 2pp demand shock with no concept in the KB adequately modeling it. DOGE-driven spending reviews, debt ceiling dynamics, and reconciliation are real political vectors. The pair_3 debate confirms this as "the single most important gap neither agent fully resolves." This is both a credit risk (fiscal support withdrawal) and a disinflationary channel (demand destruction) that could short-circuit the sticky-inflation thesis from the demand side.

**8. The KB framework exhibits confirmation bias after 47 iterations, with zero concepts pointing toward faster-than-expected disinflation.**
- **Confidence: 7/10**
- **Originating agents:** challenger_01 (primary)
- **Status:** Verifiable by inspection. 20+ concepts and 15+ relationships in the KB; none articulate a disinflationary scenario. No falsification criteria are specified. The highest confidence scores (9/10) all confirm the "above-target but not spiraling" consensus. pair_3 confirms: "Agent B is stronger here — this is a structural critique that Agent A cannot answer by citing more evidence within the existing framework." The framework needs a disinflationary scenario branch regardless of whether the sticky-inflation thesis is correct.

**9. Credit factor signals are corrupted by EBITDA addback practices.**
- **Confidence: 6-7/10**
- **Originating agents:** quant_researcher_02 (primary)
- **Status:** >90% covenant-lite with 15-40% addbacks means reported EBITDA — the denominator in leverage and coverage ratios — is systematically overstated. A firm reporting 4.0x leverage may be running 5.5-6.5x on a cash-flow-consistent basis. This corrupts accounting-based distress factors (Z-score, O-score, distance-to-default) at the input level. pair_2 confirms as "novel and important." Open question: how much survives in market-implied distress measures (CDS-implied PD, option-adjusted spreads)?

**10. AI aggregate productivity gains will not resolve services inflation within 2026-2028, but sectoral deployment is already live in narrow CPI-relevant categories.**
- **Confidence: 6/10 (downgraded from KB's 8)**
- **Originating agents:** equity_analyst_01, risk_analyst_01 (aggregate claim); challenger_01 (sectoral nuance)
- **Status:** quant_researcher_01 correctly notes n=3 technology analogues under heterogeneous conditions cannot support confidence 8. challenger_01's distinction between hardware-adoption templates and software-distribution technology is valid — cloud-based deployment compresses adoption for specific use cases. pair_3 resolves: both aggregate (won't move TFP) and sectoral (already deployed in customer service, coding, legal review) can be simultaneously true. The CPI impact depends on the *weight* of affected sub-categories, which no agent quantifies. The gap between "AI is deployed" and "AI is disinflationary in CPI" (firms may capture gains as margin) is the key unresolved question.

**11. The "Sticky Above-Target" consensus is crowded and already priced, creating asymmetric contrarian payoffs.**
- **Confidence: 6/10**
- **Originating agents:** challenger_01 (primary)
- **Status:** BofA Global Fund Manager Survey shows "above-target inflation" as the most crowded macro view. CFTC net short Treasuries near 2-year extremes. Breakevens near 2.4-2.5% in 5Y. The contrarian disinflation scenario (core PCE 2.3-2.5% for 2-3 months) would produce a 50-100bp Treasury rally vs. 10-20bp remaining return to consensus — a 3:1 to 5:1 payoff ratio. The contrarian view does not require believing inflation returns to 2%, only that the consensus outcome is already priced and the bar for a positioning unwind is low. pair_3 calls this "the single most actionable insight" from the challenger analysis.

## LOW_CONFIDENCE

**1. The triple vulnerability stack (Fed asymmetry × covenant-lite delay × CLO OC mechanics) could produce an unprecedented crisis sequence.**
- **Confidence: 5/10**
- **Originating agent:** risk_analyst_01
- **Status:** Each component is individually documented at moderate-to-high confidence, but the *interaction* is theorized rather than empirically observed. No historical precedent for this exact combination. The delay-then-burst mechanism is conceptually sound (analogues: LTCM, SVB), but timing uncertainty is extreme. Valuable as a monitoring framework; not actionable as a positioning signal.

**2. BoJ normalization could withdraw Japanese CLO AAA demand, creating cross-border contagion.**
- **Confidence: 4/10**
- **Originating agents:** risk_analyst_01, generalist_01
- **Status:** Mechanistically plausible: BoJ hikes → JGB yields rise → reduced US CLO yield pickup → Japanese demand withdrawal → wider CLO liability spreads → reduced refinancing capacity for the maturity wall. But Japanese CLO holding data is imprecise, hedging behavior is unknown, BoJ normalization pace is uncertain. pair_3 rules this belongs in Open Questions, not Key Claims. The *identification* of the channel has option value for monitoring even if unquantifiable.

**3. The export ban reflexivity threshold has shifted upward by 2-5pp in stocks-to-use terms.**
- **Confidence: 5-6/10**
- **Originating agent:** commodities_analyst_02
- **Status:** The behavioral economics argument is sound — costless defection (India, Indonesia, Argentina, Turkey 2022-24) collapses cooperation equilibrium at lower stress. But the precise 2-5pp threshold shift is estimated, not measured. WTO's inability to discipline export restrictions is factual. The direction is plausible; the magnitude is uncertain.

**4. The commodity-equity correlation will step-function higher if expectations cross the de-anchoring boundary.**
- **Confidence: 4-5/10**
- **Originating agents:** generalist_01 (primary), quant_researcher_02 (supporting)
- **Status:** Depends on expectations drift mechanism (KB confidence 5) and a regime boundary location that is "genuinely unknown" (generalist_01's own words). pair_0 correctly calls this speculative rather than calibrated. The passthrough bimodality (confidence 9) provides the theoretical underpinning, but applying it to a specific cross-asset correlation prediction exceeds the evidence.

**5. Dual policy error exposure: Type I (premature easing) and Type II (insufficient tightening from lagging indicators) operating simultaneously.**
- **Confidence: 6/10**
- **Originating agent:** generalist_02
- **Status:** Novel conceptual contribution. Each error type is individually well-documented. The compound probability is novel and may be positively correlated (OER masking encourages premature easing = Type I, which is simultaneously Type II by definition). Historical sample insufficient to estimate compound probability directly. Valuable as a risk taxonomy; confidence is limited by the novelty of the combination.

**6. The "stall zone" (core PCE 2.5-3.5%) duration is 18-36 months from goods disinflation completion, with a fat right tail to 48+ months.**
- **Confidence: 5-6/10**
- **Originating agent:** generalist_02
- **Status:** Derived from 5 episodes: median 24 months, mean 25 months, range 15-36+. Structural novelties could compress or extend. Markets pricing the left tail (12-month resolution). The directional finding (markets are optimistic on timing) is more robust than the specific duration range.

**7. DM compositional heterogeneity in food CPI weights creates cross-country policy divergence risk from agricultural shocks.**
- **Confidence: 6/10**
- **Originating agent:** commodities_analyst_02
- **Status:** CPI weights are published facts: Japan 26%, EU periphery 18-22%, UK ~16%, US ~13.5%. The BOJ vulnerability (26% food weight + yen weakness) connecting to monetary normalization risk is a valid and underappreciated channel. But translating this into a tradeable cross-DM rates dislocation requires the agricultural shock to materialize first.

## REFUTED

**1. Breakeven right-tail underpricing as an empirically established mispricing.**
- **Killed by:** quant_researcher_01 (Claim 8), challenger_01 (Claim 8)
- **Confirmed in:** pair_2 ("does not survive"), pair_3 ("Agent B is decisively stronger")
- **Reasoning:** The inflation risk premium estimate of -10 to -20bp sits within TIPS liquidity premium uncertainty of -80bp to +40bp — a 120bp noise range that is 6-10x the signal. The sign of the inflation risk premium is itself uncertain. quant_researcher_02's theoretical argument (asymmetric reaction function → positive risk premium should hold) provides weaker directional support but cannot establish the empirical claim. The KB confidence of 6 should be downgraded to 4. Level breakeven trades are not actionable on this basis.

**2. The Taylor Rule coefficient has declined to 1.0-1.3.**
- **Killed by:** quant_researcher_01 (Claim 5), challenger_01 (Claim 5)
- **Confirmed in:** pair_2 ("not a robust econometric result")
- **Reasoning:** Estimated from a single observation (September 2024 cut at core PCE ~2.7%). Conflates the inflation coefficient with the output gap coefficient, the intercept (r*), and FOMC composition. Zero degrees of freedom for testing. Multi-cycle evidence shows the coefficient is regime- and personnel-dependent. KB confidence of 7 should be downgraded to 4. The qualitative observation that the current FOMC may be less aggressive than Volcker-era is trivially true and does not require this specific coefficient claim.

**3. The 1966-69 analogue at 6.5-7.0/10 similarity as an organizing framework.**
- **Killed by:** challenger_01 (Claim 4), generalist_02 (compound framework)
- **Confirmed in:** pair_0 (compound approach wins), pair_3 (non-matches are material)
- **Reasoning:** Five non-matching features are individually material: mortgage lock-in (85%+ below 5%, no 1960s analogue), >90% covenant-lite (fundamentally different credit channel), post-Volcker credibility endowment, demographic structure (1.5%+ vs ~0.5% working-age growth), globalized supply chains. Honest score: 5-6/10. The *mechanism* (premature easing → re-acceleration) survives as valid; the *episode* fails as a predictive template. The conditional base rate (3/3 accommodative → second wave) has Fisher p≈0.10 — not significant at conventional thresholds.

**4. The food-to-wage second-round channel adds 0.2-0.4pp to services wage pressure.**
- **Killed by:** pair_1 debate
- **Reasoning:** The expenditure share data is solid (bottom quintile spends 30-35% on food). But the causal chain requires low-wage workers to extract *additional* wages specifically from food costs, when in a tight labor market they may already be extracting maximum gains. The 0.2-0.4pp estimate has no cited empirical basis. Pair_1 assesses the realistic range as 0.05-0.15pp for DM — "analytically interesting but not portfolio-relevant."

**5. The international base rate expansion for services resolution (UK 1992-93, Canada 1996-97, Australia 2000-01).**
- **Killed by:** pair_3 debate
- **Reasoning:** These economies had different CPI compositions (lower services weight), different labor market institutions (more centralized bargaining), and different monetary frameworks (some adopting inflation targeting for the first time). Expanding the denominator with non-comparable episodes corrupts the estimate rather than improving it. challenger_01's immigration and sectoral-AI arguments survive; the international comparison does not.

**6. The 1946-48 analogue as "the most relevant" for goods re-inflation.**
- **Killed by:** pair_1 debate
- **Reasoning:** Post-WWII price control removal was a one-time regime shift; tariffs are incremental and sector-specific. Wartime savings drawdown has no current parallel (savings rates near historical lows, not highs). Monetary framework was pre-Accord, fundamentally different. The *abstract mechanism* (goods disinflation can reverse via demand/policy shocks) survives; the specific historical analogy does not warrant the "most relevant" designation. generalist_02's self-assessed 5.5/10 is generous.

**7. Duration crowding will produce 2-4 sigma losses in 2-4 weeks (specific magnitude claim).**
- **Killed by:** pair_2 debate
- **Reasoning:** The claim chains four sequential assumptions: OER relief → "mission accomplished" narrative → crowding into duration-sensitive assets → specific loss profile. The conjunctive probability of the full chain is substantially lower than any individual link. The three cited historical analogues (Aug 2007, Q4 2018, March 2020) each had different triggers and mechanisms. The directional concern (duration crowding from OER trap) survives; the specific magnitude and timeline do not.

**8. ENSO transition produces a 40-50% calibrated probability of major crop disappointment.**
- **Killed by:** pair_1 debate
- **Reasoning:** "At least one major crop disappointment" across ALL Southern Hemisphere crops AND South/Southeast Asian rice is a wide enough net that even the unconditional rate may approach this once independent trials are properly counted. The definition of "major" and "disappointment" is researcher-specified. commodities_analyst_02's own 5/10 confidence is appropriate. The directional claim (ENSO transitions increase weather variance) survives; the quantification does not.

## KEY_DISAGREEMENTS

**1. Will the cross-asset inconsistency resolve through inflation repricing upward or growth repricing downward?**
- **Inflation upward:** risk_analyst_01, equity_analyst_01, quant_researcher_02
- **Growth downward:** challenger_01
- **Both possible:** generalist_01, generalist_02
- **Why it matters:** Determines whether the correct portfolio tilt is long real assets/short duration (inflation resolution) or long duration/short credit (growth resolution). challenger_01's asymmetric payoff analysis suggests the market is paying you more for the growth-down scenario even if inflation-up is more likely.

**2. How regime-relevant are agricultural supply shocks for DM inflation?**
- **Primary driver:** commodities_analyst_02 (conditional probability upgraded to 25-30%)
- **Secondary/conditional amplifier:** equity_analyst_01, generalist_01
- **Why it matters:** Determines whether agricultural shocks deserve standalone portfolio hedging or are adequately captured by broader inflation protection. pair_1 resolves: the direct DM CPI impact (0.3-0.5pp anchored) is modest, but the biofuel amplifier and lowered export ban thresholds increase tail severity. The base probability upgrade requires confirming data not yet available.

**3. Can services inflation resolve without recession via novel channels (immigration, sectoral AI)?**
- **Yes, plausibly:** challenger_01 (immigration surge, sectoral AI deployment)
- **No, historically unprecedented:** equity_analyst_01, generalist_02, risk_analyst_01
- **Statistically ambiguous:** quant_researcher_01 (CI includes up to 50%)
- **Why it matters:** This is the central question of the entire inflation regime analysis. The KB framework has no concept exploring the implications of a faster-than-expected disinflation scenario. challenger_01's falsification criteria (three consecutive core PCE ≤0.2% m/m, services ex-shelter <3.5% without unemployment >4.5%) should be adopted regardless of which view is correct.

**4. Is the consensus "Sticky Above-Target" view already priced, making the contrarian trade superior on risk-reward?**
- **Consensus is priced, contrarian is superior:** challenger_01
- **Consensus is correct and not yet fully priced:** equity_analyst_01, risk_analyst_01
- **Why it matters:** challenger_01's 3:1 to 5:1 payoff ratio analysis is the most actionable framing in the entire synthesis. The question is not "who is right about inflation?" but "what is the market paying you to bet on?" This requires continuous positioning data monitoring.

**5. Will credit or equity reprice first?**
- **Credit first (CLO mechanics):** generalist_01, risk_analyst_01
- **Credit delayed by 12-18 months (financial conditions paradox):** generalist_02
- **Neither — both simultaneously:** equity_analyst_01
- **Why it matters:** Determines the timing of the relative value trade (long inflation optionality funded by short credit). generalist_02's 4/5 historical base rate for credit cycle extension during headline disinflation masking directly challenges the near-term timing of short-credit trades.

**6. At what point does compound structural novelty invalidate analogue-based reasoning?**
- **Analogues remain useful with caveats:** generalist_02, equity_analyst_01
- **Analogues are approaching unreliability:** challenger_01, quant_researcher_01
- **Why it matters:** If the honest answer is "5-6/10 match to everything, 9/10 match to nothing," the entire base-rate methodology produces false precision. The epistemically honest conclusion may be that outcome distributions are wider than any analogue framework suggests — a finding with direct implications for position sizing and optionality structures.
