- **Confidence: 6/10**
- **Originating agents:** challenger_02 (9/10), generalist_02 (6/10), commodities_analyst_01 (5/10)
- **Evidence:** Full fiscal dominance: Fed capped long rates at 2.5%, debt/GDP peaked ~120%, inflation reached 20%+ during Korean War. - **Key insight:** The US HAS experienced full fiscal dominance and resolved it without catastrophe. This directly challenges the assumption that fiscal dominance is a one-way ratchet.

**2. The doom loop probability is materially lower than typically stated — approximately 20-30% over 5 years — due to the conjunction fallacy and underweighted circuit-breakers.**

- **Originating agents:** challenger_02 (8/10 on the critique), risk_analyst_01 (7/10 on the mechanism)

But the links are positively correlated, so the joint probability exceeds the independent product (pair_3 correction to challenger_02).

## KEY_DISAGREEMENTS

**1. Crisis vs. stagnation: What is the probable US fiscal dominance endpoint?**
- **Crisis camp:** risk_analyst_01 orients toward acute crisis mechanics (basis trade detonation, Dollar Smile bifurcation, Minsky moments). Language: "activation threshold," "doom loop," "tail risk."
- **Stagnation camp:** challenger_02 argues the modal outcome for own-currency issuers is slow degradation, not acute crisis. 0/5 advanced economy cases resulted in default or hyperinflation; 3/5 experienced chronic sub-performance. generalist_02's systematic tabulation supports this.
- **Unresolved because:** The existing analytical frameworks cannot distinguish between "approaching a crisis" and "settling into a sustainable but sub-optimal equilibrium." The policy implications of these two interpretations are nearly opposite (urgent consolidation vs. chronic management). The basis trade amplification mechanism is the key swing variable that could convert the stagnation path into the crisis path.
- **Research needed:** Define falsifiable criteria that would distinguish the two scenarios at the 3-year and 5-year horizon.

**2. Does reserve currency status permanently modify or merely delay the fiscal dominance threshold?**
- **Permanently modifies:** challenger_02 argues the threshold may be infinite for own-currency reserve issuers with deep domestic markets. Japan at 250%+ without activation is consistent.
- **Merely delays:** risk_analyst_01 argues reserve status raises the threshold but doesn't eliminate it. Declining indirect bidder share and CB gold buying signal erosion of the buffer.
- **Unresolved because:** n≈3 data points (US 1940s, UK post-war, Japan). This is a structural modeling question, not an empirical one. No amount of historical evidence can resolve it.

**3. Doom loop vs. fiscal dollar smile: Can both coexist?**
- **Identified by:** challenger_02
- **The contradiction:** The doom loop predicts fiscal expansion → higher term premium → fiscal stress → capital flight. The fiscal dollar smile predicts fiscal expansion → capital inflows → dollar strength → term premium compression. These make incompatible predictions about capital flows under fiscal expansion. If the dollar smile is correct, it suppresses the doom loop. If the doom loop activates, the dollar smile breaks.
- **Unresolved because:** The resolution likely involves sequencing (dollar smile holds in steady state; doom loop activates at a tipping point) but no agent specifies the transition mechanism or conditions.

**4. Japan: genuine counterexample or delayed case?**
- **Counterexample:** challenger_02 argues deploying Japan as both supporting evidence (progressed through all signals) and dismissing it when inconvenient (no crisis for 25 years) is an "unfalsifiable rescue hypothesis."
- **Delayed case:** risk_analyst_01 and others argue structural differences (>90% domestic ownership of JGBs vs. ~30% foreign Treasury ownership, current account surplus vs. deficit, higher savings rate) are genuine.
- **Pair_3 verdict:** "Closer to a draw." Both the epistemic critique and the structural differences are valid. The deployment of Japan in the analysis IS epistemically suspect, but the structural differences ARE real. Resolution requires falsifiable specificity about what would constitute Japan "arriving" at crisis vs. permanently avoiding it.

**5. Appropriate confidence calibration for an unprecedented regime.**

- **Unresolved because:** No agreed-upon methodology exists for calibrating confidence in genuinely novel situations.
