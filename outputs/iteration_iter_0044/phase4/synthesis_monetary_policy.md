## HIGH_CONFIDENCE

### 1. r* estimation uncertainty renders point estimates of policy stance unreliable
**Confidence: 9/10** | Agents: rates_strategist_01, challenger_02, generalist_01, generalist_02

The 480bp combined 90% CI across Laubach-Williams (1.18%), HLW (1.66%), and Lubik-Matthes (2.28%) exceeds the entire policy-relevant band. Universal convergence — challenger_02 calls it "the KB's best-calibrated concept," rates_strategist_01 demonstrates the 5y5y forward (~4.0-4.3%) embeds a compound bet on r* AND term premium that is internally inconsistent with at least one leading model. No agent contested this. The implication is foundational: any duration view built on a specific r* point estimate is building on sand.

### 2. The capex dollar ($40-60B/month tech equity inflows) has structurally disrupted the Fed-easing-to-EM-inflows transmission channel
**Confidence: 8/10** | Agents: fx_strategist_01, fx_strategist_02, generalist_01, generalist_02

TIC data, EPFR flows ($300B+ into US tech vs $45B EM outflows over 18 months), IIF EM flows ($5-10B/month vs $25B/month in 2019 easing cycle), and BEA financial account data all converge independently. Carry-to-vol compression to 0.3-0.4 (below the 0.5 systematic allocation threshold) confirms the structural nature. Debate pair_2 identified this as "the most robust shared finding." DXY at 103-107 despite 100-150bp of Fed cuts is directly observable and inconsistent with any standard interest rate parity model. The 1997-2001 dot-com dollar parallel provides historical context, though the current twin deficit (~6-7pp wider than late 1990s) suggests a sharper eventual correction.

### 3. Simultaneous rate cuts + QT is historically unprecedented and inherently unstable
**Confidence: 8/10** | Agents: generalist_01, generalist_02, rates_strategist_01, credit_analyst_02, challenger_02

Generalist_02 establishes a 0-for-3 base rate (though only the Fed 2019 case is a clean comparator, making it 0-for-1 with high-quality evidence). The current attempt at ~9 months has already seen QT slowed by 58% ($60B to $25B/month) — de facto partial abandonment. Credit_analyst_02 adds the novel observation that this configuration double-squeezes CLO economics: rate cuts reduce floating-rate loan income (hurting CLO equity) while QT widens CLO liability spreads (hurting CLO debt). The basis trade (~$800B-1T notional) is absorbing roughly half of QT's intended duration redistribution, creating synthetic QE that masks underlying tightness.

### 4. The $2.5T corporate maturity wall is the most reliable catalyst for full monetary transmission, with timing shifted to H2 2026-H1 2028
**Confidence: 8/10** | Agents: credit_analyst_02, generalist_01, generalist_02, rates_strategist_01

Mechanically determined from observable debt maturity schedules. Credit_analyst_02 updates: ~$400-500B of nearest-term maturities were pushed right through amend-and-extend activity in late 2024/early 2025. The peak now coincides with the expected QT endgame — a convergence treated as separate risks in the KB but identified as interdependent. Generalist_02 provides a 2-for-2 base rate (1989-91, 2001-02) for maturity walls under elevated rates producing credit stress. The refinancing math is stark: 2021-vintage deals face all-in coupons nearly doubling (from ~5% to ~8.75%), compressing interest coverage from 3-4x to 1.5-2x.

### 5. Non-GAAP earnings masking is the most empirically grounded monetary transmission finding
**Confidence: 8/10** | Agents: challenger_02, generalist_01, credit_analyst_02, generalist_02

The GAAP-to-non-GAAP gap has widened from $18-22/share to $28-38/share, with components directly traceable to rate increases (restructuring costs doubling $5 to $10/share, refinancing tripling $2 to $6/share, goodwill impairments rising $3 to $8/share). Even challenger_02 — the most aggressive skeptic — endorses this as "the KB's most empirically grounded insight." Verifiable from 10-K filings. Credit_analyst_02 extends to credit markets via EBITDA addback practices (median addbacks 25-40% of reported EBITDA for new LBOs). Combined: approximately 20-25% of monetary transmission's corporate profitability impact is invisible to dominant valuation frameworks.

### 6. Supply-demand technicals ($2.7-3.3T annual private-sector Treasury absorption) are a dominant structural driver of term premium
**Confidence: 8/10** | Agents: rates_strategist_01, generalist_01

Derivable from public data: QT runoff ~$300B/year + net new issuance ~$2.0-2.6T + declining foreign official demand (~$100-200B/year net selling) = ~$2.5-3.0T required private-sector absorption, at 5-6x QE-era levels. Observable evidence: 30Y auction tails averaging 1.5-2.5bp (vs 0.5-1.0bp in 2019), 20Y auctions consistently tailing 2.0-3.0bp. Not contested on the arithmetic by any agent.

### 7. Private credit ($1.7-2.0T) has structurally altered how credit risk manifests, deferring impairment recognition by 6-18 months
**Confidence: 7/10** | Agents: credit_analyst_02, generalist_01, generalist_02, challenger_02

Universal convergence on the structural shift. Credit_analyst_02's "Schrodinger's default" framing: BDC and interval fund NAVs are marked quarterly using manager-selected comparables, with an estimated 15-25% gap between reported NAVs and probable recovery values for bottom-quartile portfolios. Semi-liquid vehicle risk (quarterly/monthly redemption at NAV against illiquid assets) is a classic liquidity spiral setup. Combined with equity non-GAAP masking and credit EBITDA addbacks, an estimated 40-50% of monetary transmission may be invisible to conventional metrics. Reduced from 8 because the "40-50% invisible" aggregate is an estimate built on estimates.

### 8. Cross-asset markets embed mutually inconsistent macro stories
**Confidence: 7/10** | Agents: generalist_01, rates_strategist_01, generalist_02

Equities price soft landing (P/E 21-22x non-GAAP), credit prices perfection (IG at 90-110bp), rates price persistent inflation/fiscal risk (10Y at 4.2-4.5% with 50-120bp term premium), gold prices structural credibility loss ($400-600/oz residual). Rates_strategist_01 adds the 5y5y forward is consistent with equity valuations only if BOTH r* is 2.0-2.5% AND equity risk premium is permanently lower. Confidence 7 rather than 8 because different markets can price different probability distributions without being "wrong."

## MODERATE_CONFIDENCE

### 1. Monetary transmission eventually works — historical base rate ~100% across post-1985 cycles
**Confidence: 7/10** | Agents: challenger_02 (primary), generalist_02 (supporting)

Challenger_02's falsification table across five post-1985 cycles: 1994-95 (18 months), 1999-2000 (~18 months), 2004-07 (~24 months), 2015-18 (24+ months), 2022-25 (in progress). Every cycle featured credible "broken transmission" arguments; transmission always arrived through unexpected channels. The current 36-month lag with +0.3pp unemployment is within historical variance. Debate pair_3 confirmed this is "epistemically correct" but "operationally limited" for shorter-horizon positioning.

### 2. The maturity-dependent stock-bond correlation bifurcation is structurally significant
**Confidence: 7/10** | Agents: generalist_01 (originator), rates_strategist_01, generalist_02

Observable: 2Y-SPX at -0.25 to -0.35 vs 30Y-SPX at +0.10 to +0.25. Reflexive mechanism: positive long-end correlation reduces hedging demand, raising term premium, worsening hedge performance (30Y bid-to-cover declining from 2.4-2.6x to 2.2-2.3x). Debate pair_0 called this "the most actionable insight." Confidence 7 because 60-day rolling correlations are noisy and could partially reflect transient positioning.

### 3. Basis trade unwind would produce a discontinuous term premium shock of material but uncertain magnitude
**Confidence: 7/10** | Agents: rates_strategist_01, generalist_01, challenger_02 (partial)

$800B-1T notional absorbing ~50% of QT's duration redistribution. SRF untested at scale. Fed's 2x model miss in 2019 (reserve threshold $0.8T estimated vs $1.5T actual) proves the scarcity boundary is unknowable. Challenger_02 correctly identifies availability bias and notes institutional learning. Synthesis: probability of a 2019-style repo spike is lower (institutional adaptation), but probability of novel-form reserve stress remains underpriced. Specific magnitude estimates ("30-50bp" or "80-120bp missing") were refuted for false precision, but direction and fragility are well-established.

### 4. OPEC+ fiscal breakeven reflexivity intensifies pro-cyclical amplification of Fed policy
**Confidence: 7/10** | Agents: commodities_analyst_01 (primary), generalist_01 (supporting)

Saudi fiscal breakeven at $85-96/bbl creates a trigger zone below $75 for compliance breakdown. April 2025 OPEC+ decision (411 kb/d acceleration vs planned 138 kb/d) validates the reflexive dynamic. Challenger_02 considers this among the KB's better-calibrated claims. Confidence at 7 because precise breakeven levels are sensitive to Vision 2030 assumptions.

### 5. Discontinuous catch-up is the modal resolution for extended transmission lag episodes (3-for-4 base rate)
**Confidence: 7/10** | Agents: generalist_02 (primary)

1966-69, 2004-07, 2018-19 resolved via sudden discontinuity; 1994-95 was the exception (preemptive tightening succeeded). Debate pair_0 called this "the most important single insight." Confidence 7 because sample is only four episodes and current cycle has novel features.

### 6. CLO arbitrage deterioration creates structural demand withdrawal from leveraged lending
**Confidence: 7/10** | Agents: credit_analyst_02 (primary)

CLOs purchase ~65-70% of all new institutional leveraged loans. New-issue CLO equity IRR compressed to 10-12% (from 14-18% in 2021-2022), below the ~15% threshold for readily available CLO equity capital. The cuts+QT double-squeeze (rate cuts reduce floating-rate income, QT widens liability spreads) confirmed as "no historical precedent" in debates.

### 7. PIK toggle activation (5-8% to 12-18%) is the earliest observable symptom of monetary transmission reaching leveraged borrowers
**Confidence: 7/10** | Agents: credit_analyst_02 (primary)

PIK capitalization is economically equivalent to issuing new debt to service existing debt. Debate pair_1 called this "the single most actionable data point." Public default rates (~2-3%) significantly understate true credit stress. Confidence 7 because exact percentages vary by data source and the total PIK balance is unknown.

### 8. Dollar swap line access and PBOC BSA network are the new critical sudden stop differentiators
**Confidence: 7/10** | Agents: fx_strategist_02 (primary)

March 2020 natural experiment: 40-50% less depreciation for swap-line countries, controlling for fundamentals (Bahaj and Reis, 2022). PBOC BSA network (~$560B+ with 40+ central banks) creates parallel geopolitical safety net. Fx_strategist_02's highest-confidence claim at 8/10. Reduced to 7 because PBOC BSA effectiveness as crisis backstop is untested at scale.

### 9. The local-currency cost curve self-defeating mechanism is a validated structural dampener on commodity-channel transmission
**Confidence: 7/10** | Agents: commodities_analyst_01 (primary), fx_strategist_01 (supporting)

Fed tightening strengthens USD, lowering EM producer breakevens by 8-15% in BRL, CLP, ZAR, RUB terms, keeping marginal supply online, capping USD commodity prices. Validated across Codelco, Vale, Anglo American Platinum, Russian crude producers. Challenger_02 considers it well-calibrated.

### 10. SRF is inadequate for a CLO-driven liquidity event
**Confidence: 7/10** | Agents: credit_analyst_02 (primary)

CLO tranches are not SRF-eligible collateral. CLO warehouses are held by non-bank entities without SRF access. The transmission chain (bank contraction, warehouse collapse, CLO demand cliff, maturity wall crisis) is mechanistically clear. A 13(3) emergency facility would be needed. Confidence 7: high confidence in mechanism, lower in triggering probability.

## KEY_DISAGREEMENTS

### 1. Is monetary transmission "broken" or "delayed"?
- **"Delayed" camp** (challenger_02, 8/10): 100% base rate across 5 cycles; current lag within historical variance
- **"Broken/impaired" camp** (generalist_01, credit_analyst_02, commodities_analyst_01): multiple novel mechanisms (private credit, AI wealth effects, mortgage lock-in) create genuinely impaired channels
- **Resolution needed:** What unemployment rate at what lag would confirm genuinely broken transmission? Neither side has proposed a falsification criterion.
- **Best available framing** (from challenger_02): "Transmission operating with longer lags through different channels, consistent with the historical range of lag variation"

### 2. Is fiscal dominance a real constraint or revealed preference?
- **Preference** (challenger_02, 6/10): Volcker at 30% debt/GDP, ECB at Italy's 140%, UK gilt crisis demonstrate central banks can override
- **Constraint** (rates_strategist_01, generalist_01): at 120%+ debt/GDP with $1T+ interest expense, raising above 5.50% triggers debt service spiral
- **Missing argument:** Fiscal dominance likely operates as a *soft* constraint — technically surmountable but practically binding due to changed institutional context. The 90pp debt/GDP difference between Volcker and today matters but the counterfactual is untestable.

### 3. Does private credit change credit cycle dynamics or just delay them?
- **Delay** (generalist_01): risk surfaces as clustered NAV markdowns, 6-12 months later
- **Structural change** (generalist_02): Japanese bank forbearance analogy — extend-and-pretend can persist for years while compounding costs
- **Monitor:** PIK balances, semi-liquid vehicle redemption queues, BDC discount-to-NAV spreads

### 4. Gold residual: post-sanctions diversification or structural credibility erosion?
- If sanctions-driven: bounded, will plateau as central banks approach desired allocations
- If credibility-driven: could widen indefinitely as deficit trajectories worsen
- **Monitor:** If buying slows below 800 tonnes/year while gold holds above $3,000, credibility interpretation strengthens

### 5. Confidence calibration for n<=2 historical precedents
- Challenger_02: systematic overconfidence of 1.5-2.5 points on precedent-based claims
- Other agents do not apply this discount
- **Direction almost certainly correct** but the specific discount is itself imprecise

### 6. "Lead horse" for transmission resolution: credit channel or inflation channel?
- **Credit channel indicators:** HY OAS >550bp, private credit NAV markdowns >5% (2007 analogue)
- **Inflation channel indicators:** 5Y5Y breakeven >2.7% sustained (1966-69 analogue)
- **Current levels:** 5Y5Y ~2.4%, HY OAS ~350bp — neither activated
- Framework from generalist_02; inherently unresolved until data moves

## META-ASSESSMENT

### Calibration Observations
- **Well-calibrated claims** (confidence appropriate to evidence): r* uncertainty (9), non-GAAP masking (8), supply-demand technicals (8), capex dollar disruption (8)
- **Likely overcalibrated** (confidence too high relative to evidence): QT endgame at 8 should be 5-6 per challenger_02; toolkit bandwidth precision; BoJ shock magnitude
- **Potentially undercalibrated** (evidence stronger than confidence): PIK activation as leading indicator; SRF gap for CLO stress; Kalecki fiscal offset

### Key Structural Uncertainty
The single most important unresolved question: **Is the current cycle operating within the normal historical variance of transmission lags, or have structural changes (private credit at scale, AI productivity shock, post-pandemic fiscal dynamics) created genuinely novel impairments?** Challenger_02's base rate (100% for eventual transmission) and generalist_02's catch-up base rate (3-for-4 discontinuous) provide the strongest available frameworks, but neither is falsifiable in real-time. The signpost table above provides the closest approximation to a real-time monitoring framework.
