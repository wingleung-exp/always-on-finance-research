## HIGH_CONFIDENCE

**1. Credit-balance-sheet reflexivity creates self-reinforcing risk appetite regimes that persist beyond fundamentals in both directions.**
- **Confidence: 8/10**
- **Originating agents:** credit_analyst_01 (Claim 4, 9/10), macro_strategist_02 (Claim 1, 9/10), macro_strategist_01 (Claim 4, 7/10), credit_analyst_02 (implicit in CLO/private credit claims)
- **Surviving evidence:** Tight spreads lower refinancing costs → extend runways → delay defaults → validate risk-taking → tighter spreads. Formalized via Bernanke-Gertler-Gilchrist financial accelerator, Brunnermeier-Sannikov volatility paradox, and Minsky's hedge-speculative-Ponzi continuum. The 2015-16 energy cycle (400bp → 1200bp HY energy spreads, making refinancing impossible, accelerating defaults that widened spreads further) is the cleanest empirical demonstration. Both macro debates confirmed this as the strongest point of convergence across opposing theoretical traditions. The mechanism is self-evident: benign regimes persist because tight spreads create benign fundamentals, and vice versa — the only uncertainty is the threshold at which the feedback becomes self-reinforcing.

**2. The central bank put is structurally shallower and more conditional than in the 2010s.**
- **Confidence: 8/10**
- **Originating agents:** generalist_01 (Claim 6, 7/10), generalist_02 (Claim 4, 7/10), challenger_01 (Claim 4, 5/10), rates_strategist_01 (Claim 2, fiscal dominance regime), macro_strategist_01 (implicit), macro_strategist_02 (Claim 6)
- **Surviving evidence:** Six agents converge from three complementary framings that survived all four debates. (a) *Bifurcated put* (generalist_01): operational for financial plumbing breakdowns (March 2020 SMCCF, BOE 2022 gilt intervention), dormant for asset price declines (Fed inaction during 2022's 25%+ equity drawdown). (b) *Conditional credibility* (generalist_02): Fed has partially restored credibility (inflation 9% → ~3%) but not fully (still above target), mapping to 1995-96, where easing requires sufficient growth weakness — not pre-emptive. (c) *Fiscal dominance constraint* (rates_strategist_01, challenger_01): inflation above target + fiscal constraint + Treasury market fragility narrow the policy space. The implication across all three framings: 1998/2018-style "pivot rescue" scenarios where the Fed unilaterally restores risk appetite are less probable than consensus assumes. The 2022 UK gilt crisis is the closest analogue of a central bank forced to intervene against its inflation mandate. The debate pair_0 concluded these are "complementary framings of the same constraint" producing "a richer picture than either alone."

**3. CLO arbitrage economics are a binding, non-linear risk appetite mechanism for leveraged credit.**
- **Confidence: 8/10**
- **Originating agents:** credit_analyst_01 (Claim 7, 6/10), credit_analyst_02 (Claim 2, 9/10)
- **Surviving evidence:** CLOs hold ~65-70% of the $1.4T US leveraged loan market. When the spread between loan yields and CLO liability costs is positive, CLO creation floods the market with price-insensitive demand, compressing loan spreads and degrading documentation in a self-reinforcing loop. When arbitrage turns negative (e.g., late 2022 when AAA spreads widened), issuance effectively halts and the marginal buyer disappears. The ~$150B in annual CLO demand (credit_analyst_01) is mechanically deterministic, not sentiment-driven. Debate pair_1 rated this "one of the strongest claims in either analysis" — the mechanism is structural, the market share makes it binding, and the reversal is non-linear. credit_analyst_02 adds the critical stock-variable dimension: CLOs exiting reinvestment periods (2021-22 vintages approaching expiry) create calendar-determined demand withdrawal independent of arbitrage economics.

**4. The leveraged Treasury basis trade (~$800B+) is a systemic amplifier of regime transitions.**
- **Confidence: 8/10**
- **Originating agents:** rates_strategist_01 (Claim 4, 8/10), challenger_01 (within Claim 5), generalist_01 (within Claim 5)
- **Surviving evidence:** Well-documented by OFR, BIS, and Fed research. The trade (short cash Treasuries, long futures, leveraged 20-50x via repo) produces forced selling of cash Treasuries during unwinds, creating self-reinforcing term premium spikes. March 2020 is the unambiguous precedent: Treasury market dysfunction forced $1.6T in emergency Fed intervention. Debate pair_3 confirmed this as the strongest agreed claim. challenger_01 extends the systemic implication: basis trade unwind could impair the safe haven itself, producing simultaneous selloffs in risky and "safe" assets — a scenario RORO models cannot accommodate. The concentration of this trade means regime transitions can become non-linear at precisely the moment intermediation capacity is most needed.

**5. Dealer intermediation capacity has structurally declined relative to market size, impairing both cascade recovery and market function.**
- **Confidence: 8/10**
- **Originating agents:** challenger_01 (Claim 3), credit_analyst_01 (Claim 6), rates_strategist_01 (Claim 7), macro_strategist_02 (implicit)
- **Surviving evidence:** Bank dealer inventories ~75% below pre-GFC peaks (credit_analyst_01). Primary dealer government securities holdings as a share of outstanding debt at multi-decade lows (challenger_01). SLR and Basel III constraints binding. Meanwhile, credit fund AUM has grown 3-4x and total marketable Treasury debt has doubled since 2015. Not contested in any debate. This is the structural enabling condition for Claims 3, 4, and the broader cascade acceleration thesis: recovery requires end-investor capital rotation rather than dealer intermediation, which is inherently slower.

**6. Market microstructure accelerates but does not originate regime transitions.**
- **Confidence: 8/10**
- **Originating agents:** macro_strategist_01 (Claim 3, 8/10), macro_strategist_02 (Claim 5, 7/10), credit_analyst_01 (Claim 6), credit_analyst_02 (Claim 6)
- **Surviving evidence:** Clean convergence from opposing theoretical traditions. Passive flows, algorithmic market-making, risk-parity/vol-targeting/CTA strategies, and NBFI concentration compress the gap between displacement and revulsion but don't cause the underlying credit deterioration. Both macro debates confirmed this. The practical implication: structural risk models should calibrate amplitude and velocity to modern microstructure while locating causes in credit/monetary dynamics.

**7. Private credit (~$1.8T) masks observable risk appetite through mark-to-model suppression and creates a parallel, lagged risk cycle.**
- **Confidence: 7/10**
- **Originating agents:** credit_analyst_01 (Claim 5, 7/10), credit_analyst_02 (Claim 3, 8/10), challenger_01 (Claim 5), generalist_01 (Claim 4 connections), generalist_02 (Claim 8), macro_strategist_02 (Claim 5)
- **Surviving evidence:** Six agents converge on direction. Mark-to-model quarterly NAVs suppress observable volatility. Amendment rates of 15-20% in 2024-25 suggest underlying stress not reflected in reported performance. Deployment pressure from investment period expirations means GPs must lend even when pricing deteriorates (credit_analyst_02). The $200B+ in semi-liquid retail vehicles (interval funds, non-traded BDCs) held by 55+ HNW investors adds a fund-flow accelerant channel. Confidence reduced from potential 8 because debate pair_1 correctly flagged that "violent surfacing during regime transitions" is assumed, not demonstrated — private credit at this scale has never been through a full credit cycle.

**8. Dollar liquidity is the primary transmission channel for global risk appetite contagion.**
- **Confidence: 7/10**
- **Originating agents:** macro_strategist_01 (Claim 3, 8/10), macro_strategist_02 (Claim 7, 8/10), generalist_01 (FX connections)
- **Surviving evidence:** Miranda-Agrippino and Rey (2020) demonstrate a Global Financial Cycle driven primarily by US monetary policy. BIS estimates ~$13T in offshore dollar-denominated debt. macro_strategist_02 adds the Minskyan lens: EM borrowers earning in local currency and owing in USD are structurally Ponzi units when the dollar strengthens. Taper tantrum (2013), EM crisis (2018), and strong-dollar squeeze (2022) all followed this channel. Debate pair_2 confirmed as clean agreement.

## MODERATE_CONFIDENCE

**1. Cross-asset signal fragmentation resolves bearishly ~65-70% of the time, conditional on whether a credible Fed pivot materializes.**
- **Confidence: 6/10**
- **Originating agents:** generalist_01 (Claim 2, 7/10), generalist_02 (Claim 6, 7/10)
- **Status:** Independent convergence from two different methodologies (cross-asset ERP-MOVE divergence and expanded analogue set N=7). Both arrive at ~70% probability that equities reprice toward the more cautious rates/FX signal. However, debate pair_0 correctly weakened the precision: generalist_01's dismissal of the 2016-17 counter-example (which featured fiscal/QE support analogous to current conditions) was too convenient. The honest range is 60-75%, not ">70% from every comparable episode." The critical conditional: both agents identify that the 25-30% benign resolution requires a credible Fed pivot, which the "conditional credibility" assessment makes possible but constrained.

**2. The VIX has degraded as a risk appetite barometer due to 0DTE microstructure and systematic vol-selling.**
- **Confidence: 6/10**
- **Originating agents:** challenger_01 (Claim 2, 8/10), generalist_01 (ERP-MOVE divergence), rates_strategist_01 (MOVE/VIX ratio)
- **Status:** challenger_01's core evidence is strong: 0DTE options constitute 45-50%+ of SPX volume, overwhelmingly used for intraday income generation. Systematic vol-selling creates structural supply that depresses implied vol regardless of risk appetite. MOVE-VIX divergences in Q4 2025 (MOVE spiked while VIX subdued) are observable fact. Debate pair_3 confirmed this as the "strongest novel insight" from challenger_01. Confidence limited to 6 because only 1-2 agents deeply engage, and the claim that VIX is "broken" rather than "noisier" overshoots — VIX still responds to genuine equity stress events.

**3. Current (early 2026) credit market risk appetite is bifurcated across multiple dimensions simultaneously.**
- **Confidence: 6/10**
- **Originating agents:** credit_analyst_01 (Claim 10, 5/10), credit_analyst_02 (Claim 4, 7/10), generalist_01 (Claim 2)
- **Status:** Three distinct bifurcations identified: (a) IG near historical tights vs. CCC notably wider relative to BB compression (credit_analyst_01); (b) public credit normalized to mid-cycle vs. private credit in late-cycle aggressive deployment (credit_analyst_02); (c) equity risk premium in lowest decile vs. rates-implied uncertainty in highest quartile (generalist_01). Debate pair_1: "The combined picture is more concerning than either alone — risk appetite is fragmenting across multiple dimensions simultaneously." Confidence limited by inherent fragility of real-time regime assessment and private credit opacity.

**4. The 1965-69 period is the most underappreciated historical analogue for the current environment, pointing toward a "sideways nominal, declining real" outcome.**
- **Confidence: 6/10**
- **Originating agents:** generalist_02 (Claim 2, 7/10)
- **Status:** Debate pair_0 identified this as "the most valuable contribution in either analysis." The structural parallel table is striking: expansionary fiscal policy (~6% deficit), tight labor market (~4% unemployment), creeping inflation (~3%), technology investment boom (AI), ~20x forward P/E, reluctant central bank. The outcome template — DJIA not sustainably exceeding 1,000 in real terms until 1982 (16-year real drawdown) — introduces a scenario current positioning doesn't contemplate: neither crash nor continued expansion, but grinding P/E compression via inflation. Confidence limited because (a) single-agent origination, (b) significant market microstructure differences (pre-ETF, pre-passive, Bretton Woods), and (c) the analogue requires sustained above-target inflation without a crisis to activate a different template.

**5. Profit margin trajectory is the single best discriminator between recoverable corrections and secular regime breaks.**
- **Confidence: 6/10**
- **Originating agents:** generalist_02 (Claim 3, 8/10)
- **Status:** The binary partition across 13 episodes (expanding margins → recoverable in 8/8 cases; flat/declining → secular break in 5/5 cases) has zero misclassifications historically. Debate pair_0 confirmed the framework as "elegant and operationally useful." However, the same debate correctly noted the finding is currently uninformative: margins are "decelerating but not declining" — at the ambiguous boundary. The framework's high historical reliability (8/10) must be distinguished from its current applicability (~4/10). The next 2-3 quarters of margin data are the most important single variable for regime determination.

**6. Correlation *volatility* — not correlation level — is the primary portfolio diversification risk.**
- **Confidence: 6/10**
- **Originating agents:** generalist_01 (Claim 3, 8/10), generalist_02 (Claim 7 related)
- **Status:** The mathematical relationship is mechanical — rolling 60-day stock-bond correlation stdev of ~0.25 (vs. ~0.10 in 2012-2019) means any hedge calibrated to a specific correlation is systematically over- or under-hedged roughly half the time. generalist_01 estimates 30-40% reduction in 60/40 diversification benefit even with mildly negative average correlation. Debate pair_0 confirmed this as "analytically precise and underappreciated." Confidence limited by short empirical sample of elevated correlation volatility (~4 years since 2022).

**7. Sectoral financial balances identity constrains the sustainability of any risk-on regime — someone must be borrowing.**
- **Confidence: 6/10**
- **Originating agents:** macro_strategist_02 (Claim 2, 9/10 for identity, reduced to 7-8 in debate for application)
- **Status:** The accounting identity is irrefutable: (S-I) + (T-G) + (M-X) ≡ 0. Post-2020 deficits of 6-8% GDP provided ~$1.5-2T annually in net financial assets to the private sector, structurally supporting risk-on. Godley's track record (predicting 2001 and 2008) adds credibility. Debate pair_2 correctly distinguished the identity (9/10 confidence) from its application (7-8/10) — identifying *which* sector's deficit is unsustainable and *when* it reverses requires judgment. Confidence at 6 reflects the application, not the identity itself.

**8. Tariffs are structurally different from 2018-19 and underpriced as a slow-burn regime degrader.**
- **Confidence: 6/10**
- **Originating agents:** generalist_01 (Claim 8, 7/10), generalist_02 (Claim 5, 6/10)
- **Status:** Both generalists agree tariffs are now permanent structural policy (revenue replacement, reshoring) without an implicit reversal option, and the Fed's inflation constraint limits the ability to ease through tariff-induced weakness — fundamentally different from 2018-19 when all three conditions were absent. Debate pair_0 confirmed the direction but disputed the timeline: generalist_02's "slow burn over 12-24 months" won over generalist_01's more urgent framing. The composite analogue decomposition (price shock, investment chill, supply chain reconfiguration) is methodologically sound but loses the advantage of observing an integrated system. Confidence limited by political contingency — if tariffs are moderated, the thesis weakens.

**9. Term premium dynamics are more informative for regime identification than yield levels, but "cleanest single measure" overstates the case.**
- **Confidence: 6/10**
- **Originating agents:** rates_strategist_01 (Claims 1-3, 7-8/10)
- **Status:** The decomposition of yields into expected rates and term premium is analytically essential — the October 2023 selloff was ~70% term premium, and correctly classifying it as "fiscal dominance" rather than "reflation" has direct portfolio implications. The four-regime taxonomy (reflation, recession fear, complacency, fiscal dominance) is well-supported historically. However, debate pair_3 correctly reduced "cleanest single measure" to "useful at extremes, noisy in the middle" — r-star uncertainty (±150bp on HLW) dominates the signal in the middle range where markets spend most of their time. Model dependence (ACM vs. Kim-Wright diverge 40-80bp) further limits real-time utility.

**10. The maturity wall ($300-350B sub-IG in 2026-27) is a window of vulnerability that macro conditions either exploit or defuse.**
- **Confidence: 6/10**
- **Originating agents:** credit_analyst_01 (Claim 8, connections), credit_analyst_02 (Claim 5, 8/10)
- **Status:** Both credit analysts agree on the quantum and regime-sensitivity. But debate pair_1 refuted credit_analyst_02's stronger claim that the maturity wall "drives" transitions more than macro sentiment — the 2020-21 wall was completely defused by Fed intervention. The correct synthesis: the maturity wall is a *forcing function* that creates deterministic funding needs, but the *regime* at the time of maturity concentration determines whether those needs are met via smooth refinancing or cascading defaults. The credit_analyst_01's "dual squeeze" model (maturity wall + labor cost inflation) identifies the binding interaction.

**11. The credit impulse (second derivative of credit growth) is among the best leading indicators for regime transitions, though operationally noisy.**
- **Confidence: 6/10**
- **Originating agents:** macro_strategist_02 (Claim 4, 7/10)
- **Status:** Biggs, Mayer, and Pick (2010) formalize the mechanism: decelerating new borrowing reduces marginal asset demand, undermining Ponzi-finance units before credit *levels* decline. China's credit impulse turns preceded global risk-off episodes (2015-16, 2021-22). Debate pair_2 found macro_strategist_02 "stronger on causality" than macro_strategist_01's Taylor residual. Confidence limited to 6 because Agent B itself acknowledges measurement challenges: shadow banking opacity, data lags, and inconsistent lead times across cycles.

## LOW_CONFIDENCE

**1. Cascade threshold quantification (VIX >25, MOVE >130, dealer position <-$30B → 35-40% cascade probability).**
- **Confidence: 3/10**
- **Originating agent:** generalist_01 (Claim 5, self-rated 5/10)
- **Status:** Three events in 24 quarters is not a calibrated probability. The 95% binomial confidence interval spans ~10-70%. The three cited events had fundamentally different origins (pandemic, UK pension crisis, JPY carry unwind). The monitoring framework is useful as a dashboard; the probability estimates should be discarded. Debate pair_0 confirmed: "the numbers cannot bear the weight placed on them."

**2. Rating agency procyclicality as a distinct regime-amplification channel.**
- **Confidence: 5/10**
- **Originating agent:** credit_analyst_02 (Claim 7, 9/10)
- **Status:** Well-documented across GFC, 2015-16 energy downturn, and COVID. The mechanism (validation of aggressive structures via new-issue ratings → lagged mass downgrades → forced selling from CLOs at 7.5% CCC-bucket limits) is structural to the ratings business model. Single-agent origination but mechanistically sound. Needs corroboration from outside the credit specialist lens to reach moderate confidence.

**3. Foreign official sector demand decline structurally raises the equilibrium term premium baseline.**
- **Confidence: 5/10**
- **Originating agent:** rates_strategist_01 (Claim 6, 8/10)
- **Status:** TIC data trend is clear: foreign official holdings declining from ~33% to ~22% of marketable Treasuries while debt has doubled. Warnock and Warnock (2009) estimated 80-100bp yield suppression from foreign official purchases. But that elasticity estimate is 15+ years old and the buyer base has shifted. Single-agent origination. The implication — that today's "risk-on" features higher term premium than the 2010s' "risk-off" — reframes all historical threshold comparisons and warrants systematic validation.

**4. Phillips Curve steepening will structurally shorten risk-on regimes going forward.**
- **Confidence: 5/10**
- **Originating agent:** macro_strategist_01 (Claim 6, 7/10)
- **Status:** Debate pair_2 confirmed this as "genuinely novel and underappreciated." If the post-pandemic Phillips Curve has steepened (Benigno-Eggertsson 2023), inflation forces earlier tightening at positive output gaps, compressing the risk-on window. However, only ~4 years of post-pandemic data exists and no other agent engages with this dimension.

**5. The "two-leg instability pattern" places us in the false stability phase, 35-50% through a 9-18 month instability window.**
- **Confidence: 5/10**
- **Originating agent:** generalist_02 (Claim 7, 7/10)
- **Status:** The pattern (4 of 5 instability episodes featured initial shock → partial recovery → second decisive leg) is compelling. Debate pair_0 confirmed this framing as more parsimonious than generalist_01's "fragile risk-on as 4th regime state." But the "35-50% through" positioning is inherently dependent on when you date the instability onset, and the signposts for the second leg (credit event, data surprise, or policy mistake) are qualitative.

**6. Insurance company allocations to private credit are a poorly understood regime stability variable.**
- **Confidence: 4/10**
- **Originating agent:** credit_analyst_02 (Open Question 5)
- **Status:** Life insurers have dramatically increased private credit allocations via affiliated platforms. If regulators increase capital charges or insurers face policyholder outflows, this removes a supposedly stable capital base. Flagged as an open question, not a developed claim.

**7. EBITDA addbacks distort "real" leverage by 1-2x, meaning reported leverage of 5.5x may be 7.5x economic leverage.**
- **Confidence: 5/10**
- **Originating agent:** credit_analyst_02 (Claim 1 detail, Open Question 2)
- **Status:** The directional claim that addbacks averaging 25-40% of reported EBITDA inflate coverage ratios is well-sourced from LCD/PitchBook data. The implication for loss-given-default calculations is significant. But the precise magnitude is portfolio-specific and not empirically validated at the aggregate level.

## REFUTED

**1. "Cascade probability of 35-40% conditional on specific thresholds" (generalist_01, Claim 5)**
- **Reasoning:** Derived from n=3 events in 24 quarters. 95% binomial CI spans ~10-70%. The three events had fundamentally different origins. Debate pair_0: "the numbers cannot bear the weight placed on them." The monitoring framework survives; the probability calibration is discarded.

**2. "RORO is an intellectually bankrupt oversimplification" (challenger_01, Claim 1)**
- **Reasoning:** RORO described market behavior for 15 years (2008-2023). A framework with a specific domain of validity that is narrowing is not "bankrupt." Debate pair_3 confirmed the decorrelation thesis is directionally correct but the framing is "rhetorical overreach." The underlying claim — that cross-asset correlation structures are becoming less unitary as QE recedes — survives in moderate form.

**3. Model portfolio with specific percentage weights and scenario return estimates (generalist_01, Claim 7)**
- **Reasoning:** Debate pair_0 found the underlying regime identification (confidence 6-7/10) cannot support the implied precision of a detailed allocation table with specific weights (35% equities, 15% commodities, 8% gold, etc.) and return forecasts (+6-8%, -3-5%, etc.). The directional recommendations (underweight duration, overweight real assets and convexity, hold FX tail hedges) survive; the specific numbers are "noise dressed as signal."

**4. HY-IG quality spread as "more reliable regime classifier than any single-asset volatility metric" (credit_analyst_01, Claim 2)**
- **Reasoning:** Debate pair_1 found this is a *coincident* indicator, not a classifier with demonstrated edge over alternatives. The VIX spiked in every cited risk-off episode, often *before* the quality spread widened. The quality spread is *a* useful indicator; the superlative claim is unsupported.

**5. "Velocity of risk-off transitions in credit has roughly doubled" (credit_analyst_01, Claim 6)**
- **Reasoning:** Debate pair_1: stated without methodology for measuring velocity, definition of risk-off transition, or baseline. March 2020 was a pandemic shock (poor basis for structural inference), 2018 Q4 was orderly, 2022 was gradual. The underlying structural argument (dealer capacity down, ETF mismatch up) is directionally sound; the "roughly doubled" quantification is unsupported.

**6. Maturity wall "drives" transitions more than macro sentiment (credit_analyst_02, Claim 5)**
- **Reasoning:** The 2020-21 maturity wall was completely defused by Fed intervention. Amend-and-extend activity can push walls forward 2-3 years, making the calendar itself endogenous to risk appetite. Debate pair_1: "The wall is a vulnerability that macro conditions exploit, not an independent regime driver." The claim that the wall is a deterministic forcing function survives; the claim that it drives transitions more than sentiment is refuted.

**7. Taylor residual as "the most internally consistent" regime identifier (macro_strategist_01, Claim 2)**
- **Reasoning:** Debate pair_2 found three fatal issues: (a) requires specifying r-star, π*, and output gap weights — all estimated with substantial error; (b) undefined during unconventional policy periods (2008-2022) when regime identification matters most; (c) the 2003-2007 period, when risk appetite accelerated despite a roughly neutral Taylor residual, contradicts the claimed primacy.

**8. "Every comparable episode since 1990" resolves toward higher volatility (generalist_01, Claim 2)**
- **Reasoning:** The >70% precision and "every comparable episode" framing don't survive scrutiny. The 2016-17 counter-example was dismissed because it featured central bank liquidity provision — but current conditions feature 6% fiscal deficits and an AI productivity narrative that could play an analogous role. Debate pair_0: "The honest base rate is more like 4-5 out of 6 (67-83%)." The direction survives; the precision and comprehensiveness claims are refuted.

## KEY_DISAGREEMENTS

**1. What is the primary driver of regime transitions — monetary policy stance or endogenous credit dynamics?**
- **Agents:** macro_strategist_01 (policy rate vs. r-star) vs. macro_strategist_02 (credit impulse / Minsky cycle)
- **Debate verdict:** macro_strategist_02 stronger on causality (subprime cycle ran despite neutral Taylor residual); macro_strategist_01 stronger on operationalization. Neither metric alone is sufficient. The honest synthesis uses credit impulse for structural assessment and policy stance for duration/response calibration.
- **Future research:** Can a combined indicator (Taylor residual × credit impulse) outperform either alone? Test on 1997-2025 sample.

**2. Can risk appetite regimes be reliably identified in real time?**
- **Agents:** rates_strategist_01 (yes, via term premium decomposition + repo indicators) vs. challenger_01 (skeptical, Open Question 1) vs. generalist_02 (signposts are qualitative)
- **Status:** rates_strategist_01 provides the most specific real-time toolkit but buries a critical caveat ("are decompositions only useful in hindsight?"). Model dependence (ACM vs. Kim-Wright diverge 40-80bp) limits precision. The debate pair_3 verdict: "Agent A overstates real-time identifiability; Agent B is more honest."
- **Future research:** Retrospective vintage analysis — compare real-time ACM/Kim-Wright estimates against final revised estimates during the 2020-2025 period.

**3. Is risk appetite unitary or multi-factor?**
- **Agents:** rates_strategist_01 (unitary but with 4 regime states) vs. challenger_01 (multi-factor, asset-class-specific)
- **Debate verdict:** challenger_01 stronger on theoretical critique (SVB: credit tight while equity vol spiked), rates_strategist_01 stronger on practical framework. "A practitioner can use A's taxonomy; B's critique leaves you with nothing to trade on."
- **Future research:** Factor model decomposition of cross-asset risk appetite — can 2-3 factors explain the 2022-2025 decorrelation episodes?

**4. How will private credit behave in a sustained downturn — gradual recognition or violent surfacing?**
- **Agents:** credit_analyst_01 and credit_analyst_02 both predict eventual stress surfacing, but debate pair_1 found "violent surfacing" assumed, not demonstrated. The 2022-23 rate hiking cycle (the closest test) saw private credit NAVs barely move — interpretable as mark-to-model suppression OR genuine resilience from floating-rate structures and workout capacity.
- **Future research:** When the first major cohort of 2021-vintage private credit loans hits maturity (2026-2028), the outcome will resolve this debate empirically.

**5. Tariff impact timeline: near-term stagflationary impulse or slow-burn 12-24 month degrader?**
- **Agents:** generalist_01 (nearer-term urgency) vs. generalist_02 (12-24 month accumulation)
- **Debate verdict:** generalist_02's decomposition is more methodologically rigorous, but interaction effects between components could compress the timeline. The 1971-73 analogue suggests 6-12 months for price-level effects plus 12-18 months for second-round effects.
- **Future research:** Track real-time margin compression in tariff-exposed sectors (manufacturing, industrials, consumer goods) as the discriminant.

**6. Is the current "false stability" phase 35-50% through an instability window, or could the instability resolve without a second leg?**
- **Agents:** generalist_02 (two-leg pattern in 4/5 analogues) vs. the historical precedent that 2/5 times a sufficiently aggressive policy response prevented the second leg (1998, 2018-19)
- **Status:** Whether the second leg materializes depends on the "conditional credibility" question — can the Fed deliver a growth-supportive pivot without reigniting inflation? This is the single most consequential forward-looking question for regime determination.
