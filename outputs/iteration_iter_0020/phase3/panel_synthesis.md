## HIGH_CONFIDENCE

### 1. The correlation sign is determined by the dominant shock type (demand vs. supply), mediated by the central bank reaction function.
**Confidence: 9/10** | **Agents:** macro_strategist_01 (9/10), rates_strategist_02 (8/10), generalist_01, generalist_02 (8/10), equity_analyst_02, fx_strategist_01, challenger_01, risk_analyst_02

All eight agents anchor on the Campbell-Pflueger-Viceira (2020) framework: demand shocks produce negative stock-bond correlation (CB cuts rates → bonds rally as equities fall); supply shocks produce positive correlation (CB tightens into weakening growth → both fall). generalist_02 provides the most systematic evidence: across 13 documented post-1960 transitions, 6/7 flips to negative were triggered by growth shocks, 5/6 flips to positive by inflation shocks (median lead time 2-4 months). pair_0 and pair_2 confirmed this as the highest-confidence theoretical foundation. The current ambiguous shock mix (residual supply-side inflation persistence + demand softening) is precisely why the correlation regime remains unresolved. No agent contested the underlying mechanism; all debate concerns which shock type will dominate going forward.

### 2. The current correlation regime is transitional and unstable — not settled into persistent positive territory.
**Confidence: 9/10** | **Agents:** All eight agents converge

This is the single strongest convergence in the analysis, achieved through eight independent analytical lenses. generalist_01 (9/10): rolling 1-year correlation oscillating between -0.2 and +0.4 since 2022, "transitional and asymmetrically dangerous." generalist_02 (7/10): maps to "fiscal stalemate transitions" lasting 5-7 years. macro_strategist_01 (6/10): 60/40 demand-dominant vs. supply-dominant probability split. rates_strategist_02 (7/10): two of three conjunction conditions met/approaching. fx_strategist_01 (6/10): dollar hasn't broken below DXY 100, preventing correlation lock-in. equity_analyst_02 (7/10): index-level valuations price two contradictory macro states simultaneously. risk_analyst_02 (7/10): geopolitical drivers structural but acknowledges transitional state. challenger_01 (8/10): critiques "oscillating" as unfalsifiable consensus but does not dispute the empirical instability. pair_0 confirmed across market-data and historical-analogue frameworks. pair_2 confirmed across NK-model and transmission-mechanism frameworks. The regime has oscillated for 4 years (Q1 2022-Q1 2026) — at the upper end of monetary-policy-driven transitions (12-36 months) but within the range of fiscal stalemate transitions (5-7 years per generalist_02's three-episode analogue base).

### 3. The maturity-dependent correlation bifurcation (2Y-SPX negative, 30Y-SPX positive) is real, structurally meaningful, and reflects partial fiscal dominance.
**Confidence: 8/10** | **Agents:** generalist_01 (8/10), macro_strategist_01 (7/10), rates_strategist_02 (8/10), fx_strategist_01, generalist_02, equity_analyst_02

Six agents independently document and interpret this pattern. The convergent explanation spans three vocabularies describing the same mechanism: **expectations vs. term premium** (generalist_01: 2Y ≈ expected policy rate, 30Y ≈ expected rate + term premium); **monetary credibility vs. fiscal reality** (rates_strategist_02: front end transmits policy intent, back end transmits fiscal supply); **partial fiscal dominance** (macro_strategist_01: Bianchi-Melosi fiscal theory extension of NK model). The falsifiable prediction — 2Y-SPX turning positive signals full fiscal dominance — is endorsed by three agents. challenger_01 raises a legitimate concern (Claim 4, 6/10) that the bifurcation may be over-fitted to a 3-5 year sample with only 4 historical precedents, and that +0.05 to +0.15 at the long end is statistically indistinguishable from zero. This critique tempers confidence from 9 to 8 but does not refute the pattern's existence. pair_0, pair_1, and pair_2 all confirmed. The pattern has survived three iterations of scrutiny.

### 4. Fiscal dynamics are a co-equal determinant of the correlation regime alongside monetary policy.
**Confidence: 8/10** | **Agents:** All eight agents converge through different channels

The strongest cross-methodological convergence after Claim 2. macro_strategist_01: fiscal supply dominates 30Y term premium. rates_strategist_02: term premium at 50-80bp (ACM) driven by $2T+ annual issuance; adjusted estimate 80-130bp. equity_analyst_02: Kalecki-Levy identity channels ~$400-600B of deficit spending into corporate profits while simultaneously pushing term premium higher. generalist_02: fiscal deficits 4.5-7.0% GDP define the "corridor of instability" preventing regime resolution. risk_analyst_02: defense spending + industrial policy (CHIPS/IRA) are politically irreversible fiscal commitments. fx_strategist_01: petrodollar erosion removes price-insensitive Treasury buyers, amplifying fiscal supply pressure. challenger_01: fiscal dominance makes the CB reaction function endogenous, collapsing the Taylor Rule framework. generalist_01: the fiscal trajectory ($2T+/year with no consolidation path) is the structural difference from prior episodes that reverted to negative correlation. pair_3 confirmed fiscal dominance as a point of agreement between the geopolitical and contrarian perspectives. The US at ~6.5% deficit/GDP with ~125% debt/GDP and no credible consolidation mechanism is the defining structural feature of this correlation episode.

### 5. Inflation expectations anchoring is the critical state variable determining which correlation regime can prevail.
**Confidence: 8/10** | **Agents:** generalist_01 (8/10), macro_strategist_01 (8/10), rates_strategist_02 (7/10), generalist_02 (6/10)

Four agents identify inflation expectations as the boundary variable. 5Y breakevens at 2.3-2.5% sit at the upper bound of the historical anchored band (1.8-2.5%). Michigan 5Y-10Y expectations at 3.0-3.2% are elevated but below the Coibion-Gorodnichenko de-anchoring threshold (sustained >75-100bp divergence for >6 months). macro_strategist_01 formalizes the connection: anchored expectations → flat Phillips Curve → Fed can look through supply shocks → negative correlation. De-anchored expectations → steep Phillips Curve → Fed must tighten into weakness → positive correlation. rates_strategist_02 adds the asymmetry: the buffer is thinner than at any point since the late 1990s. generalist_02 maps this into the two-dimensional corridor (inflation × fiscal deficit) that produces structural instability. The margin of safety is narrow: a tariff-driven goods inflation shock sustained above 3.5% for 2-3 quarters could breach the threshold. pair_2 confirmed this as a shared finding.

### 6. The cross-asset pricing inconsistency — equities price negative correlation while rates/term premium price positive — is real and must eventually resolve.
**Confidence: 8/10** | **Agents:** generalist_01 (8/10), generalist_02, equity_analyst_02 (7/10), rates_strategist_02

S&P 500 at 21-22x forward P/E requires ERP of ~4.0-4.5%, justifiable only if bonds provide portfolio insurance (negative correlation). ACM term premium at +50-80bp prices duration risk inconsistent with bonds-as-insurance. IG credit spreads at ~90-110bp (near post-GFC tights) price near-zero probability of a regime where the Fed cannot aggressively ease. Gold at all-time highs prices inflation/monetary regime uncertainty. equity_analyst_02 deepens this: the index-level ERP of ~4.5-5.0% is an averaging artifact — mega-cap tech implies ERP of 2.0-3.0% (pricing permanent negative correlation) while the S&P 493 implies 5.0-6.5% (pricing positive correlation). The inconsistency is mechanically demonstrable. Resolution direction is uncertain but the inconsistency itself is high-confidence. pair_0 confirmed. Historical base rate: equity valuations lag correlation regime shifts by 18-36 months (generalist_02), possibly extended to 36-60 months by ~50% passive AUM (though this extension estimate is speculative).

### 7. ROIC-WACC spread compression creates nonlinear, convex equity sensitivity to rate changes that amplifies positive correlation at the fundamental level.
**Confidence: 9/10** | **Agents:** equity_analyst_02 (9/10), generalist_01 (connections)

This is a mathematical identity, not an empirical claim: EVA = Invested Capital × (ROIC - WACC). At a 7pp spread, 100bp WACC increase destroys ~14% of EVA. At a 4pp spread (current), the same increase destroys ~25% — a 1.8x amplification. S&P 500 ROIC has declined from cyclical peaks of ~14-15% (2021) to ~11-12% (2025E) while WACC has risen from ~6-7% to ~8-9%. The current ~3.5-5.0pp spread is the tightest since 2007-08. This creates a regime where rate increases damage earnings through the fundamental channel (not just multiple compression) — approximately 1.5-2.0x more powerfully than in any prior positive correlation episode, where ROIC-WACC spreads were 8-12pp. pair_1 called this "genuinely novel and indisputable." The only empirical uncertainty is the precise current spread estimate (±50bp). The structural implication — that consensus models treating the rate-to-equity channel as primarily a multiple effect systematically underestimate equity vulnerability — survives all debate.

### 8. Growth shocks flip correlation negative; inflation shocks flip it positive — with identifiable signpost sequencing.
**Confidence: 8/10** | **Agents:** generalist_02 (8/10), generalist_01, macro_strategist_01

generalist_02 provides the systematic evidence across 13 transitions with a formal signpost hierarchy ranked by lead time and reliability: (1) IG credit spreads widening >75bp from trough (18-month lead, 6/7 hit rate), (2) ISM manufacturing <47 for 2+ consecutive months (12-month lead, 5/6), (3) yield curve un-inversion (6-12 months, 4/5), (4) initial claims >300K (3-6 months, 7/8), (5) Fed cumulative cuts >100bp (1-3 months, ~100%). Current status (March 2026): only Tier 2b (yield curve un-inversion) is active. pair_0 called this "the most operationally actionable output across both analyses." The small sample (7 transitions) limits statistical power but the sequencing logic is grounded in the transmission mechanism: credit markets reprice fastest (institutional), then manufacturing confirms (real economy), then labor market confirms (lagged), then the Fed responds (institutional). The hit rates should be treated as approximate, not precise probabilities.

## MODERATE_CONFIDENCE

### 9. The corporate debt maturity wall ($2.5T, 2025-2027) is a concrete, time-stamped positive correlation amplifier concentrated in the S&P 493.
**Confidence: 7/10** | **Agents:** equity_analyst_02 (8/10), generalist_01

Well-sourced from rating agency data. Weighted average coupon of ~2.5-3.5% (ZIRP-era issuance) refinancing at 5.5-6.5% implies ~$50-75B incremental annual interest expense, translating to approximately $2.50-3.75 S&P 500 EPS headwind (~1.0-1.5% of earnings). Critically, this impact is not evenly distributed: top-10 firms are net cash (interest expense/EBIT ~1-5%); the S&P 493 carries the full burden (interest expense/EBIT rising from ~12% in 2021 to ~18% in 2025E, tracking toward 22-25% by 2027). This creates a measurable quality divergence that reinforces positive correlation through the earnings channel. Confidence limited by private credit substitution uncertainty ($1.5T+ AUM may absorb a material portion) and potential rate cuts reducing the refinancing cost.

### 10. The effective Taylor Rule coefficient (φ_π_eff) has diverged from the stated coefficient, placing the Fed in the "transition zone" (1.0-1.5).
**Confidence: 7/10** | **Agents:** rates_strategist_02 (8/10), challenger_01 (7/10)

rates_strategist_02 provides the backward estimation: the Fed paused at 5.25-5.50% with core PCE at 4.3%, then cut when core PCE was still 2.7%, revealing φ_π_eff of ~1.0-1.2. This contrasts with the textbook 1.5 (Taylor 1993) or 2.0 (Taylor 1999). pair_2 judged this stronger than macro_strategist_01's textbook calibration: "Agent A plugs in standard calibrations without interrogating whether the Fed's actual behavior matches those calibrations." challenger_01 arrives at the same conclusion via a different route: fiscal dominance makes the reaction function endogenous — the Fed cannot credibly commit to φ_π > 1.5 when rate hikes blow up the deficit via interest expense on $35T+ debt. The implication: at φ_π_eff of 1.0-1.2, the Taylor Principle is barely satisfied, and the correlation sign is more shock-dependent than models assuming a credible, aggressive central bank would suggest. The specific coefficient estimate (±0.2 uncertainty) is inherently imprecise.

### 11. Portfolio risk from correlation instability is ~40-50% higher than naive constant-correlation models capture.
**Confidence: 7/10** | **Agents:** generalist_01 (8/10)

The mechanical calculation is precise and uncontested: moving ρ from -0.30 to +0.30 raises 60/40 portfolio volatility from ~8.4% to ~12.3% (46% increase). pair_0 challenged the framing: sophisticated institutional risk models (MSCI Barra, Axioma) do incorporate regime-switching and stress testing — the claim that "standard risk models" miss this is a straw man. The valid core: vol-targeting ($500B-$1T AUM) and risk parity ($200-400B AUM) strategies assume locally stable correlations by construction and mechanically amplify correlation transitions through forced deleveraging. The Treasury basis trade ($800B-$1T+ at 50-100x leverage) adds acute fragility. The 46% figure applies to naive models and systematic strategies, not to all institutional risk management.

### 12. The monetary policy transmission mechanism is partially broken — the asset price channel is actively counterproductive.
**Confidence: 7/10** | **Agents:** rates_strategist_02 (8/10)

rates_strategist_02's channel-by-channel diagnostic: interest rate channel at ~60% effectiveness (mortgage lock-in effect); credit channel at ~80% (working); asset price channel at negative effectiveness (AI rally added $10-15T market cap, wealth effects of $300-750B exceeding rate tightening impulse); exchange rate channel at ~50% (goods only 25% of core PCE); expectations channel at ~50-70% (forward guidance attenuated). pair_2 called this "the single most policy-relevant insight" and judged rates_strategist_02 stronger than macro_strategist_01 because the textbook NK model "assumes transmission works as specified." The FCI loosening paradox (Goldman FCI eased ~1.0 point despite restrictive rates) corroborates the diagnosis. This transmission failure explains why the correlation regime hasn't resolved despite the shock mix evolving — and why financial conditions loosened through 2023-2025 despite the most aggressive hiking cycle since Volcker. The specific 75-150bp FCI miscalibration estimate (rates_strategist_02, Claim 3) was partially challenged in pair_2 (direction right, magnitude uncertain).

### 13. Soft landings have never independently resolved a correlation regime from positive/unstable to durably negative — some form of growth shock or financial stress has always been required.
**Confidence: 7/10** | **Agents:** generalist_02 (8/10)

generalist_02 examined five episodes and found 0/5 cases of soft-landing resolution. pair_0 correctly refuted the "exactly 0%" framing (statistically meaningless from n=5; 95% CI includes rates up to ~45%), but validated the directional finding as "genuine empirical" and "valuable." The market consensus appears to assign ~40-50% probability to exactly the "no-recession, gradual-disinflation, negative-correlation-reassertion" scenario. The analogue base rate for this outcome is zero, though the current environment may be structurally novel (50% passive AUM, AI productivity). The directional prior should update: some validating crisis is likely required, though "crisis" spans a range from a mild recession to severe financial stress.

### 14. The Kalecki-Levy fiscal earnings paradox: fiscal deficits simultaneously support near-term earnings AND compress equity multiples through term premium.
**Confidence: 6/10** | **Agents:** equity_analyst_02 (7/10)

An accounting identity: at a $2T+ deficit (~6-7% GDP), ~$400-600B flows annually into corporate profit pools (after savings offset and net export leakage), representing ~25-35% of total S&P 500 net income. The paradox: the same fiscal expansion sustaining "resilient" S&P 500 EPS ($230-260 range) is the primary driver of positive long-end correlation via term premium from Treasury supply. Equity analysts seeing "resilient earnings" as bullish ignore that the earnings source is simultaneously compressing the multiple. The reverse scenario (deficit narrowing from 6.5% to 3.5% of GDP) would mechanically reduce EPS by ~$15-25 (~6-10%) but likely restore negative correlation, supporting multiple expansion of 2-4x — a net positive for total returns. pair_1 recognized this as "genuinely valuable." Confidence limited by the several estimated parameters (savings rate offset, corporate profit share) with ±30% uncertainty.

### 15. SBC exclusion from non-GAAP earnings systematically understates equity market effective duration by ~25-35%.
**Confidence: 6/10** | **Agents:** equity_analyst_02 (7/10)

S&P 500 aggregate SBC of ~$180-200B annually represents a real economic cost concentrated in the terminal value component of equity DCFs. SBC's duration properties (3-5 year vesting, convex payoff for options, value scales with multiples) mean excluding it from reported earnings implicitly models these costs as zero-duration. In 2022, companies with above-median SBC/revenue ratios underperformed by ~22pp, while the non-GAAP "earnings" impact was negligible. pair_1 called this "the most original contribution across both analyses" — "theoretically airtight." The 25-35% duration understatement is equity_analyst_02's construction with no independent academic estimate; direction is near-certain, magnitude is estimated. This has direct implications for cross-asset risk models calibrated to non-GAAP earnings.

### 16. The "corridor of instability" — core inflation 2.0-3.5% combined with fiscal deficits 4.5-7.0% GDP — produces structurally unstable correlation.
**Confidence: 6/10** | **Agents:** generalist_02 (6/10)

Analytically derived from 4 G10 episodes occupying this parameter space simultaneously. Below the corridor on either variable, one force dominates and correlation resolves. Above on inflation (>4% sustained), positive correlation locks in. Below on deficits (<4% declining), negative correlation reasserts. Inside the corridor, inflation is moderate enough for CB credibility (short-end correlation remains negative) but fiscal deficits are large enough for term premium pressure (long-end positive). The US occupies this corridor (core PCE ~2.7%, deficit ~6.5% GDP). pair_0 judged this "more operationally useful" than unidimensional inflation focus. The parameter boundaries carry ±1pp uncertainty and derive from a small sample. The concept is more robust than the specific boundaries.

### 17. Fiscal stalemate transitions — where fiscal expansion conflicts with monetary tightening — last 5-7 years, roughly 2-3x longer than clean monetary-policy-driven transitions.
**Confidence: 6/10** | **Agents:** generalist_02 (7/10)

Three documented analogues: US 1966-71 (5 years), UK 1972-77 (5 years), Italy 2008-14 (6 years). In all three, the delayed resolution reflected a structural standoff between fiscal expansion and monetary tightening — precisely today's configuration. pair_0 judged this stronger than generalist_01's 12-36 month window: "Agent A's failure to distinguish between transition types is a meaningful analytical gap." The implication: today's 4-year transition (2022-2026) is within range, suggesting 1-3 more years absent a crystallizing event. Confidence limited by n=3 and varying institutional contexts across episodes.

### 18. USD/JPY carry trade positioning ($500B-$1T) is a regime-change accelerator that converts gradual correlation shifts into abrupt flips.

### 19. A Taiwan contingency would produce a correlation spike to +0.6-0.8 with no Treasury safe-haven rally — the opposite of standard crisis models.

## REFUTED

### R1. "60/40 returns refute the regime-shift narrative." (challenger_01, Claim 3, self-rated 9/10)
**Refuted to 4/10.** The data is correct: 60/40 returned ~+34% cumulative over 2.25 years (2023-Q1 2025), above long-run averages. But pair_3 identified a textbook error: positive correlation matters for *drawdown protection*, not average returns. The 2023-2025 period was a bull market in equities where no hedging mechanism was tested. The self-rated 9/10 is the weakest confidence assignment in the entire analysis — the data is real but the inference is wrong. Positive correlation's danger is conditional on stress periods; average returns during a benign period say nothing about the hedging mechanism. The insight that "correlation sign ≠ return sign" survives as a valid reminder; the claim that this "refutes" the regime-shift narrative does not.

### R2. "The historical base rate for soft-landing correlation reassertion is exactly 0%." (generalist_02, Claim 8 framing)
**Refuted as stated; directional finding survives at 7/10 (see Claim 13).** "Exactly 0%" from n=5 is statistically meaningless — Laplace correction gives ~14%, and the 95% CI extends to ~45%. More fundamentally, generalist_02's own Claim 5 argues the current environment is structurally unprecedented (50% passive AUM), which is internally inconsistent with claiming historical base rates are definitive. The directional finding — that no soft landing has independently transitioned correlation to negative without a crisis — is a genuine empirical contribution. The "0%" framing is rhetorical overreach.

### R3. "The Taylor Rule framework has been falsified by the 2022-2025 period." (challenger_01, Claim 7, self-rated 7/10)
**Refuted to 5/10.** pair_3 correctly identifies that the 2022 outcome is consistent with the Campbell-Shiller-Viceira framework once the shock is correctly classified as supply-side. The Taylor Rule predicts correlation based on the reaction function *conditional on shock type* — a hawkish Fed facing a supply shock is a different test case than a hawkish Fed facing a demand shock. Calling correct shock classification an "epicycle" is a mischaracterization. challenger_01's stronger argument — that fiscal dominance makes the reaction function endogenous — is a *modification* of the framework, not a falsification. The modified framework (with endogenous φ_π) retains explanatory power.

### R4. "Dollar Smile zones map directly onto maturity-bifurcated correlation." (fx_strategist_01, Claim 6, self-rated 5/10)
**Refuted to 3/10.** pair_1: the Dollar Smile framework is unfalsifiable by construction (three zones exhaust all possible macro states). Mapping a second framework onto an unfalsifiable framework does not produce falsifiable predictions. The R-squared figures cited (8-12%) are too low to support the implied precision of a regime-conditional hedging strategy. The maturity bifurcation is real and well-documented; the Dollar Smile mapping adds rhetorical structure but no predictive content.

### R5. "Election cycle clustering 2026-2027 represents unusually dense correlation-positive impulse." (risk_analyst_02, Claim 4, self-rated 6/10)
**Refuted to 4/10.** pair_3: elections are always clustering somewhere across the US, Europe, and Japan — no 18-month window lacks major elections in at least two of these regions. No baseline for typical election density is provided. The claim is also internally inconsistent with risk_analyst_02's own argument that fiscal commitments are politically irreversible and structurally driven regardless of election timing.

### R6. Specific magnitude claims that survived directionally but were refuted in precision:

## KEY_DISAGREEMENTS

### D1. How long will the transitional regime persist?
- **generalist_01:** 12-36 month historical window, implying we're already at the long end.
- **generalist_02:** 5-7 years for fiscal stalemate transitions, suggesting 1-3 more years.
- **Status:** generalist_02's analogue class was judged more appropriate for the current fiscal-monetary conflict. But n=3 is a thin base, and the current fiscal position (6-7% deficit, ~125% debt/GDP) exceeds all three precedents, making the duration extrapolation uncertain.
- **Priority: Medium.** Monitor via signpost hierarchy (Claim 8). Resolution requires either a crystallizing event or parametric movement outside the "corridor of instability."

### D2. Is the equity market's negative-correlation assumption a mispricing or an informed forward-looking view?
- **generalist_01, equity_analyst_02:** Mispricing — equities are overvalued by 2-4 turns.
- **Implicit market view:** The Fed will ultimately succeed, inflation will normalize, and negative correlation will reassert — making current valuations rational.
- **challenger_01:** Passive flows sustain valuations regardless, and AI disinflation may vindicate the equity market's bet.
- **Status:** Unresolved. The cross-asset inconsistency is demonstrable, but the equity market has been "wrong" for 4 years and still outperformed most bearish positioning.
- **Priority: High.** The most consequential unresolved question for portfolio construction. Equal-weight vs. cap-weight correlation data would partially discriminate.

### D3. Does equity market concentration create a "correlation illusion" at the index level?
- **challenger_01 (7/10), equity_analyst_02 (7/10):** Top 10 stocks (~35% of market cap) are long-duration, rate-sensitive. Index-level correlation may reflect mega-cap-tech-bond correlation, not economy-wide dynamics.
- **All other agents:** Use index-level correlation without adjusting for concentration.
- **Status:** pair_3 called this "the most important unresolved empirical question" and neither agent ran the test. Comparing cap-weighted vs. equal-weight SPX correlation with 10Y yields would resolve a significant portion of the debate.
- **Priority: Highest.** This is the single most testable hypothesis that would change the interpretation of the entire research stream if confirmed.

### D4. What is the binding constraint on regime resolution — micro channels or macro channels?
- **equity_analyst_02:** ROIC-WACC compression, maturity wall, and accruals deterioration are the mechanisms that force resolution at the fundamental level.
- **fx_strategist_01:** The dollar cycle is the missing third leg — without DXY breaking below 100, positive correlation cannot lock in.
- **pair_1 verdict:** These are complementary, not competing. Micro channels produce *episodic* positive correlation; the dollar channel determines whether episodes become *regimes*. Neither agent synthesizes this distinction.
- **Priority: Medium.** Both channels are running in parallel; the question is which one triggers a discrete event first.

### D5. Can a positive supply shock (AI) override fiscal dominance to restore negative correlation?
- **challenger_01 (7/10):** AI-driven disinflation could reproduce the demand-shock correlation regime within 3-5 years.
- **risk_analyst_02 (7/10):** Geopolitical fragmentation and fiscal commitments are structural, creating permanent supply-shock-dominant dynamics.
- **pair_3 verdict:** Neither fully convincing. AI disinflation is plausible but timing is deeply uncertain (Solow paradox applies). Geopolitical fiscal commitments are real but "permanent" is unfalsifiable.
- **Priority: High.** The AI-vs-geopolitics question is the decade's structural bet. Monitor 5Y5Y breakevens for leading signals.

### D6. Is the current period genuinely structurally unprecedented, limiting analogue applicability?
- **generalist_02:** Analogues are informative — fiscal stalemate transitions, Nifty Fifty bifurcation, 2001 recession template all provide actionable guidance.
- **challenger_01 (Claim 10):** The research stream has developed a confirmation bias loop; same agents reinforcing same conclusions via iterative KB convergence.
- **Multiple agents:** 50% passive AUM, AI capex cycle, $800B+ basis trade, ~125% debt/GDP — each individually unprecedented, collectively creating an environment that may fall outside any analogue's applicability.
- **Priority: Meta-level.** This is a question about the methodology itself, not about any specific claim. The appropriate response is to use analogues as priors while maintaining wide uncertainty bands.

### D7. Optimal portfolio construction under regime uncertainty.
- **generalist_01:** Regime-conditional framework requiring real-time shock identification.
- **generalist_02:** Structural reallocation (replace nominal duration with real assets + trend-following) based on 1968-82 evidence.
- **pair_0 verdict:** generalist_02's approach is more robust because it does not require solving the harder problem (real-time shock identification, which generalist_01 admits is imperfect). But generalist_02's 250-350bp backtest is inflated; realistic outperformance is 100-200bp.
- **Priority: High for practitioners.** The directional recommendation — replace 15-25% of nominal duration with TIPS, commodities, and trend-following — is supported by both the historical evidence and the theoretical framework. Optimal weights remain uncertain.
