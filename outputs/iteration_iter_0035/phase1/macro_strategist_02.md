# Credit-Equity Linkage — Post-Keynesian Macro Strategist Analysis

## Key Claims

1. **The credit-equity linkage is fundamentally a flow-of-funds phenomenon, not a price-correlation phenomenon.** The Merton structural model — which treats credit spreads as a derivative of equity value — gets the causality backwards. In a monetary production economy, endogenous credit creation by banks finances investment, which generates profits (Kalecki), which validates equity valuations. Credit drives equity through the income-expenditure circuit, not through balance-sheet arbitrage. The observed 2-6 month credit lead over equity in fundamental deteriorations reflects the causal primacy of credit creation in the income-generation process, not information asymmetry as mainstream finance would have it.

2. **The Kalecki-Lévy profit identity is the master equation governing the current credit-equity regime.** Corporate profits ≡ Investment + Government Deficit + Net Exports − Household Savings. With fiscal deficits at 6-7% of GDP, the government sector is mechanically injecting ~$1.5-1.8T annually into private sector financial balances. This channels directly to the corporate profit line, simultaneously compressing credit spreads (via robust interest coverage) and supporting equity valuations (via earnings growth). The current HY OAS of 350-420bp is not market-determined risk pricing — it is a fiscal artifact. Remove the fiscal impulse, and the Kalecki identity dictates a $20-40/share S&P 500 earnings haircut within 4-6 quarters.

3. **The system is in the late stages of a Minsky speculative-phase configuration, masked by the fiscal buffer.** Using the Kalecki-Minsky taxonomy: (Government Deficit/GDP) minus (Change in Private Debt/GDP) is currently positive, classifying this as a Type A (fiscal-supported) low-vol environment. However, beneath the surface, the borrower mix is deteriorating. EBITDA addbacks of 15-40% conceal true leverage of 7.5-8.5x versus reported ~6x. PIK toggle usage has risen from ~2% to 5-8%. Covenant-lite structures (>90% of leveraged loans) have eliminated the maintenance covenant tripwires that historically forced early restructuring. This is textbook Minsky: units are transitioning from hedge to speculative positions while aggregate indicators remain benign because the fiscal flow is propping up cash flows.

4. **The sectoral balance identity reveals the current configuration is inherently unstable along both resolution paths.** The identity requires: Private Sector Balance + Government Balance + Foreign Balance ≡ 0. Currently: Government deficit ~6-7% of GDP, Current Account deficit ~3% of GDP, implying Private Sector surplus of ~3-4% of GDP. This configuration supports credit quality. But it is fiscally contingent. Path A (fiscal consolidation >2pp GDP): Kalecki identity forces corporate profit decline, triggering the credit cycle downshift. Path B (fiscal continuation/expansion): $2T+ annual Treasury issuance drives term premium higher, eventually triggering basis trade unwind and the Treasury-as-contagion-source channel. As Wynne Godley demonstrated in his 1999 Levy Institute analysis, both resolution paths produce financial instability — the question is timing and trigger, not if.

5. **Private credit at $1.7-1.8T has created a Ponzi-finance blind spot in the sectoral balance data.** The Flow of Funds (Z.1) data, our primary diagnostic tool, increasingly fails to capture the economy's true financial fragility. Private credit operates entirely outside traditional credit impulse measurement. Its mark-to-model opacity (0.5-0.7 NAV autocorrelation) and absence from public default statistics means the Financial Accounts of the United States systematically understate private sector leverage. The traditional bank credit impulse R² has collapsed from 0.38 (1975-2007) to 0.12 (2010-2025) — not because the theoretical mechanism is wrong, but because the measured variable no longer captures the relevant credit flows. This is the most dangerous form of measurement failure: the sectoral balance identity still holds, but we cannot observe one of its critical components.

6. **The banking channel remains the decisive regime discriminant, and CRE exposure is the current loading mechanism.** Minsky's hierarchy of financial fragility is operationalized through the banking system. When bank balance sheets are impaired (1990, 2007-08), the credit-equity contagion produces median equity drawdowns of -38% over 18-30 months. When banks are uninvolved (1998, 2015-16, 2020), drawdowns are -22% over 3-6 months. Regional banks at 28-30% CRE concentration (versus 6-8% for largest banks) represent the current fragility loading, with office values down 25-40% from peak. The critical question in Minsky terms: are regional banks still hedge units (cash flows cover debt service), or have they transitioned to speculative units (requiring asset appreciation or refinancing to remain solvent)?

7. **Credit factor crowding and short-put optionality constitute a hidden Ponzi-finance structure in portfolio construction.** First principal component explaining >65% of variance across HY, leveraged loans, and CLO equity means effective dimensionality is only 1.5-2.0. Combined with credit's short-put return profile (skewness -1.2 to -1.8, excess kurtosis 4-8), portfolios holding 10-15% private credit are underestimating true volatility by 15-25%. In Minsky's framework, this is institutional-level Ponzi finance: investors are holding positions whose apparent risk-return profile depends on the continuation of the current vol regime. When the regime shifts — and the cross-asset vol divergence (MOVE 100-120, VIX 13-18) says it will — the correlation snap from 0.3-0.4 to 0.7-0.9 destroys the diversification assumption simultaneously across all credit sub-sectors.

8. **The CLO OC test mechanism is a discrete Minsky moment trigger embedded in market microstructure.** The 7.5% CCC-bucket threshold in CLO overcollateralization tests is a step function that converts smooth credit deterioration into non-linear contagion. At breach, cash flows divert from equity to senior tranches, destroying CLO equity returns, forcing leveraged loan selling, and shutting down new CLO formation — removing ~65-70% of leveraged loan demand. This is structurally analogous to the margin spiral in Minsky's original framework: a mechanical rule (OC test) forces pro-cyclical selling that validates the very deterioration it was designed to protect against. Current CCC-bucket utilization of 4-6.5% is one downgrade wave from triggering the cascade.

9. **The higher pain threshold from the Kalecki fiscal buffer is itself destabilizing — this is the Minsky paradox in real time.** The estimated 100-150bp elevation of the HY spread threshold at which stress becomes self-reinforcing means more credit damage accumulates beneath the surface before equities register the problem. This is precisely Minsky's insight about stability breeding instability: by suppressing small adjustments (the forest fire analogy), the fiscal buffer allows more fragility to build, producing larger eventual adjustments. Only 4 of 11 episodes where HY crossed 500bp produced >20% equity drawdowns — but this low base rate is contaminated by the fiscal buffer's distortion of the signal.

10. **The basis trade ($800B-$1T at 50-100x leverage) has converted the Treasury market from safe haven to potential contagion source, fundamentally altering the Godley sectoral balance dynamics.** In the Godley framework, government bonds are private sector financial assets — they are the mirror image of the fiscal deficit that supports corporate profits. A basis trade unwind that forces Treasury selling creates the perverse outcome where the very instrument that embodies the private sector's financial surplus becomes a source of financial instability. This inverts the normal safe-haven dynamic and means a fiscal crisis would hit both sides of the Kalecki identity simultaneously: fiscal consolidation reduces corporate profits AND Treasury market dysfunction impairs private sector balance sheets.

## Evidence & Reasoning

**Claim 1 (Flow-of-funds causality):** The Post-Keynesian endogenous money framework (Moore 1988, Lavoie 2014) establishes that loans create deposits, and credit extension precedes and enables spending. The observed 2-6 month credit-equity lead-lag across 5 of 6 major episodes since 1990 is consistent with this causal ordering. The Merton model's 50-70% cross-sectional R² (Bharath & Shumway 2008) captures the within-regime correlation but cannot explain the regime-dependent dynamics (correlation dropping to 0.3-0.5 during early stress before snapping to 0.8-0.9). The model's systematic Type I errors of 40-60% and irrelevance for ~35% of S&P 500 by weight (net-cash mega-caps) further confirm it mistakes correlation for causation.

**Claim 2 (Kalecki identity as master equation):** The Kalecki-Lévy identity is an accounting identity — it cannot be wrong. S&P 500 EPS grew ~8-10% annualized through 2024-2025 despite restrictive monetary policy precisely because fiscal deficits at 6-7% of GDP channeled income to the corporate sector. Independent derivations by multiple analysts converge on the $20-40/share S&P 500 earnings impact from fiscal consolidation. Every 100bp of rate hikes generates ~$250-320B additional government interest payments, of which an estimated 10-25% recycle as spending — creating a reflexive loop where monetary tightening partially offsets itself through the fiscal channel. This is flow-of-funds arithmetic, confirmed by Z.1 data and earnings tracking since 2020.

**Claim 3 (Minsky speculative phase):** The Kalecki-Minsky diagnostic — (Government Deficit/GDP) minus (Change in Private Debt/GDP) — currently reads positive, placing us in Type A. But the diagnostic misses the micro-level borrower deterioration: true leverage 7.5-8.5x versus reported ~6x (ECB and Fed regulatory analyses document the 15-40% addback range), PIK toggle usage tripling, and >90% covenant-lite issuance. The historical pattern is clear from Minsky: aggregate stability masks micro-level fragility buildup until a trigger event forces recognition. The 2005-2007 period was also classified as stable by aggregate metrics while subprime borrowers were transitioning to Ponzi positions.

**Claim 4 (Sectoral balance instability):** Godley's sectoral balance framework requires the three financial balances to sum to zero. The current private sector surplus of ~3-4% of GDP is entirely dependent on the government deficit. Fiscal consolidation of 2pp GDP arithmetically reduces corporate profits by a commensurate amount. The alternative path — continued $2T+ annual Treasury issuance — has driven MOVE to 100-120 for 18+ months (longest sustained elevation since 2008-2012), with three historical analogues of this vol configuration all transitioning within 6-18 months. Godley's 1999 Levy Institute prediction correctly identified the unsustainability of the late-1990s private deficit configuration — the same framework now flags both resolution paths as destabilizing.

**Claim 5 (Private credit blind spot):** The $1.7-1.8T private credit market has grown from $200B since the GFC, representing the largest structural shift in credit intermediation since the savings and loan era. Its mark-to-model NAVs (0.5-0.7 autocorrelation) suppress measured portfolio volatility by 15-25% for institutions holding 10-15% private credit. Five analogous opaque credit structures (S&Ls, SIVs, junk bonds, Japanese jusen, AT1) showed 60-80% disorderly repricing rates with 4-5 year median lags. The credit impulse R² collapse from 0.38 to 0.12 directly tracks the shift from bank-intermediated to market/private credit. The traditional bank credit impulse still captures the SME/consumer channel (~40% of economy) but misses the leveraged corporate universe.

**Claim 6 (Banking channel as regime discriminant):** Six episodes since 1990 provide the evidence base. Banking channel active (1990, 2007-08): median -38% drawdown, 18-30 month duration. Banking channel inactive (1998, 2015-16, 2020): median -22% drawdown, 3-6 month duration. The operational real-time diagnostic: if regional bank equities (KRE) fall in sympathy with HY widening, the 1990/2007 template is activating. If HY widens but bank equity is stable, the 2015-16 contained template applies. Current loading: ~$2.7T total bank CRE exposure, regional banks at 28-30% concentration, office down 25-40%. CET1 ratios of 13-14% provide more buffer than 1990 or 2007 but do not eliminate the channel.

**Claim 7 (Crowding as Ponzi finance):** PC1 at >65% means credit sub-sectors offer illusory diversification. The BB-CCC spread ratio compressed from 3.0-3.5x to ~2.0-2.5x — the market is not discriminating between credit qualities. Implementation-adjusted credit Sharpe of 0.25-0.35 (versus backtested 0.5-0.7) means investors are being compensated roughly half of what they believe. When the correlation regime shifts from 0.3-0.4 to 0.7-0.9 — active only ~7% of calendar time but producing the tail events — the diversification assumption collapses simultaneously. CDX HY open interest growth from ~$200B to ~$900B and credit ETFs at ~$400B create reverse causality channels that amplify the snap.

**Claim 8 (CLO OC test as Minsky trigger):** The $1T CLO market's 7.5% CCC-bucket threshold is a structural analogue to the margin call in Minsky's framework. CLO formation represents ~65-70% of leveraged loan demand; shutdown of this channel converts the maturity wall ($900B-$1.1T requiring refinancing at 400bp higher coupons) from a refinancing challenge to a solvency crisis. The step-function nature means monitoring CCC-bucket utilization (currently 4-6.5%) is critical. At true leverage of 7.5-8.5x, debt service at 500bp+ OAS consumes 70-80% of actual cash EBITDA for the most leveraged borrowers.

**Claim 9 (Higher threshold paradox):** The Minsky insight — stability breeds instability — is directly applicable. The Kalecki fiscal buffer's estimated 100-150bp threshold elevation means the system tolerates wider spreads before feedback loops activate. But the tolerance period allows more leverage accumulation, more covenant erosion, more Ponzi-unit creation beneath the surface. The forest fire analogy is apt: suppressing small fires (mild credit cycles) produces larger conflagrations. The 4-of-11 false positive rate on HY>500bp producing equity drawdowns may itself be a product of this threshold elevation rather than a true base rate.

**Claim 10 (Basis trade and sectoral balance inversion):** The March 2020 Treasury dysfunction and September 2019 repo crisis partially validate this mechanism. In the Godley framework, Treasuries serve as the financial asset counterpart to fiscal deficits — they are how the private sector saves the government's deficit spending. A basis trade unwind at 50-100x leverage forces selling of these assets, simultaneously impairing private sector balance sheets AND disrupting the very market that finances the fiscal deficit. The dealer inventory decline from ~$250B to ~$30-40B means the market's shock-absorbing capacity is structurally impaired.

| 2. Kalecki identity as master equation | 9/10 | This is an accounting identity — the mechanism is undisputed. The $20-40/share estimate has wide uncertainty (50-200bp spread suppression attribution), but the direction is certain. Highest confidence because this is arithmetic, not theory. |
| 3. Minsky speculative phase | 7/10 | Aggregate diagnostic says Type A, but micro-level evidence (addbacks, PIK, covenant-lite) suggests building fragility. The tension between macro stability and micro deterioration is inherently uncertain. Minsky classification is judgment, not measurement. |
| 4. Sectoral balance instability | 8/10 | Both resolution paths producing instability follows directly from the accounting identity. The timing and trigger are uncertain, but the impossibility of the current configuration persisting indefinitely is arithmetic. Godley's 1999 prediction provides out-of-sample validation. |
| 5. Private credit blind spot | 7/10 | The measurement failure is certain (direction known). The magnitude and systemic implications are highly uncertain. The 60-80% historical disorderly repricing rate from analogues has small sample problems. The mark-to-recovery gap of 5-25% is too wide to be actionable. |
| 6. Banking channel discriminant | 8/10 | Empirically the clearest and most actionable finding. Six episodes, clean bifurcation of outcomes. Reduced from 9 because current CET1 ratios (13-14%) may genuinely alter the dynamics relative to historical episodes. |

| 9. Higher threshold paradox | 6/10 | This is the strongest theoretical claim from the Minsky framework but the hardest to test empirically. The 100-150bp threshold estimate is residual-based with wide uncertainty. The paradox is logically sound but may be unfalsifiable in practice. |

## Connections to Other Topics

**Credit Cycles:** The Minsky borrower classification (hedge → speculative → Ponzi) is the master framework. EBITDA addbacks, covenant-lite structures, and PIK toggle usage are all diagnostic of the hedge-to-speculative transition at the firm level. The maturity wall of $900B-$1.1T is the forcing function that reveals whether these borrowers are truly speculative or Ponzi. The private credit structural vulnerability ($1.7-1.8T) represents a potential Ponzi blind spot entirely outside traditional credit cycle measurement. The credit impulse structural break (R² from 0.38 to 0.12) reflects the endogenous money system's evolution from bank-centric to market/private-credit-centric intermediation.

**Equity Cycles:** The Kalecki-Lévy identity is the transmission mechanism from fiscal deficits to equity valuations. Equity cycle positioning cannot be understood independently of the sectoral balance configuration — the current equity valuation regime is fiscally contingent. The credit-equity-labor sequencing (credit → equity → labor with 3-12 month leads) provides the macro cycle timing framework. The credit-equity correlation regime dependence means equity risk models using constant correlations systematically misprice tail risk, with portfolios designed for crisis correlation suboptimal 85% of the time.

**Real Estate Cycles:** CRE is the current loading mechanism for the banking channel, the single most important regime discriminant. The CRE-to-bank-equity contagion (regional banks at 28-30% CRE concentration, office down 25-40%) operates through the three-stage balance sheet mechanism (mark-to-market → provisioning → lending pullback) that is directly from Minsky's endogenous fragility framework. The Japan model (extend-and-pretend) is the primary alternative scenario, enabled by 13-14% CET1 ratios. CRE losses interact with the Kalecki channel: fiscal support sustains aggregate demand (supporting non-office CRE), but cannot prevent the sectoral reallocation (office structural decline).

**Risk Appetite Regimes:** The Kalecki-Minsky low-vol taxonomy (Type A fiscal-supported versus Type B credit-supported) is the regime classification framework. The cross-asset vol divergence (MOVE 100-120, VIX 13-18, FX vol 7-9%) is historically anomalous and unstable — all three prior analogues transitioned within 6-18 months. Vol endogeneity (vol regimes are endogenous to macro/credit conditions, not exogenous random processes) is a core Post-Keynesian insight: Minsky's leverage mechanically suppresses realized vol until it cannot. The current low equity vol is a product of the fiscal buffer, not an independent market signal.

**Fiscal Dominance / Sovereign Debt:** The fiscal deficit is the fulcrum of the entire credit-equity linkage system. The Kalecki channel requires continued fiscal deficits to sustain corporate profits. But the same fiscal trajectory requires $2T+ annual Treasury issuance that drives term premium higher and loads the basis trade contagion channel. This is the fundamental Godley paradox: the fiscal stance that stabilizes credit markets simultaneously destabilizes the sovereign funding market. Any analysis of fiscal sustainability that ignores its role in private sector balance support (as mainstream analysis uniformly does) will systematically underestimate the costs of fiscal consolidation and overestimate the benefits.

**Monetary Policy / Fed Reaction Function:** The modified Taylor rule with credit spread term (~100bp HY widening ≈ 25-50bp FFR easing) partially endogenizes the Minsky cycle by providing an automatic stabilizer. But this Fed put is itself destabilizing in the Minsky sense: by providing insurance against credit distress, it encourages the very risk-taking that produces the fragility. If inflation blocks the Fed's credit-spread response function — the central risk scenario — the HY threshold reverts to its unadjusted level, representing the worst-case credit-equity contagion setup.

## Open Questions

1. **Can the Kalecki-Minsky diagnostic be computed with sufficient frequency and precision to serve as a real-time regime indicator?** The Z.1 data has a 2-3 month publication lag, and the private credit component is unobservable. Is a proxy-based real-time diagnostic feasible, and would it have worked historically?

2. **What is the true private sector financial balance when private credit is properly accounted for?** The $1.7-1.8T private credit market may mean the measured private sector surplus of 3-4% of GDP is overstated. If private credit has been accumulating leverage that is not reflected in Z.1 data, the sectoral balance configuration may be less comfortable than it appears.

3. **Is the current regime genuinely Type A (fiscal-supported, stable) or is it a Type A surface over a Type B (credit-supported, fragile) substrate?** The aggregate diagnostic says Type A, but the micro-level Minsky classification of borrowers suggests building fragility. This tension is the central analytical challenge.

4. **What fiscal consolidation magnitude would trigger the credit cycle downshift?** The Kalecki framework suggests >2pp GDP consolidation would be sufficient, but this depends on the private sector's marginal propensity to spend out of fiscal transfers, which varies across the income distribution. DOGE-driven spending cuts may have different Kalecki multipliers than broad-based tax increases.

5. **Has the endogenous money mechanism shifted from bank credit to private credit in ways that alter the Minsky cycle dynamics?** If private credit managers have different incentive structures than bank loan officers (less regulatory oversight, longer lock-up periods, different loss recognition timing), the Minsky cycle may operate with different lead times and severity characteristics than historical episodes.

6. **Does the basis trade create a structural Minsky moment trigger in the Treasury market that has no historical precedent?** The conversion of Treasuries from safe haven to potential contagion source fundamentally alters the Godley sectoral balance dynamics. We have no template for what happens when the government sector's liability becomes a source of private sector balance sheet stress rather than private sector wealth.

7. **Is the CLO OC test threshold of 7.5% CCC-bucket a reliable Minsky moment indicator, and what is current utilization?** The data (Intex/LCD) is not freely available. Without real-time monitoring of CCC-bucket utilization across the CLO universe, this theoretically compelling mechanism cannot be operationalized.

8. **How should the financial accelerator coefficient be recalibrated for addback-distorted leverage?** The Bernanke-Gertler-Gilchrist 1.5-2x amplification factor is calibrated to reported leverage. If true leverage is 25-40% higher than reported, the actual amplification and distance-to-default are commensurately worse. Quantifying this requires firm-level data that is difficult to obtain for private/covenant-lite borrowers.

9. **Will the synthetic credit market's reverse causality (CDX lagging SPX by 5-30 minutes) eventually compress the credit-equity lead-lag to zero, eliminating the early-warning value of credit markets?** If algorithmic trading and CDX/ETF hedge demand make credit a follower rather than leader in real time, the Post-Keynesian causal ordering (credit → spending → profits → equity validation) may still hold at fundamental frequencies but become unobservable in market prices.

10. **What is the joint probability that the fiscal buffer is withdrawn (fiscal consolidation) AND the Fed is constrained by inflation simultaneously?** This is the catastrophic Minsky moment scenario: no fiscal profit support AND no monetary backstop. Historical base rates are uninformative because we have never had 6-7% fiscal deficits and 4%+ inflation simultaneously in the post-Volcker era.
