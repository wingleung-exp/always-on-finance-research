# Credit-Equity Linkage — Cross-Asset Strategist (generalist_01) Analysis

## Key Claims

1. **Credit and equity markets are currently telling contradictory stories about default risk, and the contradiction is widening: equity-implied HY spreads (via Merton conversion) are 50-75bp wider than actual HY OAS of 350-420bp, the largest basis since 2018 Q4.** Equity vol (VIX ~13-18, but realized skew on single-stock basis elevated), combined with reported leverage of ~6x (true leverage 7.5-8.5x per addback adjustment), implies higher default probabilities than credit markets price. The direction of resolution is the most actionable question in cross-asset markets today. If credit is right (tight spreads = no recession), equity risk premium is too high and equities should re-rate upward by 5-8%. If equity is right (elevated implied vol = earnings deterioration ahead), HY should widen 100-150bp toward 500-550bp. The resolution will be determined by whether the Kalecki fiscal profit floor (6-7% deficit → corporate earnings support) is sustainable or withdrawing — making fiscal policy the arbiter of the cross-asset disagreement.

2. **The credit-equity correlation regime is currently in a deceptive "early stress decorrelation" phase (~0.3-0.5) that risk models treat as normal diversification, but which historically precedes a snap to 0.8-0.9 crisis correlation — and the transition trigger is banking channel activation, not spread levels per se.** The KB documents this regime-dependent correlation pattern across 7 episodes since 1990, with the critical insight that the banking channel (regional bank equity performance relative to HY widening) is the severity discriminant: active banking channel → -38% median equity drawdown over 18-30 months; inactive → -22% over 3-6 months. Current CRE exposure concentration (regional banks at 28-30% CRE, office down 25-40% from peak) constitutes banking channel "loading" — the mechanism is primed even if not yet activated. Portfolios assuming stable 0.3-0.4 credit-equity correlation are running 15-25% underestimated true portfolio volatility, and this underestimation is worst for the ~$1T in institutional portfolios holding 10-15% private credit (where mark-to-model NAV autocorrelation of 0.5-0.7 further suppresses measured correlation).

3. **The credit-equity linkage has structurally bifurcated into two non-communicating universes — the S&P 500 top 10 (net cash ~$500B, self-funded, credit-irrelevant) and the remaining ~4,500 public companies plus the leveraged loan/private credit universe (~$3.5T, highly credit-sensitive) — and aggregate cross-asset indicators systematically understate stress in the latter by mixing the two.** Cap-weighted HY OAS at 350-420bp, the S&P 500 at ~21-22x, and VIX at 13-18 all reflect this compositional distortion. The Merton model is mechanically irrelevant for ~35% of index weight held by net-cash mega-caps (Apple, Microsoft, Google, etc.), meaning the "credit-equity linkage" operates on a progressively shrinking share of the equity index. The cross-asset implication: the equal-weighted SPX / cap-weighted SPX ratio is a more accurate credit-equity linkage diagnostic than aggregate HY-SPX correlation. Equal-weighted underperformance signals credit stress transmitting to the median firm even when the index appears healthy.

4. **The $900B-$1.1T maturity wall (2025-2027) interacting with the CLO OC test mechanism (7.5% CCC threshold) and the basis trade ($800B-$1T at 50-100x leverage) creates a triple-trigger contagion architecture where any one channel can activate the others, but the contagion chain completes only 15-25% of the time — making this a high-expected-loss, low-probability tail risk that is systematically mispriced by both "it's fine" and "it's a crisis" camps.** The specific sequence: maturity wall forces refinancing at 300-500bp higher coupons → marginal borrowers (true leverage 7.5-8.5x) cannot service debt → downgrades push CCC-bucket toward 7.5% CLO threshold → CLO formation shutdown removes 65-70% of leveraged loan demand → spread widening toward 500-600bp → if banking channel activates (bank CDS/equity sympathy), the 2007 template is live. The arithmetic is certain (maturity wall dates, coupon step-ups, leverage ratios); the systemic cascade probability is not. Policy intervention (Fed credit-spread response function: ~100bp HY widening ≈ 25-50bp FFR easing) is the primary chain-breaker — unless inflation blocks the Fed's response, which converts a manageable credit event into a systemic one.

5. **The synthetic credit market (CDX HY at ~$900B, credit ETFs at ~$400B) has created a reverse-causality channel that contaminates the credit-leads-equity signal at high frequencies, but the multi-month fundamental lead-lag (credit leads by 2-6 months in fundamental deteriorations) remains intact — and the direction of the lead-lag is itself diagnostic of the shock type.** CDX lags SPX by 5-30 minutes during stress (intraday evidence), confirming equity-to-credit flow contamination at high frequency. But the 2007 canonical case (6-8 month credit lead), 2015-16 contained episode, and the 2000-01 credit lead all validate the fundamental signal at longer horizons. The cross-asset trade implication: when credit widens without equity falling (credit leading), the 2007/2000 fundamental deterioration template is activating; when equity falls and credit widens simultaneously or with credit lagging, it is a sentiment/flow-driven episode (2018 Q4, 2020 template) that is more likely to reverse. Currently, credit is not leading — HY OAS is near cycle tights — which argues against a fundamental deterioration view but does not rule out a flow-driven event from the basis trade or CLO channels.

6. **The Kalecki fiscal channel (6-7% deficit → corporate profits → interest coverage → tight spreads + supported P/E) is the single most important variable determining whether the credit-equity basis resolves via credit widening or equity re-rating, and it creates a paradox: the same fiscal deficits that suppress spreads today ($2T+ annual issuance) generate the term premium pressure and basis trade fragility that threaten a non-linear unwind.** The Kalecki identity mechanically channels deficits to profits (R² ~0.55-0.65 with S&P EPS since 2020). Fiscal consolidation exceeding 2pp GDP would trigger a credit cycle downshift within 4-6 quarters. But the Kalecki floor is quality-differentiated: sufficient for IG and upper HY (where aggregate demand matters most), but insufficient for lower HY/leveraged loans (where firm-level interest expense of 70-80% of true cash EBITDA overwhelms the macro demand support). This bifurcation means the Kalecki channel creates false confidence about credit quality in precisely the segment of the market most exposed to the maturity wall.

7. **Private credit ($1.7T) has become the largest unobservable variable in the credit-equity linkage framework, simultaneously degrading the informational content of public credit spreads, suppressing measured portfolio volatility through mark-to-model NAV smoothing, and creating a Godley-type sectoral balance disruption channel (flow-of-funds) that operates chronically over quarters rather than through acute price contagion.** The KB documents: NAV autocorrelation of 0.5-0.7 (vs. 0.0-0.1 for public credit), secondary market discounts of 5-12% to NAV, PIK toggle usage rising from 2% to 5-8% (a deterioration indicator that bypasses price signals), and ~$300B+ in semi-liquid vehicles facing the same UK 2022 property fund-style redemption mismatch. The cross-asset implication: public HY OAS at 350-420bp is not comparable to prior cycles because the risk that historically appeared in public spreads has partially migrated to private credit, where it is invisible until a liquidity event forces mark-to-market. The credit impulse indicator (R² declined from 0.38 to 0.12 post-GFC) is degraded in part because $1.7T of credit creation is unmeasured. Any cross-asset framework that uses HY OAS as its primary credit-equity linkage variable is working with a structurally incomplete input.

8. **The cross-asset vol divergence (MOVE ~100-120, VIX ~13-18, credit vol at historical lows) is a direct manifestation of the credit-equity linkage's current regime: rates vol prices the fiscal supply/term premium channel correctly, equity vol prices the Kalecki profit floor correctly, and credit vol prices the combined effect of both — each market is internally consistent but the cross-asset configuration is unstable and has no historical precedent for persisting.** Three historical analogues of this vol divergence (2006-07, 2014, 2017-18) all preceded major cross-asset transitions within 6-18 months. The resolution mechanism in each case was different: 2006-07 resolved via credit (subprime → systemic), 2014 resolved via commodities (oil collapse → HY energy contagion), 2017-18 resolved via equity vol (volmageddon → Q4 2018 selloff). The current configuration adds a novel element: the $800B-$1T basis trade creates a rates-to-credit-to-equity transmission channel that did not exist at comparable scale in prior episodes, meaning the resolution could originate from the rates market for the first time in the credit-equity linkage framework.

9. **The credit-equity basis (Claim 1) combined with the structural bifurcation (Claim 3) creates a specific, actionable cross-asset trade: long CDX HY protection (or OTM puts on HYG) paired with long equal-weighted SPX or Russell 2000 vs. short cap-weighted SPX captures both the basis resolution and the compositional mismatch.** If the credit-equity basis resolves via credit widening, CDX protection pays while equities decline (but the equal-weight leg underperforms less than cap-weight in a credit-driven event because credit stress disproportionately affects the median firm, not the mega-cap). If the basis resolves via equity re-rating upward, the equal-weight/Russell leg outperforms the cap-weight because the re-rating benefits the credit-sensitive majority more than the already-rich mega-caps. The trade has positive carry in one leg (CDX protection is cheap at HY OAS 350-420bp; the cost of 6-month 5% OTM HYG puts is ~1.5-2.0% of notional) and positive expected return from the compositional mean-reversion in the other.

10. **The single highest-conviction cross-asset signal is negative: the absence of banking channel activation (regional bank equities stable, bank CDS not moving in sympathy with any HY widening) means the 2007/1990 systemic template is NOT activating, and any credit-equity stress episode in the near term is more likely to follow the 2015-16 or 2018 Q4 contained template (-22% equity drawdown, 3-6 month resolution).** This negative signal is the most actionable output of the entire credit-equity linkage framework because it constrains the severity distribution. As long as KRE (regional bank ETF) is stable-to-higher while HY widens, the base case is a contained episode. The monitoring framework: if KRE falls >10% in sympathy with HY widening >75bp, the banking channel is activating and the severity distribution shifts dramatically. Until then, dip-buying credit on 50-75bp of widening is the positive-EV trade, with stops triggered by banking channel activation. The 13-14% CET1 ratios provide more buffer than 1990 or 2007, making activation less likely — but CRE concentration at regional banks (28-30%) means the "loading" is present even if activation probability is low.

## Evidence & Reasoning

**Claim 1 — Credit-equity basis:**
The KB documents equity-implied spreads 20-40bp wider than actual spreads (iter_0021, iter_0028) via Merton-model conversion. My estimate of 50-75bp incorporates the true leverage adjustment (7.5-8.5x vs. reported ~6x from EBITDA addbacks) which mechanically widens equity-implied spreads by an additional 25-35bp beyond what unadjusted models show. The 2006-07 precedent is directly relevant: the basis widened from ~30bp to 80-100bp before resolving via credit blowout. The current basis is at 50-75bp — past the early warning threshold but below the 2007 peak. The Kalecki fiscal channel (6-7% deficit sustaining profits at R² ~0.55-0.65) explains why credit can remain tight: the profit floor means interest coverage ratios hold, default probabilities remain low, and spread-tightening is rational conditional on deficit maintenance. The equity market, which also prices the discount rate, sees the term premium channel (ACM +50-100bp) compressing multiples even as the Kalecki floor supports earnings — hence the divergence. This is not a market "error" but a genuine disagreement about which channel dominates: Kalecki (credit view) or term premium (equity view).

**Claim 2 — Correlation regime:**
The KB entry (credit_equity_correlation_regime_dependence, confidence 8.0) documents the empirical regularity across 420 months since 1990: normal correlation 0.3-0.4, crisis correlation 0.7-0.9, with crisis regime active only ~7% of calendar time. The critical cross-asset insight is the transition dynamics: correlation drops to 0.3-0.5 during early stress (the "false comfort" phase where diversification appears to work) before snapping to 0.8-0.9 (when it fails). This is not a smooth transition — it is a regime switch triggered by forced selling, lending pullback, and cross-asset deleveraging. The banking channel (KB confidence 8) is the trigger mechanism. The private credit overlay (KB: 10-15% allocation → 15-25% underestimation of true portfolio volatility during stress) compounds the problem because mark-to-model reporting delays the measured correlation increase by 12-18 months after the economic correlation has already shifted.

**Claim 3 — Structural bifurcation:**
The KB documents that the Merton model is mechanically irrelevant for ~35% of S&P 500 held by net-cash mega-caps. The structural decoupling extends further: the top 10 S&P 500 companies generated ~$300B+ in FCF in 2024-25, are 85-90% self-funded (KB: credit_channel_bifurcation), and have effective WACC insensitivity to credit market conditions. The HY/leveraged loan universe ($3.5-4T) consists primarily of $2-5B market-cap companies, many private, with true leverage of 7.5-8.5x. These are economically separate credit-equity ecosystems that happen to be measured by the same aggregate indicators. The equal-weighted S&P 500 has underperformed the cap-weighted index by ~15-20% cumulatively since late 2022 — a spread consistent with credit stress affecting the median firm while mega-cap earnings concentration masks it at the index level. This compositional distortion means traditional credit-equity linkage analysis that uses cap-weighted SPX as the equity variable is systematically understating credit transmission.

**Claim 4 — Triple-trigger contagion:**
Three discrete transmission channels are documented in the KB at confidence 7-9: (a) maturity wall at confidence 9-10 (unanimous consensus, arithmetic certainty: $900B-$1.1T, 300-500bp coupon step-ups); (b) CLO OC test at confidence 7 (7.5% CCC threshold, step-function not smooth, ~$1T CLO market); (c) basis trade at confidence 7 ($800B-$1T, 50-100x leverage, validated by March 2020). The cross-asset novelty is the interaction: the maturity wall generates the initial downgrades that load the CLO CCC-bucket; CLO shutdown removes leveraged loan demand, widening spreads further; wider spreads → higher vol → basis trade stress → Treasury dysfunction → repo stress → cross-asset forced deleveraging. But the KB also documents that multi-step contagion chains complete only 15-25% of the time, with policy intervention as the primary chain-breaker. The Fed's modified Taylor rule credit term (~100bp HY widening ≈ 25-50bp FFR easing) would activate at ~450-500bp HY OAS, before primary market shutdown. The inflation constraint is the key variable: if core PCE is >3.0% when spreads widen, the Fed is blocked from easing, and the contagion chain completion probability rises from ~15-25% to ~40-50%.

**Claim 5 — Reverse causality and lead-lag:**
The KB documents CDX HY growth from ~$200B to ~$900B and intraday evidence of CDX lagging SPX by 5-30 minutes during stress (synthetic_credit_reverse_causality, confidence 6). Dealer inventory decline from ~$250B to ~$30-40B amplifies non-fundamental flow impact. However, the multi-month lead-lag (credit_equity_lead_lag, confidence 8) survives the reverse-causality critique: 5 of 6 major episodes since 1990 show credit leading or simultaneous with equity, and the direction of lead-lag is diagnostic (credit leading = fundamental deterioration, equities leading = sentiment/exogenous). The cross-asset implication is operational: ignore daily/weekly credit-equity correlation moves (contaminated by CDX hedge flows), and monitor the monthly signal (still informationally valid). Currently, the monthly signal shows no credit deterioration — HY OAS is at 40th-55th percentile, well below the minimum historical threshold for equity stress in any analogue.

**Claim 6 — Kalecki as credit-equity arbiter:**
The KB entry (kalecki_fiscal_channel, confidence 7.5, iterated 4 times across iter_0004/0010/0023/0024 — the most researched concept in the credit-equity linkage topic) establishes the mechanism: Kalecki identity (Profits = Investment + Deficits + Net Exports - Savings) channels 6-7% deficits to profits. S&P 500 EPS grew ~8-10% annualized through 2024-25 despite restrictive monetary policy — directly supporting the Kalecki channel's operational significance. The quality differentiation is critical for credit-equity linkage: the Kalecki floor supports aggregate demand (sufficient for IG and upper HY), but at the firm level, borrowers at true 7.5-8.5x leverage with 500bp+ OAS see 70-80% of actual cash EBITDA consumed by debt service — the aggregate demand floor does not prevent firm-specific defaults when leverage is extreme. This means the Kalecki channel creates a bifurcated credit quality distribution: IG/BB safer than usual (macro demand support), CCC/distressed more dangerous than usual (firm-level leverage overwhelms macro support), and this bifurcation is wider than at any point in the prior three cycles.

**Claim 7 — Private credit as unobservable variable:**
The KB contains 10+ private credit concepts (confidence 5-7) documenting: $1.7T size, NAV autocorrelation 0.5-0.7, secondary market discounts 5-12%, PIK toggle rising from 2% to 5-8%, semi-liquid vehicle mismatch ($300B+), and credit impulse degradation (R² from 0.38 to 0.12). The cross-asset synthesis: private credit has absorbed a significant portion of risk that historically appeared in public HY spreads, meaning current HY OAS of 350-420bp represents a structurally incomplete view of total credit risk. The Godley-type flow disruption channel (private credit NAV writedowns → insurance/pension rebalancing → public credit selling → spread widening) is slower but potentially larger than the acute price contagion channel. The operational challenge: private credit stress is observable only through indirect indicators (PIK toggles, secondary market discounts, quarterly NAV revisions) that lag by 6-18 months, making early detection extremely difficult for cross-asset positioning purposes.

**Claim 8 — Vol divergence as linkage diagnostic:**
The KB entry (cross_asset_vol_divergence, confidence 8) documents the MOVE ~100-120 / VIX ~13-18 configuration as historically anomalous with no precedent for persistence. The credit-equity linkage significance: rates vol (MOVE) reflects the fiscal supply/term premium channel; equity vol (VIX) reflects the Kalecki profit floor suppressing earnings variance; credit vol (HY vol, CDX implied vol) reflects both channels simultaneously and is therefore suppressed by the Kalecki floor while the rates component should be elevated. The vol divergence is not an anomaly — it is the correct pricing of the two competing channels (term premium vs. Kalecki). Resolution requires one channel to dominate: if term premium forces wider spreads (2006-07 template), MOVE stays high and VIX catches up; if Kalecki sustains profits through the maturity wall (benign resolution), MOVE eventually declines toward VIX. The current configuration is unstable because MOVE above 95 for 18+ months is the longest sustained elevation since 2008-2012, and every prior episode of this duration preceded a credit event.

**Claim 9 — Cross-asset trade construction:**
The credit-equity basis trade (long CDX protection + long equal-weight/Russell) is constructed to exploit two simultaneous mispricings: the absolute basis (50-75bp equity-implied vs. actual spreads) and the compositional distortion (equal-weight underperformance as a credit-stress proxy). CDX HY 5Y protection at 350-420bp running spread costs ~3.5-4.2% annually; 6-month 5% OTM HYG puts cost ~1.5-2.0% of notional (cheaper due to suppressed credit vol). The equal-weight/Russell leg is self-financing (long vs. short). Expected payoff profile: in credit widening of 150bp+, CDX protection generates 8-12% on notional while the equity leg's relative performance (equal-weight vs. cap-weight) is approximately flat (both decline, but credit-sensitive companies are already underperforming). In benign resolution, CDX protection loses 3.5-4.2% annually while the equal-weight leg gains from mean reversion. The asymmetric payoff (large gain in stress, small loss in benign) is the hallmark of a well-constructed cross-asset trade.

**Claim 10 — Negative signal from banking channel:**
The KB entry (banking_channel_severity_discriminant, confidence 8) is the highest-conviction finding in the entire credit-equity linkage framework. The operational clarity is rare in cross-asset analysis: monitor KRE (regional bank ETF) and bank CDS relative to HY widening. If HY widens but bank equity is stable → 2015-16 contained template. If bank equity falls in sympathy → 1990/2007 systemic template. Current state: KRE has recovered from March 2023 lows, bank CDS is range-bound, and HY OAS is at cycle tights. All three indicators point to the contained template as the base case. The 13-14% CET1 ratios provide structural buffer vs. 1990 (~6%) and 2007 (~8%). However, CRE concentration at regional banks (28-30% vs. 6-8% for large banks) across ~$2.7T total exposure means the loading exists. The Japan extend-and-pretend model is the most probable resolution (regulatory forbearance → slow bleedthrough → no acute crisis → grinding underperformance). This has a different cross-asset signature than a crisis: grinding rather than acute, underperformance rather than contagion, sector-specific rather than systematic.

## Connections to Other Topics

**Rates-Equity Correlation (iter_0018):** The equity_valuations_correlation_inconsistency concept (confidence 7) directly intersects with the credit-equity basis. S&P 500 at ~21-22x forward P/E implicitly prices negative stock-bond correlation (lower required ERP), but the potentially positive correlation regime (driven by fiscal supply/term premium) implies 18-19x fair value — a 10-25% overvaluation if the correlation regime has shifted. The credit-equity basis (Claim 1) is a manifestation of this same cross-asset inconsistency viewed through the credit lens: credit prices negative correlation (tight spreads = bonds still hedge, macro is benign) while equity vol prices something closer to positive correlation (elevated risk premium = bonds are not hedging). The two inconsistencies are the same inconsistency viewed from different angles, and they should resolve simultaneously.

**Volatility Regimes (iter_0010):** The cross_asset_vol_divergence (MOVE ~100-120, VIX ~13-18) is the vol-space expression of the credit-equity linkage's regime classification. The KB's vol_endogeneity concept establishes that vol regimes are endogenous to macro-financial fragility — meaning the suppressed VIX is not a reliable indicator of low risk but rather a Minsky-consistent signal of fragility accumulation. The credit-equity linkage framework provides the specific transmission mechanism: Kalecki fiscal floor → earnings stability → low equity vol → low credit vol → spread compression → leverage accumulation → fragility loading. The vol divergence resolves when one leg of this chain breaks.

**Fiscal Dominance (iter_0024, iter_0034):** The Kalecki fiscal channel is the bridge between the fiscal dominance research and credit-equity linkage. From my prior analysis in iter_0034, the U.S. scores 2.0-2.5 on the 5-signal fiscal dominance sequence, with 25-35% probability of reaching signal 3 within 3 years. If fiscal dominance deepens, the credit-equity linkage transforms: the stock-bond correlation turns durably positive, the quality factor premium expands 2-3x, and the credit-equity basis resolves via equity de-rating (discount rate channel overwhelms the Kalecki profit channel). Conversely, if DOGE-style consolidation withdraws the fiscal floor, the Kalecki channel deactivates, credit widens, and the equity-leads-credit template activates (sentiment shock, not fundamental credit deterioration). Both fiscal scenarios are vol events.

**Real Estate Cycles (iter_0030):** CRE-to-bank-equity contagion is the specific channel through which real estate stress activates the banking channel severity discriminant. The KB documents the three-stage mechanism (mark-to-market → provisioning → lending pullback) with office down 25-40% from peak. From a cross-asset perspective, CRE stress that remains contained (Japan model, extend-and-pretend) produces grinding regional bank underperformance without activating the credit-equity contagion cascade. CRE stress that triggers Stage 3 (lending pullback) activates the banking channel, shifting the correlation regime from 0.3-0.4 to 0.7-0.9 and producing 2-4x larger equity drawdowns. The cross-asset monitoring signal is straightforward: watch the gap between CMBS BBB- spreads (currently 800-1200bp, already pricing significant stress) and regional bank equity performance (currently stable). Divergence between these two (CMBS stressed, banks stable) is the extend-and-pretend template. Convergence (banks falling to meet CMBS stress pricing) is the banking channel activation signal.

**Credit Cycles (iter_0028, iter_0029):** The maturity wall ($900B-$1.1T, confidence 9-10) is the arithmetic certainty within the credit cycle that generates the specific credit-equity linkage triggers. The KB's maturity_wall_conditional_amplifier concept (2/2 walls with recession = systemic, 0/2 without = orderly) is the cross-asset key: the maturity wall's systemic potential depends entirely on whether it coincides with recession. The Kalecki fiscal channel makes recession less likely (profit floor → employment support → consumption resilience), which argues for the orderly template — unless the fiscal channel is withdrawn first. The sequencing matters enormously: maturity wall before fiscal consolidation = orderly; fiscal consolidation before maturity wall resolution = systemic.

**Deglobalization (iter_0032):** Trade fragmentation interacts with the credit-equity linkage through margin compression in import-exposed sectors. The maturity_wall_deglobalization_interaction concept (correlation 0.5-0.7 for import-exposed sectors) means the default cluster risk from the maturity wall is correlated with the margin-compression risk from deglobalization. Both risks are mitigated by the Kalecki channel (fiscal demand offsets tariff-driven margin pressure), creating a triple interaction: maturity wall × deglobalization × fiscal policy, where any two can be manageable but all three simultaneously would overwhelm the Kalecki offset.

## Open Questions

1. **What is the actual current CCC-bucket utilization across the CLO market?** The KB identifies 4% as comfortable and 6.5% as near-cascade, but the data is not freely available (Intex/LCD required). This single data point determines the proximity to the 7.5% CLO OC test threshold and is the highest-information-density variable in the entire credit-equity linkage framework. One downgrade wave from the maturity wall could breach the threshold if current utilization is >5.5%, but is manageable if <4.5%.

2. **Has the credit-equity lead-lag compressed to below 1 month post-2015 due to algorithmic trading and ETF flows?** If the multi-month lead has compressed to weeks, the operational value of credit-as-leading-indicator is severely impaired. High-frequency data (intraday CDX/SPX correlation structure pre-2015 vs. post-2015) could answer this but requires tick-level data access. The KB flags this as open; it remains the most important methodological question for the framework.

3. **What is the probability distribution of the Kalecki channel's withdrawal?** The KB documents the mechanism thoroughly but does not assign probabilities to the withdrawal scenarios. DOGE-driven consolidation (currently ~0.3-0.6x realization rate on announced savings), political cycle dynamics (2026 midterms, 2028 presidential), and bond vigilante pressure (if r>g triggers) are all potential catalysts. A Bayesian update incorporating DOGE's actual realization rate and Congressional Budget Office trajectory revisions would sharpen the probability estimate from the current wide band (~10-30% within 2 years).

4. **Does the Fed's credit-spread response function (100bp HY widening ≈ 25-50bp FFR easing) still hold when core PCE is above 3%?** The three historical validations (2018 Q4, March 2020, 2023 pause) all occurred with inflation at or below target. The inflation constraint is the key unknown: if the Fed cannot ease in response to credit stress, the contagion chain completion probability doubles (from ~15-25% to ~35-50%), and the entire severity distribution of credit-equity episodes shifts right.

5. **What is the true economic volatility of private credit portfolios, and what is the implied "hidden spread" that would appear in public HY OAS if private credit were marked to market continuously?** The KB estimates 2-3x economic vol vs. reported vol (NAV autocorrelation 0.5-0.7). If the hidden spread is 50bp, current "true" HY OAS is ~400-470bp (still below historical stress thresholds). If 150bp, true OAS is ~500-570bp (already in the self-reinforcing zone). The answer determines whether the credit market's benign pricing is real or illusory.

6. **Can the structural bifurcation (mega-cap net-cash vs. leveraged universe) become a transmission channel rather than just a compositional distortion?** Mechanism: leveraged companies' distress → layoffs and capex cuts → reduced spending on mega-cap services (cloud, advertising, enterprise software) → mega-cap earnings deceleration → index-level correction. This second-order transmission is not well-documented in the KB and could mean that the structural bifurcation delays but does not prevent index-level contagion.

7. **What is the cross-asset leading indicator that would signal the transition from the current "extended late-cycle, fiscally-supported" regime to active credit-equity contagion?** Candidate composite: (a) bank CDS moving above 150bp; (b) HY OAS breaking above 500bp; (c) equal-weight SPX underperforming cap-weight by >5% on a rolling 3-month basis; (d) CLO AAA spreads widening >20bp from tights; (e) private credit secondary discounts widening above 15%. How many of these five need to trigger simultaneously, and what is the optimal weighting?

8. **Is the credit-equity basis (50-75bp) tradeable after accounting for model risk, execution costs, and the carry cost of CDX protection?** The basis is measured through a Merton model conversion that has known systematic errors (50-100bp IG underprediction, Type I errors of 40-60%). If model error is asymmetric (systematically overstating equity-implied spreads), the "tradeable" basis may be 20-40bp rather than 50-75bp — possibly within transaction costs. Rigorous model calibration with addback-adjusted leverage is needed before deploying capital.
