## HIGH_CONFIDENCE

**1. Banking channel activation is the single most important severity discriminant for credit-equity episodes.**
**Confidence: 9/10** | **Agents:** credit_analyst_01, generalist_01, generalist_02, macro_strategist_02, fx_strategist_02, rates_strategist_01, challenger_01 (all 7 non-EM agents plus EM agent)

Banking channel active (1990, 2007-08): median equity drawdown **-38%**, duration **18-30 months**. Inactive (1998, 2015-16, 2020): median **-22%**, duration **3-6 months**. This bifurcation holds across all 7 tabulated episodes spanning 35 years. The real-time diagnostic — whether regional bank equities (KRE) fall in sympathy with HY widening — is operationally clear and uncontested. Current state: **NOT activated** (KRE stable, bank CDS range-bound, CET1 13-14%). CRE concentration at regional banks (28-30% vs 6-8% large banks) constitutes "loading" but not activation. This is the strongest consensus finding: every agent independently identifies it, all four debates confirm it, and no critique undermines it. The only legitimate qualification (from challenger_01) is that 13-14% CET1 ratios may have structurally reduced activation probability vs historical episodes at 4-8% CET1 — a point the debates acknowledge as valid but insufficient to downgrade the discriminant's importance.

**2. The Kalecki-Lévy profit identity is the load-bearing mechanism of the current credit-equity regime.**
**Confidence: 8/10** | **Agents:** credit_analyst_01, generalist_01, generalist_02, macro_strategist_02, rates_strategist_01, fx_strategist_02, challenger_01

The identity (Profits ≡ Investment + Government Deficit + Net Exports − Household Savings) channels 6-7% fiscal deficits (~$1.5-1.8T annually) to corporate profits, mechanically compressing HY OAS and supporting equity valuations. S&P 500 EPS grew ~8-10% annualized through 2024-25 despite restrictive monetary policy — direct evidence. This is accounting arithmetic, not theory. All agents accept the mechanism; debate centers on durability and side effects. The fiscal configuration (6-7% expansion-phase deficit) is genuinely unprecedented in the credit-equity analogue set (generalist_02, confidence 9/10). Fiscal consolidation exceeding 2pp GDP would trigger a credit cycle downshift within 4-6 quarters per the Kalecki framework.

**3. The maturity wall ($900B-$1.5T sub-IG, 2025-2028) is arithmetic certainty; systemic outcome is not.**
**Confidence: 8/10 (existence/arithmetic), 5/10 (systemic outcome)** | **Agents:** credit_analyst_01, credit_analyst_02, generalist_01, generalist_02, macro_strategist_02

Maturities are contractual facts. B-rated borrowers face 200-400bp higher all-in refinancing costs, representing 40-60% cash interest increases. At true leverage of 7.5-8.5x, debt service at 500bp+ OAS consumes 70-80% of actual cash EBITDA for the most leveraged borrowers. The arithmetic is near-unanimously confirmed at confidence 8-10. However, the base rate of maturity walls producing systemic stress is **0-for-4 in modern markets** (credit_analyst_02 acknowledges this). Prior walls (2012, 2014, 2019) were defused by amend-and-extend under ZIRP conditions unavailable today. The maturity wall is a necessary condition for credit-equity contagion, not a sufficient one — and whether it coincides with recession is the key conditional (generalist_02's maturity_wall_conditional_amplifier: 2/2 walls with recession = systemic, 0/2 without = orderly).

**4. EBITDA addbacks (15-40%) and covenant-lite dominance (>90%) systematically distort true credit positioning.**
**Confidence: 8/10** | **Agents:** credit_analyst_01, credit_analyst_02, generalist_01, macro_strategist_02

True leverage of 7.5-8.5x vs reported ~6x is confirmed by ECB and Fed regulatory analyses. Covenant-lite structures eliminate maintenance covenant triggers that historically forced early recognition of distress, delaying default recognition by 12-24 months but increasing loss severity (recoveries of 40-50 cents vs historical 60-70 cents). This distortion degrades every downstream indicator: distance-to-default, credit impulse, cycle dating. Not contested in any debate. The operational implication is that reported credit metrics understate proximity to contagion thresholds across the entire leveraged credit universe.

**5. Credit-equity correlation is bimodal (0.3-0.4 normal, 0.7-0.9 crisis), with crisis regime active ~7% of calendar time and transitions that are non-linear.**
**Confidence: 8/10** | **Agents:** credit_analyst_01, generalist_01, generalist_02, macro_strategist_02

generalist_02 independently tabulates 6 transitions since 1990 with peak crisis correlation of 0.79-0.94, occupying only ~30 of 420 months. The early warning window (correlation rising from 0.3 to 0.5) lasts 2-5 months historically but may be compressing (2020 transition occurred in <1 month). Portfolios assuming stable 0.3-0.4 correlation underestimate true risk, and private credit (mark-to-model NAV autocorrelation 0.5-0.7) compounds this by delaying measured correlation increase by 12-18 months after economic correlation shifts. The regime taxonomy is well-documented; current classification is **normal regime**, not "early stress" (generalist_01's claim to the contrary was refuted in debate).

**6. Private credit opacity ($1.7-1.8T) degrades traditional credit-equity indicators in the direction of false optimism.**
**Confidence: 7/10 (direction), 4/10 (magnitude)** | **Agents:** credit_analyst_01, credit_analyst_02, generalist_01, generalist_02, macro_strategist_02, fx_strategist_02

Mark-to-model NAVs (autocorrelation 0.5-0.7 vs ~0.05 for public markets) suppress measured volatility by 2-3x. PIK toggle usage rising from ~2% to 5-8% is a stress concealment mechanism. Credit impulse R² declined from 0.38 to 0.12, partially attributable to $200B→$1.7T private credit growth. Six agents converge on direction of bias. However, the magnitude of distortion has wide uncertainty (the 15-25% portfolio volatility underestimation figure cited by multiple agents is "analytically derived but not empirically validated" per debate critique). The asset class has never been tested through a full cycle at this scale. challenger_01's counterpoint — that shifting risk from leveraged banks to locked-up capital structurally reduces banking channel activation probability — has merit specifically for the systemic contagion question, even as it concedes the measurement problem.

**7. The credit-equity lead-lag (2-6 months historically) has compressed post-2015, but the direction of lead-lag remains diagnostic of shock type.**
**Confidence: 7/10** | **Agents:** credit_analyst_01, generalist_01, generalist_02, macro_strategist_02, challenger_01

Five of 6-7 major episodes since 1990 show credit leading or moving simultaneously with equity. The compression from 6-8 months to 0-2 months is driven by two factors: (a) CDX/ETF growth creating reverse causality at high frequency (credit_analyst_01, generalist_01), and (b) private credit opacity structurally degrading the aggregate signal (credit_analyst_02 — stronger explanation per debate). The diagnostic value survives: credit leading = fundamental deterioration (2007 template, more dangerous); equity leading = exogenous/sentiment shock (2018 Q4, 2020 template, more likely to reverse). Currently, **credit is NOT leading** — HY OAS is near cycle tights — arguing against the fundamental deterioration template. challenger_01's selection bias critique (only 4 of 11 HY>500bp episodes produced >20% equity drawdowns, a 36% hit rate) is valid for HY-as-standalone-signal but does not invalidate the direction-of-lead-lag diagnostic.

**8. The CLO OC test 7.5% CCC-bucket threshold creates a discrete, non-linear contagion channel.**
**Confidence: 7/10** | **Agents:** credit_analyst_01, credit_analyst_02, generalist_01, macro_strategist_02

The mechanism is contractually specified: breach → cash flow diversion from equity to senior tranches → CLO equity return destruction → forced loan selling → new CLO formation shutdown → removal of ~65-70% of leveraged loan demand. Current CCC-bucket utilization of 4-6.5% places the system one downgrade wave from the 7.5% threshold. challenger_01 argues CLO managers actively trade to manage exposure and 2020 demonstrated resilience, but the debate correctly notes 2020 was contaminated by $1.6T of Fed intervention and does not constitute a clean stress test. The step-function framing is somewhat too binary (CLO managers do pre-emptively sell approaching-CCC credits), but the non-linear character of the mechanism at the margin is well-established. **CCC-bucket utilization is the single most operationally critical data point** — both credit analysts independently identify it as the most urgent data gap (Intex/LCD required, not freely available).

**9. The structural bifurcation between mega-cap net-cash equities (~35% of S&P 500) and the leveraged credit universe (~$3.5T) means aggregate cross-asset indicators systematically understate credit-equity stress.**
**Confidence: 8/10** | **Agents:** generalist_01, credit_analyst_01, macro_strategist_02

The top 10 S&P 500 companies hold ~$500B net cash and are 85-90% self-funded — the Merton model is mechanically irrelevant for them. The HY/leveraged loan universe consists of $2-5B market-cap companies at true 7.5-8.5x leverage. These are economically separate ecosystems measured by the same aggregate indicators. Equal-weighted S&P 500 has underperformed cap-weighted by ~15-20% since late 2022, consistent with credit stress affecting the median firm while mega-cap concentration masks it. The equal-weight/cap-weight ratio is a more accurate credit-equity diagnostic than aggregate HY-SPX correlation. Not contested in debate; generalist_02 entirely misses this insight.

## MODERATE_CONFIDENCE

**1. The Kalecki fiscal buffer is quality-differentiated: sufficient for IG/upper HY, insufficient for CCC/leveraged loans.**
**Confidence: 6/10** | **Agents:** generalist_01 (primary), credit_analyst_02

The Kalecki identity operates on aggregate corporate profits, not individual firm cash flows. A B-rated borrower at 8x true leverage refinancing at 400bp higher doesn't benefit from the aggregate demand floor — its interest coverage is collapsing regardless. This creates a bifurcated credit quality distribution wider than in prior cycles. credit_analyst_02 validates: "the maturity wall operates at the firm level, below the Kalecki floor's effective altitude." The debate confirms this is a precise and correct critique of the aggregate Kalecki thesis. However, the threshold above which the Kalecki floor becomes irrelevant — and the share of the leveraged loan universe above it — has not been quantified.

**2. Term premium is a first-order common factor driving credit-equity co-movement, and the current term premium / credit premium divergence is a potential instability signal.**
**Confidence: 7/10 (mechanism), 5/10 (divergence as actionable signal)** | **Agents:** rates_strategist_01 (primary), generalist_01

Three clean confirming episodes: 2013 Taper Tantrum, 2018 Q4, 2023 Q3 — each showing supply-driven term premium expansion producing synchronized credit widening and equity decline without fundamental earnings deterioration. The mechanism is theoretically robust and empirically supported. The current divergence (ACM term premium +80-130bp vs HY OAS 350-420bp) is an internal market inconsistency, but with only n=3 prior episodes, resolution direction is uncertain (2/3 resolved via credit widening). rates_strategist_01 calls it "the single most actionable signal" but assigns only 6/10 confidence — an internal contradiction noted in debate. The Kalecki channel provides a coherent structural explanation for persistent divergence. Monitor, don't trade.

**3. The basis trade ($800B-$1T at 50-100x leverage) has converted the Treasury market from potential safe haven to potential contagion source.**
**Confidence: 6/10** | **Agents:** rates_strategist_01, generalist_01, generalist_02, macro_strategist_02, fx_strategist_02

March 2020 partially validated the mechanism ($1.6T Fed emergency intervention required). Current notional is 2-3x the March 2020 level. Dealer inventory decline from ~$250B to ~$30-40B impairs shock absorption. The contagion chain (term premium shock → basis trade unwind → repo stress → dealer liquidity withdrawal → credit widening → equity deleveraging) is mechanistically sound. However, post-2020 regulatory attention may have reduced the risk, exact size/leverage estimates are uncertain, and the SEC central clearing mandate (Q2 2026) may force position adjustment. fx_strategist_02 extends this to EM reserve adequacy framework invalidation — theoretically important but empirically thin (5/10).

**4. Contagion chain completion probability is approximately 35-40%, not 15-25%.**
**Confidence: 6/10** | **Agents:** generalist_02 (primary), credit_analyst_01

generalist_02 derives ~35-40% from transparent analogue decomposition: 3 of 7 tabulated episodes (1990, 2001-02, 2007-09) produced severe outcomes. generalist_01's 15-25% is not transparently derived and appears deflated by conditioning on favorable Fed response. The debate correctly notes generalist_01 acknowledges the probability rises to 35-50% if inflation blocks the Fed — converging with generalist_02's unconditional estimate. The **inflation constraint on the Fed** is the critical swing variable: operational Fed put reduces completion probability to ~20-25%; constrained Fed raises it to ~40-50%.

**5. The "hidden fragility" narrative is partially priced consensus — but specific transmission mechanisms are not.**
**Confidence: 6/10** | **Agents:** challenger_01 (primary)

challenger_01 demonstrates convincingly that CRE repricing, maturity wall risk, and generic "credit event" tail risk are consensus concerns: BofA Fund Manager Survey, ubiquitous sell-side reports, KRE shorts, CMBS BBB- at 800-1200bp, CDX protection bid. However, the debates clarify that technically sophisticated mechanisms (term premium decomposition, basis trade dual distortion, CLO OC test plumbing, deficit-term premium reflexive loop) are NOT in every sell-side deck and may not be fully priced. The meta-observation is valid: the pain trade may be tighter spreads/higher equity, and the carry cost of hedging credit risk at current spread levels is punitive.

**6. CET1 ratios at 13-14% represent a genuine structural break vs historical episodes (4-8%).**
**Confidence: 7/10** | **Agents:** challenger_01 (primary), generalist_02, credit_analyst_01

SVB/Signature/First Republic failures in 2023 were contained precisely because the system was well-capitalized enough to absorb losses. This is direct empirical evidence that the banking channel operates differently at current capitalization levels. The qualitative direction is clear (more buffer = higher activation threshold), but quantitative calibration is uncertain — does 2x capital halve activation probability or merely delay it? The debate consensus: activation probability is materially lower than historical base rates imply, but CRE concentration at regional banks (28-30%) means loading exists even if the threshold is higher.

**7. Synthetic credit reverse causality (equity → credit via CDX/ETFs) contaminates the credit-equity signal at high frequency but not at the fundamental multi-month horizon.**
**Confidence: 6/10** | **Agents:** credit_analyst_01, generalist_01, challenger_01

CDX HY open interest at ~$900B and credit ETFs at ~$400B create a channel where equity vol mechanically widens credit spreads via hedge demand. Intraday evidence shows CDX lagging SPX by 5-30 minutes during stress. The multi-month fundamental signal is claimed to survive, but this requires frequency-domain decomposition that has not been rigorously performed. The claim that fundamental-horizon signals are intact is "logically sound but requires more rigorous decomposition" (credit_analyst_01's own caveat).

**8. The yield-curve-mediated contagion sequence (inversion → bear steepener → credit blowout → equity drawdown) is historically reliable but may be interrupted by the fiscal channel.**
**Confidence: 5/10** | **Agents:** generalist_02, rates_strategist_01

Documented in 4-5 of 6 post-1970 recession episodes with 14-18 month median lag. Current cycle: inversion began July 2022 (32+ months ago), steepening underway. The current lag already exceeds the historical maximum by 80%+. Either the fiscal channel has genuinely interrupted the sequence (no precedent) or the lag is simply longer (no precedent either). rates_strategist_01's claim that the current cycle has "completed stages 1-2" is weakened by the debate's observation that the current steepening is term-premium-driven, not expectations-driven — a mechanistically distinct process from historical bear steepeners.

**9. CLO reinvestment period expirations ($250-350B over 18 months) will structurally remove the marginal bid for leveraged loans independently of credit deterioration.**
**Confidence: 6/10** | **Agents:** credit_analyst_02 (primary)

Post-reinvestment CLOs become amortizing vehicles that stop buying new loans without becoming forced sellers. This gradual demand withdrawal coincides with the maturity wall's peak refinancing window. Current SOFR levels make reset activity (the historical defusing mechanism) less favorable. The 2019 CLO cliff was pre-empted by resets, but at lower rates. This is analytically distinct from the OC test breach story and represents a supply-demand headwind that does not require credit deterioration to produce spread widening.

**10. Convex EM transmission from DM credit stress, with banking channel producing 1.5-3x EM amplification.**
**Confidence: 7/10** | **Agents:** fx_strategist_02 (primary)

Empirically robust across 5+ episodes. Three channels (balance sheet/dollar liabilities, financial conditions, commodity prices) are positively correlated during stress — diversification benefit is negative. EM twin deficit screening (CA + fiscal deficit >5% GDP + short-term external debt/reserves) has 70-80% hit rate for >20% depreciation during DM tightening episodes. Current EM fundamentals are stronger than historical stress episodes (median reserves ~7 months vs ~5 months in 2013), which may reduce amplification magnitude.

**11. The Godley sectoral balance identity forces both fiscal resolution paths toward instability.**
**Confidence: 7/10** | **Agents:** macro_strategist_02 (primary)

If Private + Government + Foreign ≡ 0, and the private surplus of ~3-4% of GDP depends entirely on the government deficit, then: Path A (fiscal consolidation) arithmetically reduces corporate profits via the Kalecki identity. Path B (fiscal continuation) generates $2T+ annual Treasury issuance driving term premium higher, eventually loading the basis trade contagion channel. Godley's 1999 Levy Institute prediction (correctly calling the unsustainability of the late-1990s private deficit configuration) provides out-of-sample validation of the framework. The question is timing and trigger, not whether the current configuration is indefinitely sustainable.

## LOW_CONFIDENCE

**1. Private credit may be a net systemic risk reducer rather than amplifier.**
**Confidence: 4/10** | **Agent:** challenger_01 (Claim 5)

By shifting credit risk from leveraged banks (systemic via deposit runs, interbank funding, lending pullback) to locked-up long-duration capital (insurance, pension, endowments with 7-10 year lockups), private credit may structurally reduce banking channel activation probability. Mark-to-model smoothing prevents forced-selling cascades. Theoretically sound but untested in severe stress. Insurance/pension losses create second-order political and regulatory channels that are slower but potentially significant. Not corroborated by other agents, though not refuted either.

**2. The deficit-term premium reflexive loop is activating and inherently destabilizing.**
**Confidence: 4/10** | **Agent:** rates_strategist_01 (Claim 6)

Net interest rising from ~$350B (FY2020) to ~$1T (FY2025/26). The blended borrowing rate (~4.5%) vs nominal GDP growth (~5.5%) margin of ~100bp is historically thin. Each 100bp of additional rates generates ~$250-320B in additional interest payments, of which 10-25% recycles as spending (partially offsetting monetary tightening). Mechanism is logically sound but the US-specific activation threshold given reserve currency status is unknown. UK gilt crisis validates for non-reserve-currency sovereigns but US may differ structurally.

**3. The SEC central clearing mandate (Q2 2026) is a calendar-identifiable catalyst for basis trade position adjustment.**
**Confidence: 4/10** | **Agent:** rates_strategist_01

Concrete, date-specific, independently verifiable. A non-consensus observation with practical value. However, regulatory implementation timelines are uncertain and the market may front-run or adapt smoothly. Not corroborated by other agents.

**4. The Kalecki paradox for EM: US fiscal expansion simultaneously supports DM credit quality and tightens EM conditions.**
**Confidence: 5/10** | **Agent:** fx_strategist_02 (Claim 3)

Mechanism is arithmetically sound: fiscal deficit → Treasury issuance → dollar/term premium pressure → EM tightening. But magnitude of EM tightening from US fiscal channel alone (vs other dollar drivers) is imprecisely estimated. An important asymmetry (the fiscal channel protecting DM is the same channel stressing EM) that lacks sufficient corroboration from other agents.

**5. Japan's experience (fiscal support sustained economy but not asset prices — equity -60%+ despite deficits) is a cautionary parallel for the US.**
**Confidence: 4/10** | **Agent:** generalist_02 (Claim 3)

This is the most damaging counter-evidence to the "Kalecki floor supports asset prices" thesis. Japan ran 5-10% deficits for 15+ years after 1990 — the fiscal channel sustained the economy but did not prevent a 60%+ equity decline and 30-year bond bull market. Critical differences: Japan's corporate sector was the epicenter of excess leverage; the US parallel would require private credit losses to force multi-year loss recognition. Raised in debate as underweighted by all other agents.

**6. Reserve adequacy frameworks (Guidotti-Greenspan, IMF ARA) may be structurally broken if Treasuries can become illiquid during stress.**
**Confidence: 4/10** | **Agent:** fx_strategist_02 (Claim 5)

If Treasury dysfunction causes 5-10% mark-to-market loss on reserves during a stress event, effective reserve adequacy drops simultaneously with increased intervention demand. March 2020 partially validated ($100B+ in reserves sold during dysfunction). China/Saudi gold diversification is cited as evidence EM central banks recognize this. Theoretically important but empirically thin.

## REFUTED

**1. generalist_01's claim that the current regime is in "early stress decorrelation" phase.**
HY OAS at 350-420bp (near cycle tights), KRE stable-to-higher, bank CDS range-bound, VIX 13-18 — every indicator both generalist agents identify reads "normal," not "early stress." generalist_01 self-downgrades to 6/10 and admits "HY OAS at 350-420bp argues against stress." The label appears motivated by the need to justify the trade recommendation. Correct characterization: **normal regime with elevated structural loading**.

**2. generalist_01's cross-asset trade (long CDX protection + long equal-weight / short cap-weight SPX) as "actionable" with "positive expected return."**
generalist_01's own Open Question #8 identifies three problems that collectively destroy the trade's edge: (a) Merton model has 50-100bp systematic errors and 40-60% Type I error rate, potentially reducing the "tradeable" basis to 20-40bp; (b) CDX protection costs 3.5-4.2% annually in carry; (c) resolution time horizon is uncertain. If true basis is 20-40bp with 3.5-4.2% annual carry, the trade is negative expected value unless it resolves within ~6-9 months. Presenting it as "actionable" while acknowledging it may not be profitable is an internal contradiction.

**3. generalist_01's credit-equity basis precision of "50-75bp."**
The figure depends on addback-adjusted leverage inputs fed into a Merton model with acknowledged systematic errors of 50-100bp. The addback adjustment adds 25-35bp but is itself a judgment call. This is a model output presented with the confidence of a market observable. Directionally suggestive; the precision overstates what the methodology can deliver.

**4. macro_strategist_02's claim that "the Merton structural model gets the causality backwards."**
Overstated. The Merton model's 50-70% cross-sectional R² (which macro_strategist_02 concedes) is substantial evidence that equity values contain information about credit risk. The Post-Keynesian endogenous money framework is superior for macro-cyclical dynamics but cannot price individual credits. The claim implies equity has no informational content about credit risk, which the agent's own acknowledgment of 50-70% R² contradicts. Correct framing: Merton is incomplete and locally useful, not causally backwards.

**5. macro_strategist_02's labeling of credit factor crowding as "Ponzi finance."**
The PC1 >65% finding and skewness/kurtosis statistics are real, but "Ponzi finance" in Minsky's framework requires cash flows insufficient to cover principal or interest, requiring asset sales or new borrowing to remain solvent. Holding a correlated portfolio is mispriced risk, not Ponzi finance. The underlying observation (portfolios underestimate true volatility) is valuable; the Minsky taxonomy is stretched beyond its definitional boundaries.

**6. generalist_01's contagion chain completion probability of "only 15-25%."**
Not derived from a transparent sample. generalist_02's independently derived ~35-40% from explicit analogue tabulation (3 severe outcomes in 7 episodes) is more methodologically defensible. generalist_01 acknowledges the probability rises to 35-50% if inflation blocks the Fed, which converges with generalist_02's unconditional estimate. The lower bound appears deflated by conditioning on favorable Fed response without labeling this as conditional.

**7. fx_strategist_02's claim that "EM carry basket is collecting pennies in front of a steamroller."**
The agent assigns 6/10 confidence and admits the framing "has been wrong-but-early many times." The residual "pure" carry Sharpe of 0.15-0.30 with confidence interval including zero is unremarkable for most single-factor strategies. The "steamroller" metaphor implies near-certainty of timing while actual evidence supports only elevated conditional probability. The claim does not survive its own confidence assessment.

**8. credit_analyst_02's 9/10 confidence on the maturity wall as "single most predictable catalyst" for systemic contagion.**
The arithmetic deserves 9-10/10. The systemic claim does not. The base rate of maturity walls producing systemic stress is 0-for-4 in modern markets. "This time is different because rates are higher" is conditional reasoning that demands lower confidence. The maturity wall is a necessary condition, not sufficient. Downgrade to 6-7/10 for systemic outcome, consistent with credit_analyst_01's more measured assessment.

**9. challenger_01's 2020 CLO stress test as evidence of structural resilience.**
2020 CLO equity returns were excellent precisely because the Fed injected $1.6T in emergency liquidity and produced a V-shaped recovery. Using the outcome of a massively intervened episode to argue intervention isn't needed is survivorship reasoning. A slow grind without Fed support would be a fundamentally different test. The evidence is contaminated, not confirmatory.

## KEY_DISAGREEMENTS

**1. Is the Kalecki fiscal buffer contingent or structurally durable on any actionable timeframe?**
- **Durable camp** (challenger_01, 7/10): Political economy makes consolidation >2pp GDP impossible. Neither party has an austerity mandate. DOGE impact is $100-200B/yr vs $2T+ deficit. Japan precedent: 30+ years without consolidation.
- **Contingent camp** (macro_strategist_02, rates_strategist_01): The buffer doesn't need political withdrawal to destabilize. The same deficits that sustain profits generate issuance that elevates term premium. The reflexive loop (deficit → issuance → term premium → higher interest → larger deficit) is endogenously destabilizing.
- **Resolution needed:** What observable signal distinguishes "approaching the instability" from "years of continued fiscal-supported compression"? Neither camp provides a stopping rule.

**2. Has the financial accelerator been empirically discredited by 2022-2025 non-activation?**
- **Yes** (challenger_01, 7/10): 525bp hikes, three bank failures, 25-40% office declines — none triggered the accelerator. CET1 buffers, Kalecki support, and labor market strength absorbed the shock.
- **No** (credit_analyst_01, macro_strategist_02): The accelerator is a threshold phenomenon. Non-activation when buffers are intact simply means the threshold is higher, not that the mechanism doesn't exist. If buffers erode, the accelerator fires.
- **Resolution needed:** Formal recalibration of the accelerator coefficient using 2022-2025 data. What updated calibration parameters does the recent non-event imply?

**3. Is private credit net stabilizing or destabilizing for systemic risk?**
- **Stabilizing** (challenger_01, 6/10): Shifts risk from leveraged, runnable banks to locked-up, long-duration capital. Mark-to-model smoothing prevents forced-selling cascades.
- **Destabilizing** (credit_analyst_02, macro_strategist_02): Opacity hides stress accumulation. BDC/interval fund redemptions ($300B), insurance RBC impairment ($700B), and information contagion create spillover channels. No historical stress test at $1.7T scale.
- **Resolution needed:** What is the actual loss absorption capacity of insurance companies and pension funds for private credit losses? What is the threshold at which private credit losses impair these holders' other functions?

**4. Does the yield curve contagion sequence still operate when the steepening is term-premium-driven rather than expectations-driven?**
- **Yes** (rates_strategist_01): Term premium steepening is actually worse for credit-equity — pure discount rate compression without growth offset.
- **Uncertain** (generalist_02, challenger_01): The current cycle has exceeded the historical median lag by 80%+. The fiscal channel may have genuinely interrupted the sequence. No precedent for term-premium-driven steepening following a 32+ month inversion.
- **Resolution needed:** Is the current lag "broken" or "extended"? The 32-month inversion without credit blowout either means the sequence is structurally different this time or the lag is simply longer.

**5. What is the appropriate contagion chain completion probability?**
- **~35-40% unconditional** (generalist_02): Based on 3/7 episodes producing severe outcomes.
- **~15-25% conditional on Fed response** (generalist_01): Policy intervention as primary chain-breaker.
- **~40-50% conditional on inflation-constrained Fed** (both agents converge here).
- **Resolution needed:** The inflation constraint is the swing variable. What is P(inflation constrains Fed | credit stress emerges)? This joint probability is the most decision-relevant unknown.
