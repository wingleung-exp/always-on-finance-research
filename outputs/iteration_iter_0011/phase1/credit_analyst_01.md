1. **What is the threshold AAA spread for economically viable CLO resets, and how much of the $350-400B reinvestment cliff can be defused by reset activity at current spreads?** This was my iter_0009 Open Question 1 and remains the most consequential unanswered parameter. At AAA spreads of SOFR+140-155bp, I estimate ~30-40% of the cliff can be defused through resets. At SOFR+120 (tighter), perhaps 50-60%. At SOFR+170+ (wider), less than 20%. The CLO market itself will reveal this over H2 2026.

2. **Is the "frozen exuberance" state stable for 12+ months, or does the structural deterioration eventually force price recognition regardless of macro catalyst?** My three-phase framework (Claim 6) implies the transition from Phase 1 to Phase 2 is inevitable, but the timing is uncertain. Could the system remain frozen for 2+ years (as it arguably did in 2006-2007), or are the current structural stresses (maturity wall proximity, CLO cliff timing) more binding than the pre-GFC analogues?

3. **How does the macro-uncertainty premium (Claim 3) interact with the CLO equity IRR hurdle?** If CLO equity investors incorporate the macro-uncertainty premium into their required returns (raising the IRR threshold from 12-13% to 14-15%), the CLO arbitrage window closes at tighter AAA spreads than credit_analyst_02's threshold analysis implies. This would accelerate the demand-supply gap in Claim 2.

4. **What is the actual overlapping borrower population across CLO collateral pools and private credit portfolios?** Credit_analyst_02's iter_0010 Open Question 1 (estimated 20-40% for upper-middle-market) remains unanswered. The degree of overlap determines whether stress in one market mechanically marks down the other, or whether the markets are more independent than feared.

5. **Can the shadow default rate be tracked in real time using observable data (BDC portfolio disclosures, CLO trustee reports, amendment filing databases)?** This was my iter_0009 Open Question 6 and remains the highest-value measurement innovation this research program could produce. A quarterly shadow default rate publication — even with ±200bp uncertainty — would be more informative than the current reported rate.

6. **If the three-way indicator divergence (Claim 10) resolves toward the structural reading, what is the specific confirming event most likely to trigger the information cascade?** Candidates include: (a) a high-profile private credit fund gating, (b) a fallen angel cascade in public BBB, (c) a CLO tranche downgrade wave, (d) a sovereign or quasi-sovereign credit event in EM dollar debt. Each has different probability and different implications for the cascade speed. Identifying the most likely trigger would improve tactical positioning.

7. **Has the r-star fog created a structural "unhedgeable" risk in credit that persists until the fog clears — meaning there is no market-available instrument that can hedge the macro-uncertainty premium because the premium reflects irreducible epistemic uncertainty rather than tradeable risk?** If so, credit investors are being compensated for bearing genuinely unhedgeable risk, which has implications for whether the macro-uncertainty premium should be harvested (as compensation for real risk) or avoided (as compensation for an ambiguity that markets habitually underprice).

8. **What is the insurance industry's actual committed but undrawn allocation to private credit, and how does this constrain their ability to act as "spread floor" buyers during the next risk-off episode in public markets?** Committed but undrawn private credit allocations function as a liability that constrains available capital even before it is deployed — a "shadow demand drain" from public credit markets.
