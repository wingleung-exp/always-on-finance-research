### Claim 4: Structural Obsolescence — No Clean Analogue

Previous CRE downturns were primarily capital-market events: overbuilding + credit tightening → vacancy → repricing → eventual absorption as the economy grew into the excess supply. The demand eventually returned.

The remote/hybrid work shock is structurally different. Office utilization rates are running 50-60% of pre-COVID levels in major metros even as leases roll. This creates a "stranded asset" dynamic more akin to the coal-to-gas transition in energy than to a traditional CRE cycle. Historical CRE analogues assume the demand eventually returns — for Class B/C office, this assumption may be fundamentally wrong.

The closest analogue for structural obsolescence in real estate is retail post-ecommerce (2015-present), where category-level demand destruction produced permanent repricing in malls and lower-quality retail. The pattern: initial denial → extend-and-pretend → eventual markdown of 50-80% for impaired properties, with a long tail of conversions and demolitions.
