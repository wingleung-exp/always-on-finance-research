# AI & Tech Disruption — Cross-Asset Strategist (Generalist) Analysis

## Key Claims

1. **The cross-asset pricing inconsistency is the single most important investable signal, but the winner-take-all equilibrium provides a partial — and fragile — reconciliation.** Equities price >2% structural real growth (S&P 500 at 21-22x forward, ERP compressed to 2.5-3.0%), rates price ~1.5-1.8% (OIS ~3.4-3.7%, no r* uplift), credit prices no disruption (IG 120-180bp), and the dollar prices sustained capex inflows ($40-60B/month). If AI value accrues to 5-7 firms without macro diffusion, all four can be approximately correct simultaneously — but the structure is load-bearing on a handful of corporate capex decisions, making the reconciliation inherently unstable.

2. **The correlation regime has shifted to a persistent positive stock-bond return correlation (+0.15 to +0.35 rolling 6-month), driven by a three-force conjunction that is specifically AI-regime-dependent.** The three forces — AI capex demand-pull (inflationary), no TFP supply breakthrough (no disinflationary offset), and fiscal dominance (6-7% GDP deficits) — are mutually reinforcing. This regime makes bonds a co-risk rather than a hedge for equity-heavy portfolios. Risk parity portfolios are running ~20-30% excess equity-equivalent risk without adjustment. The regime shift condition is an AI productivity supply breakthrough, which has only 15-25% probability within 3 years.

3. **Real rates at ~2.0-2.2% 10Y TIPS are the cross-asset fulcrum, and the current level creates a "no good outcome" asymmetry for US-centric multi-asset portfolios.** A 50bp rise: Mag-7 -12-15%, S&P 500 -6-8%, IG +10-20bp, DXY +2-3%. A 50bp decline: equities rally but dollar weakens, stressing the capex-profit-inflow feedback loop that sustains equity valuations. There is no real rate path that simultaneously benefits all legs of a standard US multi-asset allocation.

4. **The equity-credit sequencing pattern is actively signaling, with equity leading credit by the historical 6-12 months, implying credit widening should accelerate by H2 2026-H1 2027.** Equal-weighted S&P 500 underperformance of 800-1200bp (breadth deterioration, Stage 1) is already active. Legacy media equity down 25-30% while IG spreads at 120-180bp vs historical distressed 300-500bp represents a negative equity-credit basis exceeding 2-sigma. In 5/6 historical episodes, equity was the correct forward signal.

5. **The self-funded nature of AI capex transforms the risk profile from crash-risk to duration-risk, making the optimal cross-asset expression a relative value trade rather than an outright short.** OCF/capex at 1.5-1.7x eliminates the Minsky sudden-stop. Historical analogues (Japan 1990s-2000s: TOPIX underperformed MSCI World by ~60% over 13 years; energy majors 2010-2014: P/E compressed from 14x to 8x over 2 years) both produced slow grinds. The basis trade ($800B-$1T at 50-100x leverage) remains the primary channel by which a slow grind could become a fast one.

6. **The Kalecki capex-profit-equity-inflow-dollar feedback loop is the defining cross-asset mechanism, and its self-reinforcing nature works in both directions.** AI capex (~$250B) sustains profits via Kalecki → profits sustain equity prices → equity prices attract $40-60B/month international inflows → inflows support dollar → dollar supports Mag-7 earnings translation. The fiscal deficit floor ($1.8-2T) provides partial insulation (a 25% capex cut is only 3-4% of the fiscal deficit), but the marginal nature of AI capex means it drives the growth rate of profits even if fiscal sustains the level.

7. **The regional ERP differential (US 2.5-3.0% vs Europe 5-6%, Japan 4-5%, EM 4-6%) represents the highest-conviction cross-asset relative value opportunity, with asymmetric payoff regardless of AI outcome resolution.** S&P 500 ex-tech vs MSCI EAFE has been roughly flat since 2010, isolating AI as the single driver of US exceptionalism. If AI disappoints: US multiples compress disproportionately. If AI delivers but diffuses: ex-US adopters benefit at lower entry valuations. Only the winner-take-all-stays-concentrated scenario favors continued US overweight — and this is the scenario with maximum systemic fragility.

8. **Factor space is severely compromised as a diversification tool: momentum ENB at ~25-35 (below 2007 quant quake), momentum-quality correlation tripled to +0.50-0.60, and PC1 explains 45-50% of return variance versus 30-35% historically.** Multi-factor portfolios holding both momentum and quality are running ~2x intended AI directional exposure with zero inter-factor diversification benefit. Market-neutral portfolios carry ~15-25% unhedged AI directional exposure. Risk models systematically underestimate true AI concentration by 3-4x.

## Evidence & Reasoning

**Claim 1 — Cross-Asset Inconsistency:**
The inconsistency is not merely observational but quantifiable. Equities require 14-16% revenue CAGR to ~$3.5-4T by 2035 to justify current multiples (reverse DCF), while the rates market refuses to price any r* uplift above Laubach-Williams ~1.0-1.2%. BLS TFP at ~1.5-2.0% (no structural break, confidence 9) validates rates. The winner-take-all partial reconciliation (confidence 6.5) works mechanically but creates macro fragility via the Kalecki dependence on narrow capex decisions. The inconsistency narrows through either: (a) TFP materializes (rates repriced higher), (b) equities derate (multiples compress), or (c) winner-take-all persists (concentration = correct pricing, but systemic fragility). Option (c) is the most probable near-term outcome but carries the highest tail risk.

**Claim 2 — Correlation Regime:**
The three-force decomposition is the analytically correct framework. AI capex of $200-250B is demand-pull (inflationary) without offsetting TFP supply (no structural break at confidence 9). Fiscal deficits at 6-7% GDP add persistent demand. Together: inflation-compatible, growth-positive — the textbook regime for positive stock-bond correlation. The regime shift requires AI TFP breakthrough (15-25% probability per 3-5yr) or fiscal consolidation (politically constrained by sovereign AI path dependency). Current 60/40 portfolios implicitly bet on Stage 2+ AI productivity supply arriving before the demand-inflation regime intensifies.

**Claim 3 — Real Rate Fulcrum:**
The no-good-outcome dynamic is mathematical. At ~2.0-2.2% 10Y TIPS: equity duration (S&P 500 effective duration ~20-25 years at current P/E) makes the index highly sensitive to real rate moves. Simultaneously, the dollar-capex loop requires sustained inflows ($40-60B/month per TIC) which depend on equity returns which depend on the real rate. The sensitivity estimates are subject to ±30% error but the directional asymmetry is robust.

**Claim 4 — Equity-Credit Sequencing:**
The pattern appeared in 3/3 well-documented episodes (1998-2002, 2005-2008, 2012-2016) with 83% hit rate. Current signals: breadth deterioration (equal-weighted underperformance 800-1200bp, matching 1998 timing), secondary beneficiary stress (legacy media equity -25-30% while credit tight). The self-funded nature of hyperscaler capex means credit receives no early debt-stress warning, making equity the necessary leading indicator. If the historical 6-12 month lead holds from current breadth deterioration signals (active since mid-2025), credit widening accelerates H2 2026-H1 2027.

**Claim 5 — Duration Risk vs Crash Risk:**
The OCF/capex ratio of 1.5-1.7x is the critical structural differentiator from telecom (debt-funded, >100% capex/OCF). With no creditor-imposed stop-loss, the Japanese corporate analogue (sub-WACC investment for decades, internally funded) and energy major analogue (P/E compression without bankruptcy) are more applicable than the telecom bust. The basis trade ($800B-$1T at 50-100x leverage) with cross-margining at multi-strategy funds is the mechanism by which a slow equity grind could trigger a rates crisis, converting duration risk to crash risk. March 2020 validated this channel at approximately half current scale.

**Claim 6 — Kalecki Feedback Loop:**
The loop is an accounting identity (Kalecki) coupled with observable capital flow data (TIC). AI capex ~$250B = entire growth margin of private investment. The loop is self-reinforcing: capex → profits (Kalecki) → equity prices → inflows (TIC $40-60B/month) → dollar strength → earnings translation → confidence → capex. The fiscal deficit provides a level floor ($1.8-2T), but AI capex provides the growth floor. The DOGE fiscal paradox (consolidation could independently break the loop) adds an exogenous risk.

**Claim 7 — Regional ERP:**
The 200-500bp US-vs-world ERP gap is the most robust quantitative signal in the entire analysis. Multiple estimation methods converge (Fed model, DDM, Fama-French cross-sectional). The gap is entirely attributable to AI-adjacent mega-cap valuations, evidenced by equal-weighted S&P 500 ERP of 4.5-5.0% — in line with international markets. The asymmetry: AI disappointment compresses US multiples 20-30% while ex-US multiples have minimal AI premium to lose. AI realization that diffuses broadly would lift ex-US markets at cheaper entry points.

**Claim 8 — Factor Space Compromise:**
Momentum ENB at ~25-35 is below the 2007 quant quake (~30-40), a threshold that preceded a significant momentum factor drawdown. The momentum-quality correlation tripling from +0.15-0.25 to +0.50-0.60 means multi-factor diversification is illusory. PC1 at 45-50% (vs 30-35% historical) contaminates market-neutral strategies. SBC quality factor contamination (100-200bp of QMJ premium is SBC artifact, shifting 2-3 Mag-7 names from top to 2nd-3rd quintile on GAAP sort) is a specific mechanism by which this occurred.

## Open Questions

1. **Which market resolves the inconsistency first — and in which direction?** Equities could derate, rates could reprice higher, or the winner-take-all equilibrium could persist for years. The sequencing matters enormously for portfolio construction. Historical base rate (0/5 analogues produced bust from mid-cycle without identifiable late-cycle signals) suggests patience, but the basis trade channel introduces a non-linearity absent from prior episodes.

2. **Is the correlation regime shift permanent or transitory?** If AI TFP breakthrough materializes (15-25% probability, 5-15yr timeline), the regime shifts to disinflationary supply — flipping correlation negative and restoring 60/40 diversification. But if fiscal dominance persists alongside demand-pull AI capex without supply response, positive correlation could endure for a decade. The portfolio construction implications of getting this wrong are 200-400bp annualized.

3. **Can the Kalecki feedback loop unwind gradually or only discontinuously?** The fiscal deficit floor ($1.8-2T) suggests gradual, but the $40-60B/month international capital flow sensitivity creates potential non-linearity. The Japan/energy analogues (gradual) had no comparable international capital flow dependence. If foreign investors are the marginal price-setter for Mag-7, the loop could snap rather than fade.

4. **What triggers the transition from mid-1990s to late-1990s analogue phase?** The KB identifies specific falsification criteria: revenue/capex <0.2x, negative hyperscaler FCF, proliferation of zero-revenue AI startups at extreme valuations, LLM scaling diminishing returns. None are currently breached. Capex/OCF at 50-65% approaching the dual threshold (ROIC below WACC for 4+ quarters AND capex/OCF >55-65%) with 2-3 hyperscalers potentially crossing by late 2027 is the most monitorable leading indicator.

5. **Does the basis trade create a qualitatively different unwind mechanism than any prior tech cycle?** At $800B-$1T and 50-100x leverage, cross-margined with AI equity at multi-strategy funds, the coupling is unprecedented in scale. March 2020 validated the channel at half current scale. The SEC central clearing mandate (Q2 2026) introduces a reform-driven catalyst. Whether the Fed would intervene proactively given known vulnerability is an open institutional question with no historical precedent.

6. **Is the value-growth spread signal (5th-8th percentile post-1963, implying 400-700bp annualized over 5-7 years) actionable with AI-era structural breaks in factor composition?** The uniquely asymmetric quality of the current spread (cheap quintile is non-distressed financials/utilities/energy at 8-12x; expensive quintile is entirely AI narrative) strengthens the signal, but the 1999-2000 precedent shows spreads can widen further 12-18 months before reverting violently. Timing is unreliable; the 5-7 year holding period is the relevant frame.

7. **How should cross-asset portfolios hedge the TSMC concentration risk that no single instrument captures?** The conjunction of supply disruption + forced divestment ($300-500B+) + capex freeze (12-24+ months) is uncaptured by Taiwan CDS (30-40bp, implying ~2-3% probability vs analyst assessments of 5-8% cumulative over 5 years), SOX puts, or any individual hedge. A portfolio approach combining TWD/KRW vol, long physical commodity (copper, uranium), and short AI equity beta may approximate the multi-dimensional exposure.
