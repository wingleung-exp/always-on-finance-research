# AI and Tech Disruption — Central Bank Policy Transmission Strategist Analysis

## Key Claims

1. **Monetary policy transmission is impaired across all five channels simultaneously — a historically unprecedented condition.** The interest rate channel is bifurcated (85-90% of AI capex rate-insensitive), the credit channel is attenuated (fintech 25-30% of consumer origination), the asset price channel is narrowed (93% of equities held by top 10% with MPC of 0.02-0.05), the exchange rate channel is jammed (dollar sustained by capex flows orthogonal to rate differentials), and the expectations channel is degraded (Fed cannot guide on variables it cannot observe). No prior tightening or easing cycle has faced simultaneous impairment across all five channels.

2. **Quantitative tightening is being systematically offset by AI equity appreciation, creating a ~75-125bp FCI offset that the Fed does not explicitly model.** The GS FCI equity component has eased ~100-150bp from AI-driven cap-weighted equity appreciation since QT began, while QT has tightened the FCI by an estimated ~50-75bp through the reserves/credit channel. Net: QT is roughly half as effective as the Fed's models suggest. The Fed's quarterly FCI decompositions do not isolate the AI equity component, meaning staff forecasts systematically overstate the restrictiveness of current policy.

3. **The effective Taylor Rule coefficients are time-varying and currently unstable.** The standard Taylor Rule assumes stable coefficients on the output gap (~0.5) and inflation gap (~1.5). But when the output gap is unmeasurable (AI may be shifting potential output in real-time), the inflation gap is contaminated by supply-side ambiguity (AI-driven productivity gains vs. AI-driven energy demand), and r* has a 150bp uncertainty range (0.5-2.0%), the Taylor Rule produces a policy rate range of 3.0-5.5% — a 250bp band that renders the rule informationally empty. The Fed is flying without instruments.

4. **The term premium should be 30-60bp higher than current levels given AI-driven r* uncertainty, implying a mispricing in the 5Y-10Y sector.** The ACM and KW term premium models estimate ~0-30bp on 10Y Treasuries. But the SEP longer-run dot dispersion widening from ~50bp to ~125bp+ represents a direct proxy for r* uncertainty that should feed into higher term premia. The 10x range in expert AI productivity estimates (Acemoglu 0.06pp/yr vs. McKinsey 0.6pp/yr) maps to a 30-100bp r* range, which under standard affine term structure models should generate 30-60bp of additional term premium. The current compression is consistent with AI narrative-driven demand for duration as a hedge against the tech bust scenario, creating a self-referential premium suppression.

5. **Forward guidance credibility is approaching a structural inflection point.** The Fed's forward guidance framework assumes it can communicate a state-contingent reaction function to markets. But AI creates genuine Knightian uncertainty (not quantifiable risk) about potential output, NAIRU, and r* — the three variables that define the reaction function. The SEP longer-run GDP growth estimate remaining at 1.8-2.0% despite the largest AI investment boom in history is not conservatism; it is an institutional admission that the Fed cannot estimate AI's impact. Forward guidance that cannot specify the states it is contingent upon degrades from a commitment device to cheap talk. The market should and does discount it, as evidenced by OIS curve pricing diverging from dot-plot medians by 50-75bp at the 2-3Y horizon.

6. **The ECB-Fed-BOJ policy divergence driven by AI adoption asymmetry is creating a self-reinforcing FX-inflation feedback loop that constrains all three central banks.** AI capex/GDP of ~1.0% (US) vs. ~0.15-0.2% (eurozone) vs. ~0.1-0.3% (Japan) implies structural r* differentials that widen with AI adoption. EUR and JPY depreciation from AI capital outflows imports inflation, constraining ECB and BOJ easing capacity. This forces a higher-for-longer stance in economies that need easing for domestic demand, compounding the growth differential and amplifying capital outflows. The loop is visible: USD/JPY structurally above 150 despite BOJ rate hikes; EUR/USD failure to appreciate despite 100bp+ Fed cuts.

7. **The next-cycle easing toolkit is pre-degraded.** If an AI bust triggers a recession, the Fed faces: (a) rate cuts with impaired pass-through to the rate-insensitive AI capex channel, (b) QE operating through a portfolio balance channel contaminated by the same concentrated equity market that is unwinding, (c) forward guidance with no credible state-contingent commitment given r* uncertainty, and (d) a basis trade ($800B-$1T at 50-100x leverage) that could transmit equity stress into Treasury markets, potentially forcing the Fed to intervene in Treasuries while simultaneously trying to ease via Treasuries. The 1998-2001 precedent of 550bp easing is inapplicable because the transmission mechanism was intact then.

8. **The Fed's data-dependent approach creates a systematic policy error during the AI Solow paradox window (2026-2030).** BLS productivity data lags reality by 2-3 years. If AI is genuinely raising productivity but measurement hasn't captured it, data-dependent policy will over-tighten (holding rates too high relative to a rising r*). If AI is not raising productivity but asset prices reflect the expectation, data-dependent policy will under-tighten (tolerating an asset bubble because measured inflation and output appear well-behaved). Both errors are plausible; neither is detectable in real time. The policy error distribution is asymmetric: over-tightening produces a recession that is correctable; under-tightening produces a financial instability event that is not.

## Evidence & Reasoning

**Claim 1 (Multi-channel impairment):** Evidence synthesized from KB entries: credit_channel_bifurcation (200bp rate hike changes hyperscaler WACC by only 20-30bp), fci_distributional_masking (top 10% own 93% of equities with low MPC), dollar_overvaluation_ai_inflows (DXY 103-107 despite 100-150bp cuts), and fed_ai_r_star_trilemma (SEP longer-run GDP growth unchanged at 1.8-2.0%). The novelty is the *simultaneous* impairment — prior episodes typically saw 1-2 channels impaired while others compensated (e.g., 2008 credit channel impaired but exchange rate channel functional; 2015-2018 asset price channel strong but credit channel tight).

**Claim 2 (QT-FCI offset):** QT has reduced the Fed balance sheet by ~$1.5-2T since mid-2022. Standard estimates (Greenlaw et al. 2018, Bonis et al. 2017) suggest ~2-3bp per $100B of QT, implying ~30-60bp of tightening through the reserves channel. However, the S&P 500 AI-weighted appreciation over the same period has added ~100-150bp to the GS FCI equity component (which carries ~15-20% weight in the overall index). The net effect is systematically easier than QT alone would suggest, and the Fed's Tealbook decompositions have historically not isolated sector-specific equity contributions.

**Claim 3 (Taylor Rule instability):** Standard Taylor Rule: r = r* + π + 0.5(π - π*) + 0.5(y - y*). With r* = 0.5-2.0% (150bp range), π = 2.5-3.5% (100bp measurement ambiguity), and (y - y*) = -1.0% to +1.0% (200bp range given potential output uncertainty), the prescribed rate ranges from ~3.0% to ~5.5%. Current fed funds at 4.25-4.50% is inside the range but provides no signal about whether policy is tight or accommodative. This is the Taylor Rule equivalent of a dead instrument gauge.

**Claim 4 (Term premium mispricing):** The Adrian-Crump-Moench (ACM) model estimates 10Y term premium near 0-30bp. Kim-Wright produces similar estimates. But these models are estimated on historical data where r* uncertainty was ~50bp (the 2017-2019 SEP dispersion). Current r* uncertainty is ~125bp+ (SEP dispersion). Under the Cochrane (2015) affine term structure framework, doubling the variance of expected future short rates should mechanically increase the term premium by the variance increase × duration × risk aversion parameter. Back-of-envelope: 75bp additional r* variance × 5Y average maturity × 0.1 risk aversion = ~37bp additional term premium required — consistent with the 30-60bp claim.

**Claim 5 (Forward guidance degradation):** The Woodford (2012) and Campbell et al. (2012) framework distinguishes Delphic guidance (forecasts) from Odyssean guidance (commitments). Effective Odyssean guidance requires the central bank to specify the states under which the commitment changes. When the state space itself is uncertain (r*, potential output, NAIRU all have AI-contingent ranges), the commitment becomes vacuous because the contingencies are undefined. The 50-75bp OIS-dot divergence at 2-3Y is the market's revealed discount of this degradation — substantially wider than the 20-30bp divergence observed in 2017-2019 when forward guidance was credible.

**Claim 6 (FX-inflation feedback):** Japan provides the clearest case. BOJ hiked to 0.5% (highest since 2008) but USD/JPY remains above 150 because the rate differential is insufficient to overcome AI-driven capital outflows to US tech. The resulting yen weakness imports ~0.5-1.0pp of additional inflation via import prices, constraining the BOJ from pausing despite domestic demand weakness. The ECB faces the symmetric problem: EUR weakness from AI outflows imports inflation, but ECB easing is needed for domestic demand. Both central banks are trapped in a "wrong-way" monetary policy stance driven by AI adoption differential.

**Claim 7 (Pre-degraded easing toolkit):** The 2001 easing operated through an intact banking system with SLOOS showing normal pass-through, a functioning Treasury market (basis trade ~$100B vs. current $800B-$1T), and forward guidance with measurable state variables. The 2020 easing was into a deflationary shock with below-2% inflation providing unlimited easing runway. An AI bust with 2.5-3.5% inflation and impaired Treasury transmission has no historical analogue. The Fed's own Financial Stability Report identifies the basis trade as a vulnerability but does not address how it constrains easing capacity.

**Claim 8 (Solow paradox policy error):** The 1996-2000 analogue is instructive. The Fed allowed an asset bubble because measured inflation was low (productivity was genuinely rising). But the bubble still produced financial instability independent of the productivity reality. If current AI optimism is warranted (30-40% probability per rates_strategist_02's assessment), the Fed should be raising r* and signaling higher terminal rates. If unwarranted (60-70% probability), the Fed should be leaning against financial conditions. Data-dependence cannot resolve the question in real time because the data arrives with a 2-3 year lag.

## Confidence Assessment

| Claim | Confidence | Justification |
|-------|-----------|---------------|
| 1. Multi-channel impairment | 7/10 | Each individual channel impairment is well-evidenced in KB. The *simultaneous* impairment assertion is a synthesis, not independently verified. Historical comparison claims require broader survey. |
| 2. QT-FCI offset | 6/10 | Directionally robust. Specific magnitudes (75-125bp offset) are model-dependent and carry ±40% estimation error. GS FCI decomposition methodology is not public. |
| 3. Taylor Rule instability | 8/10 | Arithmetic claim — the input uncertainty ranges are well-documented in KB (SEP dispersion, r* estimates). The "informationally empty" conclusion follows directly. |
| 4. Term premium mispricing | 5/10 | The theoretical argument is sound, but term premium models have notoriously wide confidence intervals. The specific 30-60bp estimate could be off by 50%. Multiple determinants beyond r* uncertainty affect the term premium. |
| 5. Forward guidance degradation | 7/10 | The theoretical framework (Woodford, Campbell et al.) is well-established. The OIS-dot divergence is observable. Whether this represents forward guidance degradation specifically vs. other factors (policy uncertainty, political risk) is partially ambiguous. |
| 6. ECB-Fed-BOJ divergence loop | 7/10 | The mechanism is directly observable (USD/JPY > 150 despite BOJ hikes). Whether AI adoption asymmetry is the primary driver vs. other structural factors (demographics, fiscal) is debatable. |
| 7. Pre-degraded easing toolkit | 5/10 | Speculative by nature — describes a counterfactual scenario. The individual degradation mechanisms are well-evidenced but the compound effect is untested. The Fed's crisis response has historically been more creative than pre-crisis analysis anticipates. |
| 8. Solow paradox policy error | 7/10 | The measurement lag is factual (BLS confirmation 2-3 years). The asymmetric error distribution is a judgment call. The 1996-2000 analogue is instructive but n=1. |

## Connections to Other Topics

**To macro/fiscal policy:** The sovereign_ai_fiscal_path_dependency concept creates fiscal rigidity that constrains the fiscal side of the policy mix. If monetary transmission is impaired AND fiscal policy is locked in by AI industrial policy, the overall macro stabilization toolkit is degraded. The Kalecki profit channel links AI capex to aggregate profits, meaning monetary policy's impact on the real economy is partially mediated through its effect (or non-effect) on AI investment decisions.

**To equity markets:** The erp_compression and equity_market_concentration concepts are the transmission mechanism through which AI distorts the FCI's equity component. The sbc_reflexivity_loop amplifies the FCI distortion by inflating reported earnings, making financial conditions appear even easier than they are. Any correction in these dynamics would simultaneously tighten financial conditions (through the FCI equity component) and potentially trigger the basis_trade_equity_coupling, creating a two-front crisis for the Fed.

**To FX/international:** The dollar_overvaluation_ai_inflows and monetary_policy_divergence_usd_bid concepts are the exchange rate channel manifestation. The "capex dollar" operating orthogonally to rate differentials means the exchange rate channel of monetary policy is jammed — Fed cuts don't weaken the dollar because AI capital flows dominate rate differentials. This breaks the traditional external adjustment mechanism.

**To credit/financial stability:** The basis_trade_equity_coupling is the critical linkage between equity market AI concentration and Treasury market functioning. The $800B-$1T basis trade at 50-100x leverage is the mechanism through which an AI equity correction could become a rates crisis, directly impairing the Fed's primary policy transmission instrument (Treasuries).

**To labor markets:** The white_collar_displacement_novelty and segmented labor market create a Phillips curve that may be non-linear and sector-specific, further complicating monetary policy calibration. If AI displaces white-collar workers while blue-collar labor remains tight, aggregate measures (unemployment rate, average hourly earnings) send mixed signals.

## Open Questions

1. **Is the Fed privately modeling AI-specific FCI adjustments?** The staff has the capability; the question is whether the institutional framework allows incorporating unmeasurable structural shifts. Staff presentations at Jackson Hole and Brookings have skirted this issue.

2. **At what point does the multi-channel transmission impairment force a framework change rather than a parameter adjustment?** The 2020 FAIT adoption was driven by a specific problem (zero lower bound). Is multi-channel impairment a sufficiently well-defined problem to generate institutional action?

3. **Can the ECB-Fed-BOJ divergence loop be broken from the non-US side?** If Europe or Japan launch credible AI industrial policy, does the capital flow reversal destabilize the dollar enough to create a non-linear FX adjustment?

4. **What is the Fed's actual reaction function to AI equity concentration?** The Greenspan Fed tolerated NASDAQ appreciation until 2000; the Bernanke/Yellen Fed used "macroprudential" tools to avoid targeting asset prices. The Powell Fed has not been tested on this. Does the current FOMC have an implicit AI equity threshold?

5. **How does the SEC central clearing mandate for Treasuries (Q2 2026) interact with the basis trade vulnerability?** This is a calendar-identifiable catalyst that could either reduce systemic risk (by improving netting) or create transition risk (by forcing position unwinding during the implementation period). The interaction with the Fed's easing toolkit is unexplored.

6. **Is there a measurement of "effective monetary policy transmission" that would capture multi-channel impairment in real-time?** The Bank of England's "effective monetary conditions index" attempts something similar but does not account for AI-specific distortions. A composite metric tracking pass-through rates across all five channels would be analytically valuable.

7. **If AI productivity gains do eventually materialize (5-15 year timeline), does the transition from demand-driven to supply-driven AI effects restore or further impair transmission?** Supply-driven effects would theoretically operate through the traditional productivity-to-prices channel, but if the equity market has already priced them in, the asset price channel may be exhausted rather than restored.
