## REFUTED

**1. "75% historical base rate" for equity-implied HY divergence resolving via credit widening**
Refuted in: pair_0 debate | Originally: generalist_01 (Claim 2)
N=4 episodes. Wilson score 95% CI: [22%, 99%]. Presenting "75%" implies precision the sample cannot support. The directional claim (credit more likely to widen than not) is reasonable; the specific base rate is statistically meaningless. Reduced to a qualitative directional signal.

**2. "Correlation regime transition is 40-60% complete"**
Refuted in: pair_0 debate | Originally: generalist_01 (Claim 5)
No metric for measuring completion, no historical calibration for intermediate stages, no observable indicator distinguishing 40% from 70%. Pseudo-quantification of a qualitative judgment. The underlying insight (transition underway but unresolved) survives; the numeric precision does not.

**3. "The deficit is only unprecedented if you ignore the sectoral balance identity"**
Refuted in: pair_2 debate | Originally: macro_strategist_02 (Claim 1)
The sectoral balance identity holds in *every* period, including those with balanced budgets. It explains how the deficit is financed, not why it exists at this level. The identity is an accounting constraint, not a normalization device. The deficit contains 3-4pp endogenous/structural and 2-3pp discretionary components; the discretionary portion is what makes the configuration unprecedented.

**4. Japan at 250%+ debt/GDP is quantitatively transferable as a US sustainability analogue**
Refuted in: pair_2 debate | Originally: macro_strategist_02 (Claim 2)
Fails on three structural dimensions: (a) Japan runs persistent current account surpluses of 3-4% GDP; US runs deficits of ~3.5%. (b) >90% of JGBs held domestically; ~30% of USTs held by foreign investors with price-elastic demand. (c) Japan's deflation meant negative real yields; the US faces above-target inflation with positive real yields. Japan demonstrates that currency issuers have more space (correct in principle); it does not demonstrate the US has equivalent quantitative headroom.

**5. R² of 0.55-0.65 on Kalecki regressions is "just measurement error"**
Refuted in: pair_2 debate | Originally: macro_strategist_02 (Claim 4)
BEA measurement error for major aggregates is 0.5-1.0% of GDP — far too small to explain 35-45% unexplained variance. The gap reflects real phenomena: regressions on changes (not levels), for the corporate sector specifically (not the full economy), with inventory cycles, international profit shifting, and financial vs. nonfinancial divergence creating legitimate unexplained variance. macro_strategist_02's own 6/10 confidence and caveat about causal direction implicitly concede this.

**6. Specific dollar probability assignments (45% weakening / 35% range-bound / 20% strength)**
Refuted in: pair_2 debate | Originally: macro_strategist_01 (Claim 2)
Drawn from N=3 historical episodes that produced radically different outcomes under conditions acknowledged as dissimilar. Drawing probability distributions from N=3 is not analysis. macro_strategist_01's own honesty about "genuinely uncertain" direction (5/10 confidence) contradicts assigning three-way probabilities. The correct statement: Mundell-Fleming gives ambiguous predictions under the current policy mix.

**7. Accelerating Treasury dislocation frequency as a clean trend**
Partially refuted in: pair_0 debate | Originally: generalist_02 (Claim 6)
The sequence (LTCM → Lehman → Repo → COVID → UK LDI) conflates fundamentally different event types. Only three of five (LTCM, COVID basis trade, UK LDI) are primarily leveraged-position-driven Treasury dislocations. Including Lehman (banking crisis) and COVID (exogenous shock) inflates the pattern. The compression narrative is also sensitive to start-date selection. The *directional* observation (structural vulnerability is worsening) survives; the clean-acceleration-trend framing does not.

**8. Tariff revenue of $200-400B at static scoring**
Partially refuted in: pair_0 debate | Originally: generalist_01 (Claim 4)
Historical tariff revenue has consistently realized 60-70% of static projections (2018-19 precedent). Behavioral responses (trade diversion, demand destruction, retaliation) reduce the taxable base. Adjusted range: $120-280B. The directional point (tariffs are a fiscal revenue source with inflationary side effects) survives; the magnitude does not.

## KEY_DISAGREEMENTS

**1. Doom loop: central scenario vs. tail risk**
- rates_strategist_01, generalist_01: treat the doom loop as the operative framework, with the slow burn *already active*
- challenger_02: 5-15% probability over 5 years; conjunction of required failures degrades multiplicatively
- macro_strategist_01: 8-12% for disorderly adjustment, 35-45% for gradual fiscal dominance
- pair_3 verdict: challenger_02's probability discipline is stronger, but independence assumption likely overstates the case; correlated failures raise joint probability to 15-30%
- **Resolution needed:** A formal event tree with conditional (not independent) branching probabilities. The 2009-2024 period (unprecedented deficits without sovereign stress) is either evidence of stabilizer effectiveness or evidence of luck — this is the fundamental interpretive fork.

**2. Term premium: "elevated" vs. "normalized"**
- rates_strategist_01: elevated relative to fiscal supply requirements and post-2020 structural shift
- challenger_02: normalized to pre-GFC levels (100-150bp average), anchoring to QE-suppressed 2010-2021 baseline is the error
- pair_3 verdict: challenger_02 wins on framing (current levels are historically normal), rates_strategist_01 wins on mechanism (the *drivers* are structurally different — fiscal supply saturation vs. pre-GFC inflation uncertainty)
- **Resolution needed:** Decomposition of pre-GFC vs. current term premium into source components. The same level from different sources may carry different risk implications.

**3. Deficit causality: endogenous (sectoral balance) vs. discretionary**
- macro_strategist_02: endogenous to private sector saving desire and current account deficit
- macro_strategist_01: discretionary fiscal stance wildly misaligned with cycle phase
- pair_2 verdict: neither extreme is correct; ~3-4pp is endogenous/structural, ~2-3pp is traceable to specific legislation (TCJA, defense, entitlements)
- **Resolution needed:** Formal decomposition of the deficit into automatic stabilizer, structural, and discretionary components. This determines whether DOGE is fighting the tide or trimming excess.

**4. Kalecki channel: accounting identity vs. behavioral mechanism**
- macro_strategist_02: holds by construction; R² reflects measurement error
- macro_strategist_01: accounting identity "dressed up as causal mechanism"; GE crowding-out offsets are real
- challenger_02: precision is overstated; R² comparison across different models is invalid
- pair_2 verdict: the identity holds in levels; the *causal direction* and behavioral response to changes is the real question. QT potentially reactivating crowding-out (40-50% probability per macro_strategist_01) is the conditional to monitor.
- **Resolution needed:** Does post-2008 crowding-out attenuation reflect structural change (global savings glut, intangible capital) or QE-era artifact? The answer determines whether the Kalecki floor holds as QT progresses.

**5. Cross-asset resolution: meta-stable equilibrium with non-linear break vs. standard sequential repricing**
- generalist_01: self-reinforcing equilibrium persists until discrete catalyst breaks one link; resolution is simultaneous and non-linear
- generalist_02: standard 4-8 month lag sequence (rates → FX → credit → equity) with the US 2011 outlier complicating the pattern
- pair_0 verdict: "neither framework is clearly superior" — non-linear dynamics likely operate within a loosely sequential structure
- **Resolution needed:** Monitoring the 2s5s10s butterfly jointly with credit spreads and EM FX vol could distinguish between meta-stable equilibrium (all break at once) and sequential repricing (observable lag structure).

**6. Reserve currency exceptionalism: how much protection does it provide?**
- macro_strategist_02: currency issuers cannot face involuntary default; the constraint is inflation, not solvency
- challenger_02: the US 2009-2024 period is evidence of genuine structural resilience, not just luck
- generalist_02: US 2011 paradox (flight to quality *into* the downgraded asset) shows it works but may only delay, not prevent
- macro_strategist_01: reserve currency status reduces crisis probability from IMF base rate (35-40%) to 8-12%
- **Resolution needed:** Is reserve currency status a *level* shift in the fiscal limit (permanently higher capacity) or a *delay* mechanism (same ultimate constraint, longer fuse)?

**7. KB confidence calibration: should all extrapolation-dependent scores receive a systematic haircut?**
- challenger_02: the KB mandates a 2-3 point haircut for regime novelty but doesn't apply it; mechanically reducing every extrapolation-dependent score by 2 points is the most actionable recommendation
- Agents vary from 1-3 points in self-applied haircuts
- **Resolution needed:** Implement the mechanical reduction and observe how analytical conclusions change. If conclusions are robust to 2-point haircuts, confidence in the framework increases. If they collapse, the narrative was load-bearing and the evidence was not.
