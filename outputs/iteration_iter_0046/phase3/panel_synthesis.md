## REFUTED

**1. "Japan devastatingly falsifies the fiscal codetermination thesis."**
- **Originating agent:** challenger_01 (Claim 2, self-rated 7/10)
- **Refutation:** pair_3 identified the fatal confound: BOJ absorbed essentially all net JGB issuance through QQE, neutralizing the fiscal supply channel entirely. Japan demonstrates that fiscal supply pressure is neutralized when the central bank is the buyer of last resort — the US is in the opposite condition (QT). challenger_01's own Open Question #2 acknowledges this confound. Correct inference: fiscal supply matters *conditional on the central bank not absorbing it*. Revised confidence: 4/10.

**2. Passive investing extends repricing lag from 18-36 to 36-60 months.**
- **Originating agents:** equity_analyst_01 (Claim 7, self-rated 5/10), generalist_02 (Claim 5, self-rated 6/10)
- **Refutation:** pair_0 and pair_2 converged: "judgment, not calibrated from data." March 2020 (passive ~40% AUM) produced 34% peak-to-trough in 23 trading days — contradicting the extended-lag mechanism. Linear extrapolation from two incomparable data points (1967 at 95% active, 1998 at 85% active) across completely different market microstructures is pseudo-quantitative. The *direction* (passive extending lags somewhat) is plausible. The *specific doubling* is unsupported and creates dangerous complacency.

**3. 60/40 positive total returns prove the "death of 60/40" narrative is wrong.**
- **Originating agent:** challenger_01 (Claim 7)
- **Refutation:** pair_3 clarified this conflates total return with diversification benefit. 60/40 returned well because equities returned 20%+ in 2023-2024; the bond leg (AGG ~-1% to +2%) contributed negligibly or negatively. The "death of 60/40" thesis concerns the correlation-driven diversification benefit, not total returns. A portfolio carried entirely by the equity allocation is not evidence that the 40% bond hedge "works."

**4. The "optimal" cross-asset trade is long real assets vs. nominal duration.**
- **Originating agent:** generalist_01 (Claim 7, self-rated 7/10)
- **Refutation:** pair_0 identified the internal contradiction: gold at all-time highs despite rising real rates is "historically anomalous, creating entry-point risk" — acknowledged by generalist_01 itself. The asymmetry argument depends on probability weights that favor the regime (D at 55-60%) where the trade partially underperforms. The word "optimal" is not supported by the evidence provided.

**5. The cross-border carry complex totals "$2-3T."**
- **Originating agent:** fx_strategist_02 (Claim 8, self-rated 4/10)
- **Refutation:** pair_2 found an arithmetic error. fx_strategist_02's own EM carry components sum to $200-385B, not "$200-500B." Adding JPY carry ($500B-$1T) gives $700B-$1.5T total. The $3T upper bound is double the supported figure. Honest range: $700B-$1.5T. The contagion mechanism is sound; the headline number is inflated.

**6. 5Y5Y at 3.8-4.2% represents a "permanent" structural r-star repricing.**
- **Originating agent:** rates_strategist_01 (Claim 3, self-rated 7/10)
- **Refutation:** pair_3 identified circularity (using a yield-based measure to estimate a concept that determines yields). If 30-40bp of the elevated 5Y5Y is embedded term premium (plausible under rates_strategist_01's own framework), implied r-star drops to 1.3-1.6% — elevated but not the regime break claimed. "Elevated for the current fiscal cycle" is defensible; "permanent" is not.

**7. Recession base rate should be revised downward from 25-30%.**
- **Originating agent:** challenger_01 (Claim 9, self-rated 6/10)
- **Refutation:** pair_3 identified circular reasoning: the economy is resilient because of fiscal deficits, and fiscal deficits are sustainable because the economy is resilient. The Kalecki earnings buffer is itself contingent on sustained deficits. If deficit reduction becomes necessary, the buffer disappears when most needed. The 25-30% base rate over 18 months is reasonable and should not be revised on this basis.

**8. Semi-liquid private credit represents a systemic "breaking the buck" risk.**
- **Originating agent:** credit_analyst_02 (Claim 7, self-rated 5/10)
- **Refutation:** pair_1 identified the structural flaw: MMFs were cash equivalents embedded in corporate payment systems; semi-liquid PC vehicles are alternatives held by wealth management clients with explicit illiquidity disclosures. Behavioral dynamics are fundamentally different. The contagion via establishing marks is real but slower and more contained. The mark-based contagion pathway should be retained as a low-confidence monitoring item, not presented as systemic risk.

**9. CLO formation pace should be ranked "Tier 0.5" above IG spreads in the signpost hierarchy.**
- **Originating agent:** credit_analyst_02 (Claim 10, partial)
- **Refutation:** pair_1: n=2 evidence (one contaminated by sector-specific energy stress). Cannot rank above a signal with 6/7 hit rate across 13 transitions and 5x the backtest data. CLO formation is a useful *supplementary* indicator, not a superior one.

**10. The S&P 493 "likely exhibits" robustly positive correlation of +0.3 to +0.5 with rates.**
- **Originating agent:** generalist_01 (Claim 4, self-rated 5/10)
- **Refutation:** pair_0 flagged this as "resting on zero empirical verification" — the definitive empirical test has not been conducted. Presented as evidence for trade construction when it is an untested hypothesis. The directional claim (S&P 493 more positively correlated than cap-weighted index) is plausible but the specific range is fabricated. Retained as an empirical priority, not a finding.

## KEY_DISAGREEMENTS

**1. What does the maturity bifurcation *mean*?**
