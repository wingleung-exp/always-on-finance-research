## KEY_DISAGREEMENTS

**1. Dollar trajectory: structural weakening cycle vs. fiscal-supported resilience.**
fx_strategist_01 argues the dollar is in early-to-mid stages of a structural weakening cycle (historical 7-10 year phases). fx_strategist_02 argues US fiscal expansion (6-7% deficit in non-recession) structurally reshapes the Dollar Smile into an asymmetric "smirk" that supports dollar strength even in the middle zone. pair_2 debate found fx_strategist_02 stronger because fx_strategist_01 identifies the AI capex confound but then dismisses it without resolution. **Unresolved**: whether unprecedented fiscal deficits + AI capex capital inflows represent a structural break in the dollar cycle framework or a temporary extension of the right side of the smile.

**2. JPY carry unwind probability: what is the right conditional estimate?**
challenger_02 argues 0.5-2% (unconditional base rate from 150-200 BoJ decisions). Prior KB states 10-15% per action. pair_3 debate resolved this directionally to ~3-8% conditional on elevated positioning, but the range remains wide. **Unresolved**: defining the precise conditioning set (positioning levels, vol regime, credit cycle stage) and whether the BoJ's revealed circuit-breaker function (August 2024 pause) durably reduces tail severity.

**3. Where does the next FX crisis originate: G10 systemic (carry unwind → Treasury basis trade) or EM exporter (Dutch Disease → fiscal collapse)?**
fx_strategist_01/generalist_01 focus on the G10 transmission channel (JPY carry → $800B-$1T basis trade → UST illiquidity). fx_strategist_02 argues the next crisis originates with a commodity exporter. pair_2 debate found these complementary but neither agent integrates both: an EM exporter crisis could trigger JPY carry unwind, which feeds into the Treasury-FX fragility corridor. **Unresolved**: the concatenated scenario and its joint probability.

**4. Dollar Smile: salvageable with recalibration or fundamentally broken?**
rates_strategist_02 recalibrates (left tail impaired by fiscal dominance, right tail by growth convergence, DXY 95-108). challenger_02 argues the framework is unfalsifiable narrative construction. pair_3 debate found: "B is stronger on the meta-level critique; A is more useful operationally." **Unresolved**: whether any regime identification rule can be specified that generates testable predictions with >60% hit rate on 1-year dollar direction. challenger_02 sets this as the falsification bar.

**5. FX vol suppression: genuine anomaly or reference period artifact?**
Multiple agents (generalist_01, fx_strategist_02) treat the MOVE-FX vol divergence as a genuine anomaly signaling imminent repricing. challenger_02 demonstrates it is partially a reference period artifact — against a 1973-2007 baseline, current vol is normal. **Unresolved**: the correct reference distribution for vol anomaly detection. challenger_02 identifies this as "the single most important unresolved methodological issue."

**6. Size and structure of the JPY carry ecosystem.**
equity_analyst_01 cites $300-500B (BIS FX-funded cross-border positions). equity_analyst_02/fx_strategist_01 cite $4-6T (total notional including all JPY-funded lending, corporate treasury, derivatives). pair_1 debate concluded the $4-6T figure "conflates total JPY-denominated cross-border lending with carry trade positioning." **Unresolved**: what fraction of the $4-6T is actually unhedged speculative exposure vs. naturally hedged trade/interbank lending.
