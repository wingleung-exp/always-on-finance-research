## REFUTED CLAIMS

**1. Agent A, Claim 3: "FX regime transitions [are] more consequential than equity bear markets or credit widenings."**

The 2008 GFC was a credit-led event where multi-asset portfolio losses were driven primarily by credit contagion and equity collapse, not the accompanying dollar cycle. The 2020 COVID crash was equity-led. Agent A's evidence (three major FX regime transitions since 2000) shows that FX transitions *coincide with* correlation regime shifts, not that they *dominate* them. The Gopinath dominant currency channel is real but it doesn't establish FX transitions as the *most consequential* event type — it establishes them as the *most broadly transmitted*. These are different claims.

**3. Agent B, Claim 5: 60/40 EUR strength skew.**
Agent B's own analysis undermines this. The fundamental case is explicitly described as "ambiguous" — rate differentials favor USD, valuation/current account favor EUR. Agent B then assigns a 60/40 EUR strength skew without justification for the directional lean. **4. Agent A, Claim 6: "$200-400B missing demand over a 6-month stress period."**
This number lacks derivation. **5. Agent B, Claim 3: "Remarkably consistent secular trend" of 0.5-0.7pp/year USD reserve decline.**
Agent B's own numbers show acceleration: 71% to 65% over 15 years (2000-2015) = 0.4pp/year, then 65% to 58% over 10 years (2015-2025) = 0.7pp/year. If the fiscal dominance bifurcation is real (even at <15% probability), then the carry unwind loop in Claim 5 has a branching path that isn't modeled. The August 2024 unwind happened *without* fiscal stress, so the loop held — but the next one might not, and that scenario is absent.
