## REFUTED

**R1. "August 2024 proved the system's resilience" (challenger_01, Claim 3).**
pair_3 refutation: The BoJ *capitulated* — officials signaled no further hikes within days. This is a circuit-breaker functioning, not resilience. If the stress test only "passes" because the examiner intervenes, you haven't passed. The episode demonstrates BoJ willingness to intervene, not systemic robustness.

**R2. Agricultural-FX doom loop "supersedes interest rate differentials as a crisis predictor" (commodities_analyst_02, Claim 1).**
pair_1 refutation: Turkey's crisis was driven by unorthodox monetary policy first, food prices second. Argentina's crises are fiscal/institutional. The 2013 Taper Tantrum hit EMs by rate differential expectations regardless of food CPI weight. The doom loop is *a* predictor, not *the* predictor that supersedes rates. No comparative predictive accuracy test was provided.

**R3. Export ban contagion amplifies FX impact by "30-60%" with ">60% conditional probability" (commodities_analyst_02, Claim 5).**
pair_1 refutation: Three episodes with heterogeneous triggers, responses, and outcomes cannot generate a reliable confidence interval. The game-theoretic mechanism is sound; the specific quantification is false precision from n=3.

**R4. Regime-conditional earnings quality is a "direct analogue" to carry-to-vol (equity_analyst_02, Claim 5).**
pair_1 refutation: Agent explicitly acknowledged "I lack sector-level data linking vol regimes to accruals quality directly." Borrowing the Chow test F-statistic from rate-FX R-squared to lend credibility to an untested earnings quality hypothesis is a methodological red flag.

**R5. AI capex flows constitute a "structural break" sustainable for 3-5 years, supported by the dot-com parallel (challenger_01, Claim 1).**
pair_3 partial refutation: The dot-com parallel cited as evidence of persistence *ended in one of the sharpest dollar corrections in modern history*. Calling a pattern a "structural break" when the best analogue terminated in a crash is incoherent. The AI capex premium CI (3-12 cents EUR/USD) is too wide to be actionable. The flows are real; the "structural break" label is not earned.

**R6. The dollar is in a bimodal distribution with the muddled middle having lower probability than either tail (generalist_01, Claim 2).**
pair_0 refutation: The supporting Kalecki feedback loop argument is theoretical, not empirical. DXY ranged 95-99 for 22 months in 2018-19 under weaker fiscal conditions. generalist_01's own probability split (55%/35%/10% from iter_0037) contradicts strict bimodality — 55% "plateau-then-correction" implies an extended middle. Agent self-conceded: "bimodality is a structural inference, not a measured distribution."

**R7. EM crisis archetype is losing predictive power via Goodhart's Law (challenger_01, Claim 7).**
pair_3 refutation: Recent evidence directly contradicts — Turkey 2021-23, Sri Lanka 2022, Pakistan 2022-23, Egypt 2023-24. EM crises keep happening despite the supposedly reflexive learning. No evidence provided of rising false positive rates or falling hit rates.

**R8. PBOC "will defend food price stability over export competitiveness" (commodities_analyst_02, Claim 7).**
pair_1 refutation: China has extensive buffers — strategic grain reserves (50-65% of global wheat stocks), administered pricing, fiscal subsidies. The 2015-16 CNY depreciation occurred alongside elevated pork prices without PBOC prioritizing food stability. Agriculture is an input to PBOC decisions, not the binding constraint.

**R9. Basis trade is "8-10x larger than LTCM" (generalist_01, Claim 8).**
pair_0 refutation: Conflates a single highly-leveraged fund with a distributed market-wide position held by hundreds of participants with different risk management, correlation structures, and unwind timelines. The alarm is directionally warranted but the comparison inflates perceived severity.

**R10. Dollar cycle "median peak-to-trough of 7-9 years" as a predictive framework (generalist_02, Claim 2).**
pair_0 refutation: With n≤4 complete post-Bretton Woods cycles, the confidence interval includes essentially any outcome. Agent acknowledged "small sample" but then used the framework as though it had predictive power. The structural breaks identified (technology flows, AI capex) are sufficient to invalidate cyclical extrapolation.

**R11. fx_strategist_01's specific trade expression P&L estimates (+100-250bp, Sharpe 0.4-0.7).**
pair_2 refutation: Scenario probabilities are purely judgmental. Shifting 5% probability between scenarios changes expected value by 60-80bp. The scenario framework is useful as a thinking tool; the numerical precision is false. Agent self-rated confidence at 5/10.

## KEY_DISAGREEMENTS

**D1. The correct reference period for FX vol anomaly detection.**
This is the single most important unresolved methodological question. Against 1973-2007: current G10 FX vol (7-9%) is 35th-40th percentile — unremarkable. Against 2010-2024: anomalously low. The entire compressed spring thesis, gamma positioning rationale, and MOVE-FX divergence trade all depend on this baseline choice. challenger_01 frames this as structural; rates_strategist_02 and generalist_01 default to the shorter baseline. pair_3: "The honest answer is that neither agent can resolve this." The question is structural, not statistical: have FX markets permanently changed?

**D2. What is the primary driver of the dollar?**
rates_strategist_02: Policy divergence (Fed-BoJ ~450bp, Fed-ECB ~125-175bp). challenger_01: AI capex flows ($40-60B/month) have displaced rate differentials. fx_strategist_01: Three-factor model (rates, valuation, capital flows) with regime-conditional weights. pair_3 judged "neither convincing as stated" on exclusivity — ECB-Fed narrowing of ~100bp+ produced minimal EUR/USD response (contradicting rate primacy), while the dot-com parallel undermines AI flow persistence. The honest assessment: multiple factors operate simultaneously in a regime where no single driver dominates, and agents over-index on their pet channel.

**D3. JPY carry unwind severity — systemic or contained?**
rates_strategist_02/generalist_01: Systemic vulnerability, BoJ transmission paradox, $4-6T exposure. challenger_01: Overstated by 2-3x; speculative exposure is $300-500B, August 2024 was a passed test with BoJ circuit-breaker. The debate resolution: challenger_01 wins on flow decomposition (speculative vs. hedged) but rates_strategist_02 wins on mechanism (transmission paradox). pair_3 refuted the "resilience" framing. Net: the mechanism is validated but the severity depends on credit cycle position at the time of unwind and whether the BoJ circuit-breaker is durable — both unknowable ex ante.

**D4. Dollar resolution probability distribution: broadly distributed vs. skewed.**
generalist_02: No single scenario exceeds 35% probability. generalist_01: Bimodal (refuted as stated, but the underlying observation of negative skew may be directionally correct). fx_strategist_01: "Late-cycle transitional with asymmetric downside" — mean -3 to -7%, tail -12 to -18%, modal outcome range-bound. The structural arguments for negative skew (BEER overvaluation, twin deficits, concentrated long-dollar positioning) are sound, but the magnitude and timing of any resolution remain genuinely uncertain. The 2018-19 muddled middle (22 months of range-trading) cautions against assuming imminent resolution.

**D5. Is the fiscal dollar smile a durable support or a time bomb?**
fx_strategist_01: Capital inflow + growth channels support dollar, but self-undermining at high deficit levels. generalist_01: Time-inconsistent — near-term support, medium-term headwind. challenger_01: Unfalsifiable addition to an already unfalsifiable framework. The fiscal deficit at 6-7% in non-recession has zero post-WWII precedent, making all probability estimates out-of-sample. The key unresolved question: does the capital inflow channel (foreign investors funding the deficit) become fragile as debt/GDP rises, and if so, at what threshold?
