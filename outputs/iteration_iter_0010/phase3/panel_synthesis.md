**1. Government debt-to-GDP ratios are "irrelevant" to rates vol for currency-issuing sovereigns (macro_strategist_02, Claim 8, self-assessed 8/10).**
**Killed in:** Pair_2 debate.

The claim correctly identifies that default risk is near-zero for currency issuers, but conflates this with the stronger claim that fiscal trajectory doesn't affect inflation expectations and term premia — which are the actual rates vol drivers. The partial insight (distinguish currency issuers from users for default risk) survives; the headline claim ("irrelevant") does not.

**6. Vol transmission sequencing at 70% unconditional reliability (generalist_02, Claim 5).**

## KEY_DISAGREEMENTS

**1. Is the MOVE-VIX divergence a permanent structural feature or a transitional state that must resolve?**
- **Structural:** challenger_01 argues the post-2022 regime represents a permanent break in the MOVE-VIX relationship driven by inflation regime change, end of effective forward guidance, and secular Treasury supply increase.
- **Transitional:** generalist_02 shows 15-20% historical probability of persistence beyond 18 months (5/6 episodes converged within 12 months). generalist_01 and rates_strategist_01 lean toward eventual resolution via VIX repricing higher.
- **Resolution needed:** A formal structural break test on the MOVE-VIX relationship is required. If the Kalecki fiscal channel persists (no fiscal consolidation), the structural case strengthens. If fiscal consolidation or a macro shock occurs, the transitional case resolves.

**2. What is the primary driver of vol regimes — output gap uncertainty or private credit dynamics?**
- **NK camp (macro_strategist_01):** Output gap, inflation gap, and Taylor Rule stance determine vol; VIX-output gap correlation is robust; VRP predicts economic activity.
- **Post-Keynesian camp (macro_strategist_02):** Minsky cycle position determines vol; credit impulse and sectoral balances are the binding constraints; the output gap is derivative of the credit cycle.
- **Pair_2 verdict:** "Agent B's framework is more explanatory at cycle turning points; Agent A's is more operational at higher frequencies." The ideal synthesis grafts Minsky cycle classification onto NK machinery — using credit dynamics to identify *which* low-vol regime you're in, then using the NK framework for pricing within that regime. Neither framework is complete alone.

**3. Is monetary policy exogenous (an independent vol driver) or endogenous (a response to financial fragility)?**
- **Exogenous:** macro_strategist_01 documents reaction function uncertainty as a distinct vol source (Bianchi 2012); Fed pivots are independent catalysts.
- **Endogenous:** macro_strategist_02 argues policy responds to the Minsky fragility it previously created; there's no independent "policy surprise" channel separate from the credit cycle.
- **Unresolved:** Historical examples exist for both — genuine discretionary surprises (ECB "whatever it takes," BOJ YCC) and Minsky-forced responses (2008 emergency cuts, 2020 BTFP). The truth is likely state-dependent: in normal times, reaction function uncertainty is additive; near Minsky moments, policy becomes endogenous. The distinction matters for whether "long rates vol" is a pure macro-uncertainty bet or a credit-cycle bet.

**4. Can regime transitions skip intermediate states?**
- **Sequential:** generalist_01 and the risk appetite regime framework (iter_0001, confidence 9) argue regimes must be traversed sequentially, providing time to adjust positioning.
- **Non-sequential:** challenger_01 (8/10) cites Feb 2018, March 2020, October 2023 as near-instantaneous transitions. Pair_3 debate: "Agent B wins this one clearly."
- **Resolution:** challenger_01's distinction between fundamentally-driven transitions (sequential holds) and microstructure-driven transitions (can skip states) is the most productive framing. The practical implication: the "time to adjust" assumed by sequential models may not exist when the trigger is a positioning unwind or gamma flip rather than fundamental deterioration. This directly affects hedge timing.

**5. What is the "correct" floor for MOVE in the current structural environment?**
