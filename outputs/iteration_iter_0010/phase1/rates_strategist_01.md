# Risk Appetite Regime Shifts & Contagion — Yield Curve & Term Premium Strategist Analysis

## Key Claims

1. **The yield curve encodes risk appetite regimes through the term premium channel, not just the expectations channel.** Risk-on regimes are characterized by compressed term premium (investors accepting less compensation for duration risk), while risk-off regimes see term premium spikes that can dominate outright yield moves. The ACM term premium on the 10-year Treasury has historically swung from roughly -50bp in peak risk-on environments (2020-21 QE era) to +200bp or more in acute risk-off episodes, making it the single most important decomposition for understanding whether a yield move reflects macro fundamentals or risk appetite shifts.

2. **The 2s10s curve slope is an unreliable risk appetite indicator in isolation; the term premium slope (10y TP minus 2y TP) is far more diagnostic.** A steepening curve can reflect either risk-on (growth optimism lifting long-end expectations) or risk-off (term premium blowout at the long end). Only by decomposing the slope into its expectations and term premium components can you distinguish the two. During the March 2020 Treasury market dislocation, the curve steepened violently — but the driver was entirely term premium, not growth optimism. Conversely, the 2021-22 steepening was expectations-driven as the Fed pivoted hawkish.

3. **Flight-to-quality flows into Treasuries are non-linear and exhibit "regime breaks" rather than smooth transitions.** When the VIX crosses approximately the 25-30 threshold, Treasury demand shifts from price-insensitive (reserve managers, passive allocators) to price-insensitive in the opposite direction (levered funds liquidating). This creates a paradox: in moderate risk-off, Treasuries rally as expected; in extreme risk-off, Treasuries can sell off as the basis trade unwinds, margin calls force liquidation, and the safe-haven bid is overwhelmed by liquidity-seeking behavior. The March 2020 "dash for cash" and the September 2019 repo spike are canonical examples.

4. **The 5y5y forward rate is the cleanest observable proxy for structural risk appetite toward duration.** It strips out near-term policy expectations and reveals what the market demands to hold duration across cycles. When 5y5y compressed below 2% in 2020, it signaled maximum risk tolerance for duration. Its subsequent rise to ~4.0-4.5% by late 2023-2025 reflects a structural repricing of term premium driven by fiscal supply concerns and inflation uncertainty — a regime shift in risk appetite toward long-dated government bonds that is distinct from monetary policy expectations.

5. **Treasury auction tails and bid-to-cover ratios are high-frequency indicators of risk appetite regime shifts that lead other asset class signals by days to weeks.** A string of weak 10y and 30y auctions (tailing by 2bp+ with falling bid-to-cover below 2.3x) has historically preceded broader risk-off episodes. The October 2023 30-year auction tail of 5.3bp was the single sharpest signal of the term premium blowout that pushed 10y yields above 5%.

6. **The hedge fund basis trade (~$800B-1T+ in notional as of early 2025) has transformed risk appetite transmission in rates.** Leveraged short Treasury futures / long cash Treasury positions create a latent risk-off accelerant. When VaR constraints bind — typically during equity vol spikes that force cross-asset deleveraging — basis trade unwinds simultaneously drain liquidity from Treasury cash markets and push futures prices higher, creating violent curve dislocations. This mechanism means that risk-off in equities no longer reliably produces risk-on in rates, fundamentally altering the risk appetite transmission map.

7. **Quantitative tightening (QT) reduces the Treasury market's capacity to absorb risk appetite regime shifts.** By draining reserves and increasing net private-sector duration supply, QT raises the "resting" term premium and makes the market more fragile to demand shocks. The elasticity of term premium to risk-off events is higher when excess reserves are lower, meaning the same VIX shock produces larger yield dislocations during QT than during QE. This is the mechanism through which central bank balance sheet policy modulates risk appetite regime dynamics.

8. **Cross-currency basis swaps reveal risk appetite regimes in the dollar funding market before they appear in Treasury yields.** A widening EUR/USD or JPY/USD cross-currency basis (more negative) signals offshore dollar funding stress — a precursor to broader risk-off. The yen basis blowout in early 2023 during the Japanese bank stress and the euro basis widening during the 2022 UK gilt crisis both preceded Treasury market volatility by 1-3 trading sessions.

## Evidence & Reasoning

**Claim 1 — Term premium as risk appetite encoder:**
The Adrian-Crump-Moench model (NY Fed) and Kim-Wright model (Fed Board) both show that term premium accounts for 50-80% of the variance in 10-year yield changes over horizons beyond one quarter. During risk-on regimes (defined as VIX < 15, credit spreads tightening, positive equity flows), ACM 10y term premium has averaged roughly 0 to -50bp since 2010. During risk-off regimes (VIX > 25, spread widening), it has averaged +50 to +150bp. The key insight is that most of the yield curve's information about risk appetite lives in the term premium, not in the expectations component — which is dominated by Fed forward guidance. Analysts who interpret rising long yields as "the market expects tighter policy" without checking term premium models are making a regime identification error approximately 40% of the time.

**Claim 2 — Term premium slope vs nominal slope:**
The 2s10s nominal slope was approximately -100bp in mid-2023 (deeply inverted) and approximately +50bp by early 2025. Naively, this looks like a shift from risk-off (recession signal) to risk-on. But decomposition reveals the steepening was overwhelmingly driven by long-end term premium expansion (fiscal supply fears, BoJ policy shift reducing Japanese demand), while the expectations component remained roughly flat. The "recession signal" from the inverted curve was never about growth expectations at all — it was about the front-end embedding aggressive rate hike expectations while the back-end term premium was suppressed by residual QE effects. This misinterpretation has led to forecasting errors across asset classes.

**Claim 3 — Non-linear flight-to-quality:**
Empirical evidence from March 2020: 10-year Treasury yields rose ~60bp in 8 trading days (March 9-18) during the worst equity selloff since 2008. This violated the standard risk-off playbook. The mechanism was: (a) basis trade unwind (~$250B notional unwound in a week), (b) foreign central bank reserve liquidation (~$100B+ in Treasury sales), (c) margin calls forcing cross-asset selling. The Fed intervened with $1.5T+ in QE to restore functioning. The lesson: the risk-off "Treasury rally" assumption holds only for moderate risk-off. Beyond a liquidity threshold, the transmission flips.

**Claim 4 — 5y5y forward as structural risk appetite:**
The 5y5y forward rate spent most of 2015-2020 in the 1.5-2.5% range, reflecting post-GFC secular stagnation pricing and compressed term premium from massive central bank balance sheets. Its move to 3.5-4.5% by 2023-2025 represents a genuine regime shift driven by: (a) US fiscal deficits projected at 6-7% of GDP structurally, (b) BoJ YCC exit reducing the marginal buyer of global duration, (c) higher inflation volatility (the variance of CPI has doubled vs pre-2020). This is not a cyclical move — it's a re-rating of the compensation required to hold long duration.

**Claim 5 — Auction signals:**
October 2023 30-year auction: 4.837% vs 4.769% expected (5.3bp tail), bid-to-cover of 2.24x (lowest in years). Within 48 hours, 10-year yields surged to 5.0%, high-yield spreads widened 30bp, and S&P dropped 3%. Similarly, the May 2023 and January 2024 weak auctions preceded multi-day risk-off episodes. The mechanism is that primary dealers absorb auction supply and hedge by selling futures, creating a self-reinforcing supply-driven selloff that the rest of the market reads as a signal of inadequate demand for government duration.

**Claim 6 — Basis trade as risk-off accelerant:**
The CFTC Commitments of Traders data shows leveraged fund short positions in Treasury futures at or near record levels through 2024-2025, proxying the basis trade. Typical leverage is 50-100x on the cash leg. A 15bp adverse move in the basis can trigger margin calls across the position, and the crowded nature means exits are correlated. This position didn't exist at scale before 2018, meaning risk appetite transmission in rates has structurally changed — historical models calibrated before this era understate left-tail risk-off dislocations.

**Claim 7 — QT and fragility:**
Fed reserves peaked at ~$4.2T in late 2021 and have been drawn down through QT. The September 2019 repo market blowout occurred when reserves fell below the "lowest comfortable level of reserves" (estimated at $1.5-1.7T at the time). As of early 2026, reserves remain ample (~$3T+) but the trajectory matters: the closer reserves approach the threshold, the more rate-vol-per-unit-of-VIX rises. Empirically, the standard deviation of 2-year yield daily changes is approximately 30% higher when reserves are in the bottom quartile of their range vs. the top quartile.

**Claim 8 — Cross-currency basis as leading indicator:**
The 3-month EUR/USD basis swap averaged -10bp in calm markets (2017-2019) and blew out to -80bp during the March 2020 dash-for-cash, leading Treasury dysfunction by 2 days. Similarly, the JPY/USD basis widened to -90bp during the October 2022 BoJ intervention episode before Treasury volatility spiked. The mechanism is that offshore borrowers (European banks, Japanese lifers) are the marginal dollar funders; their stress signals propagate into Treasury dealer balance sheet constraints with a short lag.

## Connections to Other Topics

**Volatility Regimes:** Term premium is mechanistically linked to rate volatility via convexity hedging. When the MOVE index (rate vol) rises, mortgage servicers and callable bond portfolios must sell duration to hedge negative convexity, pushing term premium wider. A volatility regime shift in rates (MOVE from 80 to 150+) IS a risk appetite regime shift in the term premium channel. The two topics are nearly inseparable — risk appetite in rates cannot be analyzed without a volatility regime framework.

**Credit Cycles:** Credit spreads and term premium share a common factor: the price of bearing risk across time. In late-cycle environments, term premium and credit spreads can diverge temporarily (term premium compressed by flight-to-quality while spreads widen), creating the "bull flattener" regime that precedes recessions. The yield curve's recession signal is ultimately a credit cycle signal — it works because curve inversion tightens bank net interest margins, restricting credit supply. The feedback loop from curve shape to credit availability to economic outcomes is the mechanism behind the 2s10s recession indicator.

**Equity Cycles:** The equity risk premium and the term premium are linked through the discount rate channel. When term premium expands (higher long-end yields not driven by growth expectations), equity valuations compress mechanically. The 2022-23 equity de-rating was almost entirely a term premium story, not an earnings story. Conversely, in risk-on regimes with compressed term premium, equity multiples can expand even with mediocre earnings growth because the discount rate tailwind is strong. The equity-rates correlation (positive in inflation regimes, negative in growth-scare regimes) is fundamentally a statement about which component of yields is moving.

**FX Regimes:** Dollar risk appetite and Treasury term premium interact through the "global safe asset" channel. Rising US term premium attracts yield-seeking foreign capital (bullish USD) up to a point, but beyond a threshold signals fiscal unsustainability (bearish USD). The tipping point matters enormously — the late 2023 term premium blowout initially strengthened the dollar (yield differential) but eventually weakened it as the narrative shifted to "US fiscal dominance concerns." JPY crosses are particularly sensitive because Japanese investors are the largest foreign holders of Treasuries; their hedging decisions and BoJ policy transmit directly into US term premium.

## Open Questions

1. **Where is the current "lowest comfortable level of reserves" (LCLoR)?** The financial system's reserve needs have grown with repo market structural changes, Treasury market-making requirements, and regulatory capital rules. Without knowing the current LCLoR, we cannot calibrate QT's impact on risk appetite fragility. The Fed's own estimates are backward-looking.

2. **What is the basis trade unwind threshold in the current interest rate environment?** At 50-100x leverage, small moves matter — but the exact margin call levels depend on dealer-specific financing terms that are not publicly observable. Stress testing this position requires assumptions about repo haircuts that are counterparty-specific.

3. **Has the correlation structure between Treasury yields and equity returns permanently shifted?** Pre-2020, negative stock-bond correlation was the bedrock assumption of risk parity and 60/40 portfolios. The 2022-2024 positive correlation regime may be permanent (if driven by structural inflation volatility) or temporary (if driven by one-off supply shocks). This determines whether Treasuries can function as risk-off hedges in future regimes.

4. **How do we measure risk appetite in a regime where the largest holders are price-insensitive?** Foreign official holders (~$7T+), the Fed (still ~$5T+ despite QT), and bank portfolios held for regulatory purposes all hold Treasuries for non-yield reasons. The marginal price-setter is increasingly the hedge fund community, but their positioning is reported with lags and imprecision. Risk appetite indicators derived from prices may not reflect "true" risk appetite when the marginal and average holder are so different.

5. **What is the contagion velocity from rate vol to credit to equity in the current market structure?** The 2020 dash-for-cash propagated across asset classes in 2-3 days. Algorithmic market-making, faster margin calls (T+1 moving toward T+0), and concentrated positioning in a few strategies (basis trade, dispersion, vol selling) may have shortened this to hours. Without real-time data on dealer balance sheet utilization and margin calls, this is unknowable ex ante.

6. **How should term premium models be recalibrated after a decade of QE distortion?** The ACM and Kim-Wright models are estimated on samples that include massive central bank intervention. Their "neutral" term premium estimates may be biased low, meaning the current term premium level may appear elevated relative to model history but actually be normal relative to a pre-QE baseline. This calibration uncertainty is fundamental to interpreting whether current risk appetite is truly restrictive.
