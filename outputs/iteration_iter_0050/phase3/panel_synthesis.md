## KEY_DISAGREEMENTS

**1. Will the maturity wall produce systemic stress or be defused for the fifth time?**
- **Bear case (credit_analyst_01, credit_analyst_02):** CLO reinvestment expiration removes the mechanism that historically defused walls. B-rated refinancing at 40-60% higher costs compresses coverage to 1.2-1.5x. Three-way CLO squeeze is unprecedented.
- **Bull case (challenger_01):** 0-for-4 base rate. "This time is different" is the most dangerous consensus error pattern. Fiscal buffers, demonstrated central bank backstops, and market adaptive capacity provide multiple defuse mechanisms.
- **Resolution path:** Monitor CLO reset activity through Q2 2026. If SOFR declines 100-150bp, reset economics restore and the wall is likely defused. If SOFR holds and CLO formation remains below 15% hurdle rates, the bear case strengthens materially. Also monitor CCC bucket utilization — the difference between 4% and 6.5% toward the 7.5% threshold is the most important real-time structured credit variable.

**2. Is the credit cycle "late" in any actionable sense, given the fiscal buffer?**
- **Late-cycle camp (credit_analyst_01, credit_analyst_02):** Leverage, issuance quality, covenant erosion, and PIK usage all confirm late-cycle conditions. The fiscal buffer delays but doesn't prevent the turn.
- **Cycle-extended camp (challenger_01, fx_strategist_01):** The fiscal buffer may suppress defaults for 3-5 additional years. "Late-cycle" dating without a timebound disconfirmation criterion has no informational content. The carry cost of being early on the short side (200-300bp/year) may be the dominant risk.
- **Resolution path:** Specify falsification criteria. Proposed: if fiscal deficits remain above 5% GDP AND trailing 12-month leveraged credit defaults (addback-adjusted) remain below 4% through December 2027, the late-cycle thesis should be reclassified as "extended plateau." If deficits narrow below 4.5% AND defaults rise above 4%, the late-cycle thesis is confirmed.

**3. Is composition improvement a sufficient explanation for compressed spreads?**
- **Sufficient camp (challenger_01):** BB 38%→52% mechanically explains 70-130bp of compression. Residual compression may fall within estimation error.
- **Insufficient camp (credit_analyst_01, generalist_01):** Addback adjustment shifts many BB issuers to B-equivalent leverage. Residual risk premium is in the bottom decile of post-2000 distribution. Composition improvement is real but doesn't close the gap.
- **Resolution path:** Issuer-level analysis comparing addback-adjusted leverage to rating-implied leverage for a representative BB sample. If >20% of BB issuers carry B-equivalent true leverage, composition improvement is partially illusory. If <10%, the challenger case strengthens.

**4. How does the term premium / credit premium divergence resolve?**
- **Credit widening (credit_analyst_01, generalist_01):** 2 of 3 historical precedents resolved via credit repricing. Factor crowding reversal is the mechanism.
- **Rational coexistence (rates_strategist_01, rates_strategist_02):** Different transmission speeds — term premium reprices supply in real-time, credit reprices defaults with 12-24 month lag. Can coexist for years in fiscal-dominated regime.
- **FX-mediated (fx_strategist_01):** Converges when dollar weakness forces external constraint to bind on domestic profit channel.
- **Resolution path:** Monitor whether term premium reaches the 175-225bp range where financial conditions tightening overwhelms Kalecki profit support. Below that range, rational coexistence continues.

**5. What is the actual carry cost of the bearish credit trade, and does it dominate the risk/reward?**
