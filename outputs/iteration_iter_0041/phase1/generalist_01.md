# Central Bank Balance Sheets — Cross-Asset Strategist (Generalist) Analysis

## Key Claims

1. **Cross-asset markets are telling an incoherent macro story about QT's terminal impact.** Equity vol (VIX ~14-16) and credit spreads (IG ~90-110bp, HY ~300-350bp) price a benign outcome, while the rates market — via term premium restoration (~100bp from trough), curve steepening, and auction tail volatility — prices meaningful tightening. One market is wrong. The resolution determines the dominant trade for the next 12-18 months.

2. **The stock-bond correlation regime has bifurcated by maturity, and this is the single most portfolio-relevant consequence of balance sheet normalization.** The 2Y-SPX correlation remains negative (-0.15 to -0.30), preserving recession-hedge properties. The 30Y-SPX correlation has turned slightly positive (+0.05 to +0.15), driven by supply/term premium dynamics. This maturity bifurcation invalidates any portfolio construction that treats "bonds" as a monolithic hedge.

3. **The basis trade ($800B-$1T) is functioning as a synthetic correlation regime stabilizer, not just a synthetic QE.** By absorbing duration, it suppresses term premium and keeps long-bond volatility artificially low, which in turn keeps the stock-bond correlation from moving more decisively positive. Its unwind would simultaneously spike rates vol, widen credit, and sell equities — a triple correlation convergence event where all assets decline together.

4. **The relative value across asset classes is most distorted in the credit-equity basis.** Equity-implied credit spreads (derived from Merton-model equity vol and leverage) suggest IG should be 20-40bp wider and HY 75-125bp wider than actual traded spreads. This gap is directly attributable to the basis trade's term premium suppression flowing through to credit via the rates channel. Credit is the most mispriced asset class relative to its own fundamentals.

5. **G3 balance sheet divergence creates an exploitable cross-asset relative value opportunity in FX-rates space.** The BoJ's structural inability to normalize (>50% JGB ownership) vs. the Fed's active QT creates a persistent JPY undervaluation that will correct nonlinearly when normalization eventually occurs. The timing is uncertain (2-3 year thematic) but the magnitude is large: $130-200B in foreign bond selling pressure creates simultaneous JPY appreciation + UST/bund term premium widening.

6. **We are in a late-cycle correlation regime that is unstable and could shift rapidly.** Current regime: risk-on with deteriorating internals — equities elevated, credit tight, rates volatile, FX calm. The conditions for regime shift are: (a) reserve scarcity threshold breach triggering repo stress, (b) basis trade unwind, (c) BoJ normalization shock, or (d) fiscal shock widening duration supply further. Any of these would flip us from positive stock-bond return correlation (current) to a stagflationary regime (stocks and bonds falling together) because the long-end would sell off on supply/term premium, not rally on growth fears.

7. **The RRP exhaustion → reserve-draining phase transition (confidence 9 in KB) has cross-asset implications that are underpriced.** Phase 2 QT should widen funding spreads (FRA-OIS, cross-currency basis), increase repo rate volatility, and selectively stress leveraged positions — particularly the basis trade. The cross-asset signature is: wider money market spreads → forced deleveraging → rates vol spike → credit spread widening → equity multiple compression. This is a sequenced contagion chain, not a simultaneous move.

8. **The optimal hedging maturity for equity portfolios has shifted from 10Y+ to 3-5Y.** Given the supply-driven positive correlation at the long end, 30Y Treasuries are no longer reliable equity hedges. The 3-5Y sector retains negative equity correlation (growth-driven) while offering sufficient duration to provide meaningful P&L offset during equity drawdowns. This is a structural shift, not tactical.

## Evidence & Reasoning

**Claim 1 (Cross-asset incoherence):**
- Rates market: term premium ~100bp from trough (KB: qt_term_premium_channel, 30-60bp from QT alone + fiscal/foreign demand), auction tails widening, new-issue concessions at 15-25bp vs near-zero during QE
- Credit/equity market: IG spreads ~90-110bp (tight by historical standards), VIX sub-16, equity multiples near cycle highs
- The incoherence resolves either by rates rallying (credit/equity correct) or credit/equity repricing wider (rates correct). Given $2.0-2.6T/yr in duration absorption needs vs $500-800B pre-pandemic, the structural supply argument favors rates being more correct.

**Claim 2 (Correlation bifurcation):**
- KB data: 2Y-SPX at -0.15 to -0.30, 30Y-SPX at +0.05 to +0.15
- Mechanism: compound duration absorption ($2.0-2.6T/yr) makes the long end supply-driven. The front end remains monetary policy-driven (growth expectations → rate cut expectations)
- Portfolio implication: a 60/40 portfolio using aggregate bond index (duration ~6Y) is getting a blend of negative and positive equity correlation, reducing effective diversification by 30-50% vs the pre-QT regime

**Claim 3 (Basis trade as correlation stabilizer):**
- Basis trade at $800B-$1T absorbs cash Treasury duration at 50-100x leverage
- By suppressing term premium 20-40bp, it keeps MOVE index subdued and long-rate vol low
- Low rates vol → low credit vol → low equity vol → stable correlations (the VIX-MOVE-CDX feedback loop)
- March 2020 unwind: rates vol spiked, credit blew out, equities sold off simultaneously — correlation went to +1 across all assets. Current notional is 3-4x that level.

**Claim 4 (Credit-equity basis distortion):**
- With equity vol (VIX) at 14-16 and leverage ratios at current levels, Merton-model implied IG spread should be 130-150bp (actual: ~90-110bp). HY implied spread should be 425-475bp (actual: ~300-350bp)
- The gap is not explainable by fundamentals alone — earnings are not strong enough to justify this compression
- The suppressed term premium channel: lower long rates → lower discount rates → higher equity valuations → lower equity vol → lower Merton-implied spreads → tighter actual spreads via benchmarking. Remove the basis trade's 20-40bp term premium suppression and the chain reverses.

**Claim 5 (G3 divergence FX-rates opportunity):**
- BoJ holds >50% of JGBs ($4.5T). Japanese institutions hold $1.3T in foreign bonds
- Even 10-15% reallocation = $130-200B selling pressure on UST/bunds
- JPY is structurally undervalued because normalization is priced as near-impossible
- The carry-to-risk ratio on short USD/JPY is improving as the rate differential narrows

**Claim 6 (Unstable correlation regime):**
- Current regime classification: risk-on with supply-side complications
- Stock-bond return correlation is positive in the 2022-2025 period (both driven by inflation/supply dynamics), unlike the 2000-2020 negative correlation era
- Regime shift triggers are binary/threshold events (reserve scarcity kink, basis trade unwind), not gradual — implying correlation shifts will be sudden

**Claim 7 (Phase 2 QT contagion chain):**
- RRP from $2.55T to <$200B — now draining reserves directly
- Fed's June 2024 taper (Treasury cap $60B→$25B/month) is implicit acknowledgment
- The sequencing matters: money markets tighten first (observable in FRA-OIS), then leveraged strategies deleverage (basis trade), then credit reprices, then equities adjust. Lead time between first signal and equity impact: historically 2-6 weeks.

**Claim 8 (Hedging maturity shift):**
- 30Y-SPX positive correlation means long bonds add risk during equity drawdowns caused by rate/supply shocks (the dominant risk in this regime)
- 3-5Y sector: growth-sensitive (negative equity correlation) + sufficient duration (3.5-4.5Y modified duration) + less supply-driven
- During a pure growth shock (recession), 30Y would still rally — but the current risk distribution is skewed toward supply/inflation shocks where long bonds are counterproductive

## Connections to Other Topics

- **Fiscal policy / sovereign debt dynamics:** Compound duration absorption ($2.0-2.6T/yr) is co-determined by fiscal deficits. The Kalecki fiscal buffer (referenced in KB) interacts — large deficits simultaneously support corporate earnings (keeping credit tight) while worsening the duration supply problem (pushing term premium higher). This is the source of the cross-asset incoherence in Claim 1.

- **Credit cycle / leveraged finance:** The maturity wall ($900B-$1.5T for 2025-2028) refinancing into SOFR+400-550bp is a credit-specific transmission of balance sheet policy. Covenant-lite dynamics (true leverage 5.5-7.5x) mean the credit cycle turn, when it comes, will be binary rather than gradual — creating cliff risk for portfolios. Private credit's $1.7T with NAV smoothing (autocorrelation 0.5-0.7) is a hidden correlation bomb: reported correlations with public markets are artificially low; true correlations are much higher.

- **Inflation / monetary policy regime:** The stock-bond correlation regime is fundamentally about whether inflation or growth is the dominant macro driver. QT keeps the inflation/supply channel active in long rates even if CPI moderates, extending the positive stock-bond correlation regime beyond what inflation data alone would suggest.

- **Financial plumbing / market structure:** The SRF's untested status is a cross-asset tail risk. If it works (no stigma, adequate counterparty access), the reserve floor is lower and QT can run longer — bearish for short-duration rates, neutral for risk assets. If it fails when tested, it's the mechanism by which repo stress propagates to all asset classes simultaneously.

- **Geopolitics / de-globalization:** Foreign official demand for USTs has structurally declined (reserve diversification, sanctions risk). This adds to compound duration absorption and reinforces the supply-driven term premium regime — a secular, not cyclical, shift in the cross-asset correlation structure.

## Open Questions

1. **What is the basis trade's breaking point?** At $800B-$1T and 50-100x leverage, what combination of repo rate volatility, margin calls, and funding cost increases triggers a self-reinforcing unwind? The March 2020 episode occurred at 1/3-1/4 of current notional. Is the relationship between notional size and systemic impact linear, convex, or has infrastructure adapted?

2. **Is the credit-equity basis a genuine mispricing or a structural repricing?** If private credit has permanently absorbed the marginal borrower, public credit spreads may be structurally tighter because the surviving public market is higher quality. This would invalidate the Merton-model comparison.

3. **Can the correlation bifurcation persist during a genuine recession?** Flight-to-quality dynamics could overwhelm supply effects at the long end, temporarily restoring negative 30Y-SPX correlation. The hedging maturity shift (Claim 8) could be wrong exactly when it matters most — during a deep recession where the long end rallies aggressively.

4. **How does the ECB's TPI interact with the cross-asset correlation regime?** If TPI credibility erodes and BTP-Bund spreads widen beyond 200bp, does European sovereign stress create a flight-to-quality bid for USTs (term premium suppression) or a contagion sell-off in global duration (term premium amplification)?

5. **What is the actual cross-central-bank coordination on QT pacing?** The global QT Nash equilibrium concept (KB confidence 6) is underspecified. If there is implicit coordination (BoJ delays normalization because Fed is active), the cross-border spillover risks are lower than modeled. If each acts independently, spillovers are higher.

6. **Where is the reserve scarcity kink?** Fed staff estimates range $2.5-3.5T — a $1T uncertainty band that spans the difference between "QT can run another 12+ months" and "QT needs to stop now." This is the single most portfolio-relevant unknown and it is fundamentally unknowable ex ante.

7. **How should portfolio construction adapt to regime-dependent correlations?** Static mean-variance optimization using trailing correlations will systematically misprice risk in a regime-shifting environment. What is the right framework — regime-switching models, dynamic conditional correlation, or simpler heuristics like the maturity-bifurcation approach in Claim 8?
