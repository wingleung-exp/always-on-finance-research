## REFUTED

**1. Swaption mispricing of 15-20x in OTM wings (rates_strategist_02, Claim 10)**
- **Refuted in:** pair_3 debate
- **Reasoning:** Claims 100bp OTM payer swaptions are priced at ~1.5% probability when they should be ~30-40%. Treats uncertain scenario probabilities as known parameters. Swaptions price all macro scenarios, not just AI-conditional ones. The bimodal distribution probabilities are themselves uncertain (30-40% range = 10pp uncertainty that propagates enormously). A 2-3x mispricing might survive; 15-20x is an artifact of false precision.

**2. Barbell portfolio Sharpe ratio of 0.5-0.8 (generalist_01, Claim 10)**
- **Refuted in:** pair_0 debate
- **Reasoning:** Multi-leg position combining short single-stock vol, long infrastructure equity, short-dated credit, OTM index puts, rate straddles, and gold — with unknown regime-conditional correlations between all components — cannot produce a meaningful ex-ante Sharpe estimate. generalist_01's own Open Question 6 acknowledges the correlation structure is unknown and creates "hidden concentration risk." The barbell framework is directionally appropriate; the Sharpe quantification is fabricated precision.

**3. AI-energy nexus as macro-regime-changing inflation factor (equity_analyst_01, Claim 9)**
- **Refuted in:** pair_1 debate
- **Reasoning:** equity_analyst_01's own evidence shows CPI impact of 0.06-0.24pp — noise in the context of 2.5-2.8% core PCE. The claim that this is "the difference between the Fed cutting 75bp and 25bp" requires the FOMC to be exquisitely sensitive to a single sub-component of energy CPI indistinguishable from broader demand. pair_1: "quantitatively immaterial for macro regime classification." The infrastructure bottleneck is real (see Moderate Confidence #22); the macro inflation story is not.

**4. S&P 493 "AI optionality premium" is identifiable (equity_analyst_02, original Claim 9)**
- **Refuted in:** pair_1 debate; equity_analyst_02 self-downgraded to 4/10
- **Reasoning:** Confounding factors (rate cuts, soft-landing narrative, earnings recovery) prevent clean attribution of the 2.5-turn P/E expansion. Earnings quality metrics for S&P 493 companies citing AI have NOT improved. The reframing as "testable hypothesis" is more honest but the test (companies reducing AI language should de-rate) confuses causation with correlation.

**5. 1920s mechanization as clean analogy for AI demand deficiency (macro_strategist_02, Claim 5 element)**
- **Refuted in:** challenger_02's analysis, confirmed in pair_3 debate
- **Reasoning:** Real wages rose 20-25% during the 1920s (BLS data). The "stagnant wages" claim conflates absolute wages (which rose) with labor share (which declined). Gold standard, 1921/1924 immigration acts, agricultural depression, and speculative leverage are critical confounders. The causal chain (mechanization → underconsumption → crash) omits at least four confounding variables. The valid element — productivity-capital concentration coexisting with financial fragility — is a Minsky observation, not a Kalecki underconsumption one.

**6. Factor rotation timing of "3-6 months continued momentum" (equity_analyst_01, Claim 5)**
- **Refuted in:** pair_1 debate
- **Reasoning:** 6/10 confidence is below the threshold where directional timing calls are useful. The four catalyst categories (rate shock, capex disappointment, earnings breadth, regulatory) cover essentially all possible negative scenarios, making the framework unfalsifiable. equity_analyst_01's own caveat — "factor rotation calls have historically been early more often than late" — should have led to withdrawing the timing estimate.

**7. "Sudden stop" framing for services-exporting EM economies (fx_strategist_02, Claim 3 framing)**
- **Refuted in:** pair_2 debate (framing, not directional vulnerability)
- **Reasoning:** Structural comparative advantage erosion doesn't cause Calvo-style sudden stops — those require confidence crises and liquidity events. Philippines has ~$100B reserves, IG rating, flexible exchange rate. A 10-15% BPO growth reduction widening the current account by 1.5-2% of GDP over 2-3 years is gradual adjustment, not crisis. The vulnerability is real (retained in Moderate Confidence #23); the crisis framing is overstated.

**8. FX vol surface mispricing from AI-driven compression (fx_strategist_01, Claim 6)**
- **Refuted in:** pair_2 debate (by its own internal logic)
- **Reasoning:** fx_strategist_01 argues AI-compressed vol creates cheap tail-risk optionality, then concedes vol-selling has generated persistent positive returns for a decade "precisely because the AI-driven compression is structural, not temporary." If structural, the surface is correctly priced for the new regime. Flash crashes in individual pairs (CHF 2015, GBP 2016, JPY 2019) are liquidity events, not dollar regime changes. The directional dollar view dressed up as a vol mispricing argument doesn't survive scrutiny.

## KEY_DISAGREEMENTS

**1. Slow burn vs. kill-zone timeline for capex stress**
- generalist_01: 50% probability of "muddled middle" — persistent ROIC dilution without capex crash, multi-year grind
- generalist_02: 12-24 month buffer to kill-zone breach at current capex growth rates
- **Why unresolved:** The slow burn requires unprecedented corporate self-restraint in the face of competitive pressure (no mechanism identified for why boards would discipline before pain). The kill-zone thresholds are derived from debt-funded cycles structurally different from self-funded hyperscalers. The truth likely sits between: slow burn is near-term reality (12-18 months), kill-zone correctly identifies the horizon at which it becomes unsustainable. **Monitor:** quarterly capex/OCF ratios; management language on capex discipline; any unilateral capex cut (prisoner's dilemma defection).

**2. ROIC measurability and analytical implications**
- equity_analyst_01/generalist_01: provide specific ROIC ranges (3-8% base, 10-12% bull) and build trajectories
- equity_analyst_02/generalist_02: AI-specific ROIC is nearly unmeasurable when bundled into existing products
- **Why unresolved:** Fundamental attribution problem — when Google embeds AI into Search or Microsoft adds Copilot to Office 365, no decomposition can reliably isolate AI-specific returns. This undermines all ROIC-based analyses, including the EVA framework and blended ROIC trajectories. **Monitor:** hyperscaler willingness to disaggregate AI-specific revenue in earnings disclosures; if they don't, the measurement problem is likely unfavorable.

**3. Cross-asset inconsistency: mispricing vs. multi-horizon rational pricing**
- generalist_01/equity_analyst_01/rates_strategist_02: actionable mispricing
- challenger_02: partly artifactual — different time horizons legitimately explain divergence
- **Why unresolved:** Both interpretations are internally consistent. No empirical test distinguishes them in real time. The convergence timeline is unknown; historical base rates for cross-asset inconsistency trades are "under-documented" (challenger_02's Open Question 3). Carry costs of holding convergence positions erode theoretical edge. **Research needed:** systematic study of cross-asset inconsistency convergence timelines and hit rates.

**4. AI put severity: rates_strategist_02 vs. challenger_02**
- rates_strategist_02: $18-22T AI market cap makes wealth-effect easing structurally guaranteed in bust
- challenger_02: 2022 tech drawdown (~$5T, no cascade, Fed continued hiking) is direct counterexample
- **Partially resolved:** The AI put exists but its strike price is lower and trigger conditions more demanding than rates_strategist_02 implies. The 2022 counterexample addresses rate-driven valuation compression (technology thesis intact); an AI-specific bust (technology thesis breaks) would produce different dynamics (simultaneous equity, credit, and capex shock). These are different risks. **Monitor:** whether any future drawdown triggers capex cuts (the mechanism that would cascade).

**5. Whether potential GDP is endogenous to demand or supply-determined**
- macro_strategist_02: CBO's serial revisions prove path-dependence; output gap is meaningless
- Mainstream framework implicit in most other agents: potential GDP is exogenous benchmark
- **Why unresolved:** Deep theoretical disagreement. Empirical pattern (CBO revisions toward actual) supports the Post-Keynesian position. But some supply constraints are genuinely exogenous (demographics, resource depletion). The practical implication: if potential is endogenous, AI's macro impact depends on the demand regime (who captures gains and spends them), not just the technology. This changes the analytical framework fundamentally.

**6. Can AI's software-native deployment characteristics compress the historical productivity lag?**
- generalist_02's "recursive reorganization": AI is the first GPT that can assist in its own Phase 2 organizational restructuring, potentially compressing the 5-8 year reorganization lag to 2-4 years
- Base-rate analysis from all historical-analogue agents: no GPT has compressed below 5 years
- **Why unresolved:** No historical precedent exists. The near-zero marginal deployment cost argument is legitimate (unlike electricity/railroads, no physical infrastructure to rebuild). challenger_02 identifies this as the strongest version of the bull case that skeptics fail to steel-man. This is the single most important unresolvable question for the AI macro thesis.
